Marş Sistemi — Starter System

Last updated: 2026-04-13
Slides covered: Intro (görev), Parts diagram (4 parts), Detailed part definitions (akümülatör, kontak anahtarı, marş motoru)

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

Marş sistemi: Motorumuzun çalışabilmesi için ilk hareketi vermektir.
(The starter system provides the first movement needed for the engine to run.)

A car engine cannot start itself from a dead stop. Pistons must be moving, fuel must be injected, and in gasoline engines the spark plug must fire — but all of that requires the engine to already be turning. Something external has to give the crankshaft its first kick. That external kick is the job of the marş sistemi.

The flow:
1. You turn the key
2. Electricity from the akü → kontak anahtarı → marş motoru
3. Marş motoru spins, engaging the volan via the marş dişlisi
4. Volan spins → crankshaft spins → combustion begins
5. Engine "catches" and runs on its own
6. Starter motor disengages

Once the engine is running, the starter system is done — its job is only the first movement.

2. SLIDE 1 — Marş Sistemi: Intro

Slide title: MARŞ SİSTEMİ
Subtitle: "Motorumuzun çalışabilmesi için ilk hareketi vermektir..."

Clean English explanation

This slide establishes the single definition of the starter system. Commit the Turkish sentence to memory word-for-word — the exam will test exactly this phrasing.

The definition: The starter system's one job is to give the engine its first movement so that it can begin to run.

Critical exam fact

Marş sisteminin görevi, motora ilk hareketi vermektir.
(The job of the starter system is to give the engine its first movement.)

This one sentence will answer the majority of "what is the görev of…" questions about the starter system.

3. SLIDE 2 — Marş Sistemi Parçaları (Parts Diagram)

Slide content: A wiring diagram showing 4 labeled parts connected by blue wires:
- AKÜ (battery) — top-left
- KONTAK ANAHTARI (ignition switch) — center, with key illustration
- MARŞ MOTORU (starter motor) — top-right, with MARŞ DİŞLİSİ (pinion gear) labeled as an extension
- VOLAN (flywheel) — bottom, heavy toothed wheel

The flow chain (memorize this!)

AKÜ ─▶ KONTAK ANAHTARI ─▶ MARŞ MOTORU ─▶ MARŞ DİŞLİSİ ─▶ VOLAN ─▶ engine cranks

The 4 main parts explained

1. AKÜ — Battery

The power source. Stores electrical energy and provides the high current needed to spin the starter motor. Without a charged akü, nothing in the starter system works → car won't start. (On the exam, akü and akümülatör mean the same thing — see Slide 3.)

2. KONTAK ANAHTARI — Ignition Switch / Key

The switch you turn (or button you press). It completes the electrical circuit between the battery and the starter motor. Turning the key sends the signal "connect akü to marş motoru now."

3. MARŞ MOTORU — Starter Motor

The small but powerful electric motor that does the actual work of cranking the engine. When electricity from the akü reaches it, it spins rapidly. Attached to it is the MARŞ DİŞLİSİ (pinion gear) — a small gear that jumps out and meshes with the volan's teeth when the starter engages.

4. VOLAN — Flywheel

A large, heavy toothed wheel attached to the engine's crankshaft. The marş dişlisi engages with the teeth on the edge of the volan and spins it → the crankshaft spins → the engine begins to run. Once the engine catches, the marş dişlisi disengages and retracts so the running engine doesn't damage the starter.

Exam trap

A classic distractor question: "Aşağıdakilerden hangisi marş sisteminin parçası değildir?"
The trap answer is usually from another system: karbüratör, enjektör, radyatör, alternatör, yağ pompası. Memorize the 4 marş parts above (plus the marş dişlisi as an attached sub-part) so you can spot distractors instantly.

4. SLIDE 3 — Detailed Definitions (Akümülatör, Kontak Anahtarı, Marş Motoru)

What the slide said

AKÜMÜLATÖR: Marş motoru için gerekli ilk elektrik enerjisini temin eden parçadır.

KONTAK ANAHTARI: Marş konumunu açar ve marşa basma süresi 10–15 saniye olmalıdır.

MARŞ MOTORU: Elektrik enerjisini mekanik enerjiye çeviren motor elemanıdır.

This slide gives the exact exam definitions for 3 of the 4 main parts. The hoca quotes these word-for-word — so should you.

1. AKÜMÜLATÖR — Battery / Accumulator

"Marş motoru için gerekli ilk elektrik enerjisini temin eden parçadır."
— "The part that supplies the initial electrical energy needed by the starter motor."

Akü and akümülatör are the same thing — akü is everyday Turkish, akümülatör is the formal exam term. It's a rechargeable battery that stores electrical energy and releases a large burst of current when you turn the key.

Once the engine is running, the şarj sistemi (charging system — see 04-şarj-sistemi.md) takes over and refills the akü so it's ready for next time.

2. KONTAK ANAHTARI — Ignition Switch / Key

"Marş konumunu açar ve marşa basma süresi 10–15 saniye olmalıdır."
— "It activates the starter position and the time pressing the starter must be 10–15 seconds."

Turning the key passes through several positions:
- OFF → everything off
- ON (kontak açık) → dashboard and electronics powered
- MARŞ (start) → battery connects to starter motor, engine cranks

⚠️ CRITICAL EXAM FACT — the 10–15 second rule

Marşa basma süresi 10–15 saniye olmalıdır.
(The time pressing the starter must be 10–15 seconds.)

You should never hold the key in the marş position longer than 10–15 seconds at a time. If the engine doesn't start:
- Release the key, wait, then try again
- Holding it longer will overheat and burn out the marş motoru AND drain the akü

This 10–15 second number is one of the most frequently tested facts in the entire Araç Tekniği section. Memorize the exact range.

3. MARŞ MOTORU — Starter Motor

"Elektrik enerjisini mekanik enerjiye çeviren motor elemanıdır."
— "The motor component that converts electrical energy into mechanical energy."

This is the key definition. The marş motoru takes electricity from the akü (electrical energy) and converts it into spinning motion (mechanical energy) that cranks the volan. It's the bridge between "electricity in the battery" and "the engine physically turning."

The golden contrast (known cold)

System Input Output Key part
Marş sistemi (Starter system) Elektrik enerjisi (electrical energy, from akü / battery) Mekanik hareket (mechanical motion — cranking the engine) Marş motoru (starter motor)
Şarj sistemi (Charging system) Mekanik hareket (mechanical motion, from the running engine) Elektrik enerjisi (electrical energy — refills the akü) Alternatör (alternator / şarj dinamosu)

These two are mirror opposites. Exam questions exploit the confusion.

4B. Hoca's Live Class Additions (2026-04-13)

These points came from the live class discussion, not the slides. All are exam-relevant or safety-critical.

⚠️ NEVER re-turn the key on a running engine ("dişi sıyırmak")

"Araba çalıştığı halde bazen adaylar bunu fark etmiyor. Çalışan kontak anahtarını tekrar çeviriyor. İşte buradaki dişliler sıyrılır. Dişi sıyırmış denilir."
(Sometimes students don't notice the engine is already running and turn the key again. The gear teeth strip off — we call it "stripping the teeth.")

What happens: When you turn the key while the engine is already spinning, the marş dişlisi (pinion gear) tries to engage with a volan (flywheel) that's already rotating at high speed. The gear teeth grind together violently and dişi sıyırmak — the teeth literally shear off.

Consequence: The starter is damaged, may fail to engage next time, and you'll hear a horrible grinding sound while it happens.

Rule: Once the engine starts and you hear it running, release the key immediately. Listen for the engine — don't just count seconds. If unsure whether it's running, trust your ears.

Exam answer vs real-world: the 10–15 saniye rule

"Günlük hayatta o da beş saniye sürer ve yemez. İki saniye, üç saniye, bir saniye en fazla. Ama size eğer sınav sorarsa müfredat böyle on, on beş demiş. O zaman biz on, on beş saniyeyi işaretleyeceğiz."

Translation: In real life, 1–5 seconds is enough to start a modern car. BUT the MEB exam curriculum says 10–15 saniye, so on the exam you must answer 10–15 saniye. Don't overthink — the curriculum is the authority.

Exam answer: Marşa basma süresi 10–15 saniye olmalıdır.
Real-world: 1–5 seconds is actually enough.

Akü ömrü (Battery lifespan) — no fixed standard

"Akünün ömrüyle ilgili bir standart yok. Herkesin kullanmasına göre değişir."

Battery life depends on usage patterns:
- Heavy daily use (many start-stops) → shorter life
- Infrequent use (car parked for weeks) → battery dies from self-discharge
- Extreme cold/heat → shortens life
- Bad charging system → prematurely kills the battery

No fixed "X years" answer — if the exam asks, the right concept is "kullanım sıklığına bağlıdır" (depends on usage frequency).

Parked cars and dead batteries

"Arabayı kapının önüne çektin. Bir ay geçti. İki ay geçti. Hâlâ kullanmadın. Arabanın aküsü bitti."

A car left unused for weeks/months will have a drained battery even if nothing is "on." The clock, alarm, ECU memory, and normal self-discharge slowly pull power from the akü.

Hoca's advice: Even if you don't drive, start the engine every 3–7 days and let it idle for a few minutes. This keeps the akü charged. If the car sat too long, you may need akü takviyesi.

Akü takviyesi (Jump-starting) — solution for a dead battery

"Eğer ki aracın elektrikli değilse nasıl çalıştırabilirsin? Akü takviyesi yaparak."

Akü takviyesi = jump-starting. Connect your dead battery to another car's working battery (or a portable jump pack) using cables, then turn the key. The external power provides the marş motoru with the current it needs to crank the engine.

Once the engine starts, your car's şarj sistemi (alternator) takes over and begins recharging your akü automatically (see 04-şarj-sistemi.md).

Note: This only works for combustion engine cars (benzinli/dizel). Electric vehicles have a completely different traction battery system and can't be jump-started the same way.

7. Cross-references

  • Mirror system: 04-şarj-sistemi.md — the şarj sistemi refills the akü while the engine runs. Together with marş sistemi, these form the complete battery-power cycle.
  • Dependency: The marş sistemi needs the akü charged. If the şarj sistemi fails, eventually the marş sistemi also fails (you can't start the car).
  • Common trap: Don't confuse marş motoru with motor (the engine itself). Marş motoru = starter; motor = the whole engine.
