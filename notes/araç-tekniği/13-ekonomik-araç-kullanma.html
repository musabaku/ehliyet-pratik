Ekonomik Araç Kullanma — Fuel-Efficient Driving

Last updated: 2026-04-14 (class 2)
Covered in class: Araçtan kaynaklanan kusurlar (slide 1), Sürücüden kaynaklanan kusurlar (slide 2), Ekonomik sürüş için alınacak önlemler (slide 3), Araçta yakıt tasarrufu 8-item checklist (slide 4), Closing ekonomik sürüş tips — 90 km/h + kaliteli yakıt + dolu depo (slide 5 — FINAL SLIDE OF THE CLASS)

1. Overview — What is "ekonomik sürüş"?

Ekonomik sürüş (fuel-efficient driving) is about minimizing fuel consumption (yakıt tüketimi) without sacrificing safety or vehicle lifespan. The Turkish MEB curriculum treats this as its own subject (Ekonomik Araç Kullanma — Araç Tekniği Bilgisi subject 4/6) because fuel is expensive, fuel efficiency affects both your wallet AND the environment (emissions), and most drivers significantly over-consume fuel due to bad habits and poor vehicle maintenance.

The core insight: fuel consumption is determined by two things:

  1. The car — how efficiently the engine, fuel system, and aerodynamics work
  2. The driver — how the car is operated (shifting, braking, accelerating, speed)

Fix either one and you save fuel. Fix both and you can cut fuel consumption by 30–40% compared to a poorly-maintained car with a careless driver.

Reference: e-kitap/03-araç-tekniği/04-ekonomik-arac-kullanma.md — the portal's dedicated subject on this topic.

The 9 vehicle-side faults that waste fuel

# Turkish English Why it wastes fuel
1 Karbüratörün bakımsız ve eski olması Worn / un-serviced carburetor Wrong fuel/air mixture → engine runs rich → excess fuel burned
2 Bujilerin bozuk ve ayarsız olması Faulty / mis-gapped spark plugs Weak or delayed ignition → incomplete combustion → wasted fuel + lost power
3 Lastik hava basıncının az olması Under-inflated tires Increased rolling resistance → engine works harder to maintain speed → more fuel used
4 Fren ayarının sıkı olması Over-tight brake adjustment Brakes constantly dragging → engine fights against constant friction → see 09-fren-sistemi.md slide 3 fault 3
5 Hava filtresinin tıkalı olması Clogged air filter Engine starves for air → runs rich → wastes fuel, loses power
6 Distribütör platin ayarının katalog değerinde olmaması Distributor points mis-adjusted Ignition timing off → engine runs inefficiently
7 Akümülatör ve bağlantı kablolarının bozuk olması Faulty battery / cables Weak electrical feed → weak sparks → poor combustion
8 Rölanti devrinin katalog değerinde olmaması Idle RPM set incorrectly Idling too high → wastes fuel at every stop
9 Debriyajın kaçırması Clutch slipping Engine power doesn't fully reach wheels → have to press gas more for same speed

Key terms explained

Rölanti (idle): the engine's running speed when no gas is applied — typically 700–900 RPM for modern cars. If this is set too high (e.g., 1500 RPM), the engine is constantly burning fuel at a higher rate even when the car is sitting still.

Distribütör platin: an old-school ignition component (points-based distributor) that times the spark. Found on older cars; modern cars use electronic ignition. Still on the exam because older cars are still common.

Debriyaj kaçırması (clutch slipping): when the clutch is worn out, it no longer fully grips — so even when the pedal is fully released, some engine power is lost as heat in the clutch plate instead of being delivered to the wheels. You feel it as the engine revving but the car not accelerating proportionally.

Karbüratör: an older mechanical fuel-air mixing device. Modern cars use fuel injection (enjektör) instead. The exam still references karbüratör because the curriculum is old.

Critical exam fact

Aracın fazla yakıt yakmasının araçtan kaynaklanan 9 nedeni: karbüratör, bujiler, lastik basıncı, fren ayarı, hava filtresi, distribütör platin, akü/kablolar, rölanti devri, debriyaj kaçırması.

3. SLIDE 2 — Sürücüden Kaynaklanan Kusurlar (Driver-Side Causes)

What the slide showed

Title: "Sürücüden kaynaklanan kusurlar" (Driver-related faults). A bulleted list of 6 habits that waste fuel, plus a transition line to the "yakıt tasarrufu için neler yapılmalıdır" (fuel-saving tips) section.

Verbatim Turkish → English

Sürücüden kaynaklanan kusurlar:
- Düşük vitesle seyretmek (motoru yüksek devirde tutmak)
- Şehir içerisinde yapılan duraklamalarda motoru kapatarak, tekrar çalıştırmak
- Aniden hızlanmak, gereksiz yere fren yapmak, marşa gereğinden fazla basarak motoru boğmak
- Klimanın bilinçsiz kullanılması
- Arabayı çalıştırırken gaza basmak
- Yakıt tasarrufu için neler yapılmalıdır

Driver-related faults:
- Driving in a low gear (keeping the engine at high RPM)
- In city stops, turning the engine off and restarting it (thinking this saves fuel)
- Sudden acceleration, unnecessary braking, over-cranking the starter and flooding the engine
- Using the A/C carelessly / unconsciously
- Pressing the gas while starting the engine
- What should be done for fuel savings

The 5 driver-side faults — detail

1. Düşük vitesle seyretmek (driving in a low gear)

Plain English: Staying in 1st or 2nd gear when you should be in 3rd, 4th, or 5th. The engine is running at too high an RPM for the speed, which means:

  • More fuel per second is being burned
  • The engine is straining / screaming
  • Premature engine wear

The fix: shift up promptly. See 08-güç-aktarma-organları.md section 2E for the correct 0-20 / 20-40 / 40-60 / 60-80 / 80+ km/h gear speed ranges.

Exam trigger: "Düşük viteste seyretmek yakıta etkisi nedir?" → Yakıt tüketimini artırır.

2. Turning the engine off at every city stop

Plain English: Counter-intuitive but important: some drivers think that turning off the engine at every red light or short stop saves fuel. It does NOT — it actually wastes fuel because:

  • Restarting the engine takes a burst of fuel (enrichment for cold start)
  • The marş motoru draws energy from the akü, which then has to be re-charged by the engine running harder
  • Stop-start cycles are harder on the starter and battery than just idling

Modern note: many new cars have start-stop sistemi (automatic start-stop at red lights) — this IS efficient because it's computer-controlled and optimized. But manually cycling the ignition off-on is not.

The exam rule: don't manually stop/start the engine at short city stops. Let it idle.

3. Sudden acceleration + unnecessary braking + over-cranking

Three related habits:

Aniden hızlanmak (sudden acceleration)

Flooring the gas pedal causes the fuel-air mixture to go rich (more fuel than air), which wastes fuel AND produces excess emissions. Gentle, progressive acceleration uses less fuel for the same end speed.

The habit to build: accelerate as if there's an egg between your foot and the gas pedal. Gentle, smooth.

Gereksiz yere fren yapmak (unnecessary braking)

Every time you brake, you're throwing away kinetic energy as heat. The energy you burned fuel to create is being dissipated at the brake disc. Then you have to burn more fuel to re-accelerate. Unnecessary braking = double waste.

The habit to build: look ahead and anticipate. If you see a red light 300 m away, lift off the gas instead of racing up to it and braking hard.

Marşa gereğinden fazla basarak motoru boğmak (over-cranking → flooding the engine)

When you crank the starter for too long on a reluctant engine, the engine becomes boğulmuş (flooded) — the cylinders fill with too much fuel relative to air, and the spark plugs can't ignite the over-rich mixture. You waste fuel + battery power + starter motor life.

The rule (from 03-marş-sistemi.md): never crank for more than 10–15 saniye continuously. If the engine doesn't start in that time, release the key, wait 30 seconds, and try again. If it still won't start, there's a real problem — don't just keep cranking.

4. Klimanın bilinçsiz kullanılması (careless A/C use)

Plain English: The A/C compressor is driven by the engine — it adds load, and therefore increases fuel consumption. The penalty is real:

  • Running the A/C at city speeds can increase fuel consumption by 10–20%
  • At highway speeds, A/C is cheaper than driving with windows open (aerodynamic drag penalty of open windows is worse)

The rules for smart A/C use:
- Don't leave the A/C on all the time — turn it off when you don't need it
- Park in shade so the cabin isn't hot to start with
- Open windows for the first minute to flush out hot air before turning on A/C
- Recirculation mode (iç hava dolaşımı) is slightly more efficient than fresh air mode
- At city speeds (below 60 km/h), windows open is fine
- At highway speeds (above 80 km/h), A/C + windows closed is more efficient

5. Arabayı çalıştırırken gaza basmak (pressing the gas while starting)

Plain English: Some drivers press the gas pedal while cranking the starter, thinking this helps the car start. On modern fuel-injected cars, this is wrong and wastes fuel — the ECU is already delivering the correct starting mixture, and adding throttle just pumps extra fuel into the cylinders unnecessarily.

On older carbureted cars, a brief tap of the gas pedal to prime the carb was sometimes needed, but it's obsolete on modern cars.

The rule: don't touch the gas pedal while starting the engine. Just turn the key and wait for it to start. If it won't start, investigate — don't pump the pedal.

Critical exam fact

Aracın fazla yakıt yakmasının sürücüden kaynaklanan nedenleri: düşük vitesle seyretme, gereksiz motor kapat-aç, ani hızlanma, gereksiz fren, gereğinden fazla marşa basma, bilinçsiz klima kullanımı, marş sırasında gaza basma.

4. SLIDE 3 — Ekonomik Sürüş İçin Alınacak Önlemler (Fuel-Saving Measures)

What the slide showed

Title: "Ekonomik sürüş için alınacak önlemler" (Measures for fuel-efficient driving). Subtitle: "İşte az yakıtı an önlemler" (Here are the fuel-saving measures). A bulleted list of 7 concrete habits to minimize fuel consumption.

Verbatim Turkish → English

Ekonomik sürüş için alınacak önlemler

  • Zorunlu olmadıkça otomobiliniz 2000-3000 devir aralığında kullanın.
  • Hep yeşil ışıklarda geçmek, kırmızıya yakalanıp ışık kentlerdeki yeşil dalga hizasına dikkat edin.
  • Çok uzun süre yanan kırmızı ışıklarda kesinlikle motoru stop etmeyin.
  • Otomobilinizin bagajında gereksiz yükler bulundurmayın.
  • Şehir içinde, kırmızı ışık görür görmez gazdan ayağınızı çekin ve aracınız momentum ile yaklaşsın.
  • Motoru tam ısınmadan aracınızı yola çıkarmayın.
  • Yüksek hızda pencere camları açık seyretmeyin (aerodinamik kayıpları).

The 7 fuel-saving habits

1. Keep the engine in the 2000–3000 RPM range

Zorunlu olmadıkça otomobiliniz 2000-3000 devir aralığında kullanın.

Plain English: Modern gasoline engines are most efficient around 2000-3000 RPM. Below that, the engine is lugging (struggling at low RPM) and above that, it's burning fuel inefficiently. Shift up or down to keep the RPM in this sweet spot during normal driving.

Exam-ready fact: the ideal ekonomik sürüş RPM range is 2000–3000 RPM.

2. Try to hit green lights / follow the "yeşil dalga" (green wave)

Plain English: In modern cities, many traffic lights are synchronized to create a yeşil dalga (green wave) — if you drive at the recommended speed (usually 50 km/h), you hit green lights one after another without stopping. Every avoided red light = no stop-and-start fuel waste. Pay attention to the timing patterns in your city.

3. Don't stop the engine at long red lights

Çok uzun süre yanan kırmızı ışıklarda kesinlikle motoru stop etmeyin.

This is the same rule as slide 2, driver fault #2 — restating the point. Don't manually cycle the ignition off/on, it wastes more fuel than it saves.

4. Don't carry unnecessary weight in the trunk

Otomobilinizin bagajında gereksiz yükler bulundurmayın.

Plain English: Every kilo of extra weight means the engine has to work harder to accelerate the car. If you're driving around with 50 kg of junk in the trunk that doesn't need to be there, you're burning ~3–5% extra fuel constantly.

Rule: clean out your trunk regularly. Keep only essentials: yedek lastik, kriko, bijon anahtarı, ilk yardım çantası, reflektör, yangın söndürücü, yedek ampul. Everything else comes out.

5. Lift off the gas the moment you see a red light ahead

Kırmızı ışık görür görmez gazdan ayağınızı çekin ve aracınız momentum ile yaklaşsın.

Plain English: The single biggest ekonomik sürüş habit is: anticipate stops and coast (engine braking) toward them instead of continuing at full speed and then braking hard.

When you lift off the gas:
- The engine stops injecting fuel (or injects very little)
- The car coasts forward using its momentum
- The engine braking slowly reduces speed
- You arrive at the red light at a low speed
- Minimal (or no) actual brake pedal needed

Compare to the wasteful way:
- Keep the gas on until close to the light
- Stomp on the brake at the last moment
- Throw away all the kinetic energy as brake heat
- Start from zero on green, burning a lot of fuel to re-accelerate

Exam angle: look-ahead driving is the key habit that separates efficient drivers from fuel-wasting drivers.

6. Don't drive off before the engine is warmed up

Motoru tam ısınmadan aracınızı yola çıkarmayın.

Plain English: A cold engine runs rich (extra fuel) for its first minute or two, because cold oil has high viscosity and the fuel doesn't atomize well. During this warm-up period, fuel consumption is 30-50% higher than normal. If you drive off aggressively while the engine is cold, you:

  • Burn much more fuel than normal
  • Cause premature engine wear (cold oil isn't flowing properly)
  • Produce more emissions

The rule: let the engine idle for 30 sn – 1 dk (see 12-güvenli-araç-kullanma.md slide 11 on the warm-up rule), or at least drive very gently for the first 2 minutes until the engine is at operating temperature.

In winter: the warm-up period is longer, up to 2–3 minutes. Don't rush it.

7. Don't drive at high speed with windows open

Yüksek hızda pencere camları açık seyretmeyin (aerodynamik kayıpları).

Plain English: At speeds above ~80 km/h, open windows create significant aerodynamic drag — the wind rushes into the cabin and pushes against the inside of the car. This drag can increase fuel consumption by 5–10% at highway speeds.

The rule:
- City speeds (below 60 km/h) → open windows is fine (A/C penalty is higher)
- Highway speeds (above 80 km/h) → close windows, use A/C (A/C penalty is lower than drag penalty)

The crossover point is around 60-80 km/h. Below that, windows open; above that, windows closed + A/C if needed.

Critical exam facts

Ekonomik sürüş devir aralığı: 2000-3000 devir.

Ekonomik sürüş altın kuralı: önceden görüp gazdan ayağını çek (look-ahead driving + coasting).

Yüksek hızda pencereler kapalı, A/C açık (aerodynamic drag > A/C penalty above ~80 km/h).

ARAÇTA YAKIT TASARRUFU

  1. Bujiler temizlenmeli (belirli kilometrede değiştirilmelidir).
  2. Hava filtresi tıkalı olmamalıdır (belirli kilometrede değiştirilmelidir).
  3. Araç uygun viteste ve hızda kullanılmalıdır.
  4. Lastiklerin hava basıncı normal değerde olmalı.
  5. Fren ayarları sıkı olmamalı.
  6. Karbüratör ayarları (Rölanti) doğru yapılmalıdır.
  7. Jikle çekili unutulmamalıdır.
  8. Kavrama (debriyaj) kaçırmamalıdır.

The 8-item checklist — detail

This is the summary / synthesis slide — it combines the best advice from slides 1–3 into one compact list. Memorize all 8 items; this is high-frequency exam material.

1. Bujiler temizlenmeli (Spark plugs cleaned / replaced)

Plain English: Bujiler (spark plugs) are the components that create the spark to ignite the fuel-air mixture in gasoline engines (not diesels — diesels have no bujiler). Over time they develop carbon deposits (karbon birikintisi) that weaken the spark. Weak spark → incomplete combustion → wasted fuel.

Maintenance:
- Clean at every periyodik bakım
- Replace at the manufacturer-specified interval (typically 40,000–100,000 km depending on plug type)

2. Hava filtresi tıkalı olmamalı (Air filter not clogged)

Plain English: The hava filtresi (air filter) cleans the air entering the engine. Over time, it gets clogged with dust. A clogged filter restricts airflow, causing:

  • The engine to run rich (too much fuel relative to air)
  • Fuel wastage (extra unburned fuel exits via exhaust)
  • Power loss
  • Emissions increase

Maintenance: check at every periyodik bakım, replace every 15,000–30,000 km or when it looks dirty.

3. Araç uygun viteste ve hızda kullanılmalı (Use correct gear at correct speed)

Plain English: match gear to speed using the 0-20/20-40/40-60/60-80/80+ km/h rule from 08-güç-aktarma-organları.md slide 2E. Don't lug the engine in high gears at low speeds, and don't scream the engine in low gears at high speeds.

4. Lastiklerin hava basıncı normal değerde olmalı (Tire pressure at normal value)

Plain English: Under-inflated tires create excess rolling resistance — they flex more against the road, generating heat instead of rolling freely. The engine has to work harder to maintain speed. A 0.5 bar pressure drop from optimal can increase fuel consumption by 3–5%.

Rule: check tire pressure weekly, especially before long trips. See 08b-lastikler.md for the correct pressures and contact-patch illustrations.

5. Fren ayarları sıkı olmamalı (Brakes not adjusted too tight)

Plain English: If the brake adjustment is too tight, the balatas are constantly touching the disc / drum even when the pedal is released. This creates continuous drag — the engine has to work harder against that drag, wasting fuel.

See 09-fren-sistemi.md slide 3 fault 3: "Fren ayarları sıkıysa balatalar aşınır, fazla yakıt sarf eder" — this is the same fault being re-stated.

Maintenance: if you notice your car has less coasting distance than before (slows down quickly when you lift off the gas), check the brake adjustment.

6. Karbüratör (rölanti) ayarları doğru olmalı (Carburetor / idle set correctly)

Plain English: The rölanti (idle speed) should be set to the manufacturer's specified value — typically 700-900 RPM. If the idle is too high, the engine is burning fuel faster than necessary every time you sit at a red light. If too low, the engine may stall.

Modern note: fuel-injected engines have electronically-controlled idle speeds that rarely drift out of spec. Older carbureted engines need manual adjustment.

7. Jikle çekili unutulmamalı (Don't leave the choke engaged)

Plain English: The jikle (choke) is an old-school carburetor component that restricts air intake to create a richer fuel mixture for cold starting. Once the engine is warm, you're supposed to push the jikle back in (disengage it).

If you forget to release the jikle:
- The engine keeps running rich all the time
- Excess fuel is burned every second
- Power is reduced
- Exhaust smells of unburned fuel
- Fuel economy tanks

Modern note: fuel-injected cars don't have a manual jikle — the ECU handles cold enrichment automatically. But the exam still tests this because older cars (especially in rural Turkey) still have manual chokes.

Rule: after the engine warms up (~1-2 minutes), push the jikle button back in. Don't forget.

8. Kavrama (debriyaj) kaçırmamalı (Clutch should not slip)

Plain English: A slipping clutch doesn't fully transfer engine power to the wheels — some of it is lost as friction heat in the clutch disc. Symptoms:

  • The engine revs but the car accelerates slowly
  • A burning smell under the hood
  • The car doesn't pull strongly uphill
  • Fuel consumption goes up because you have to press the gas more for the same speed

Fix: replace the worn clutch assembly. It's expensive but necessary — a slipping clutch only gets worse with time, and eventually the car won't move at all.

Memory device: the 8-item checklist

Organize it mentally into 3 groups:

Verbatim Turkish → English

  • Ani gaza basmak yakıt sarfiyatını artırır.
  • Yakıtımızı dolu bulundurmalıyız.
  • Kaliteli yakıt almalıyız.
  • Aracımızı uygun devirde kullanmalıyız.

  • 90 km/saat üzerine çıkmamaya özen göstermeliyiz...

  • Sudden acceleration increases fuel consumption.

  • We should keep our fuel tank full.
  • We should use quality fuel.
  • We should use the car at the correct engine RPM.
  • We should take care not to exceed 90 km/h.

The 5 closing tips — what's new vs recap

Tip 1 — Ani gaz = daha fazla yakıt (RECAP)

Already covered in slide 2 (driver-side fault #3) and slide 3 (anticipate stops, don't brake hard). Restated here as a simple one-liner for final emphasis.

Tip 2 — ⭐ NEW: Yakıt deposunu DOLU bulundur (Keep the fuel tank FULL)

Plain English: Counter-intuitive but real — keeping the fuel tank full (or at least half-full) saves fuel and protects the fuel system. Here's why:

  1. Less air space = less evaporation. A nearly-empty tank has a lot of air above the fuel. Gasoline slowly evaporates into that air space, and the fumes escape through the tank vent (even on modern cars with recovery systems, some is lost). Full tank = minimal air space = minimal evaporation loss.

  2. Less water condensation. In temperature swings (cold nights, warm days), humid air in a partially-empty tank condenses water droplets on the inner tank walls. That water eventually drops into the fuel, contaminating it. Contaminated fuel = clogged filters, rusted fuel lines, rough running, hard starting. Especially important for dizel (see 01-yakıt-sistemi.md — "yakıt deposundan günlük su boşaltılmalıdır"), but also relevant for gasoline.

  3. Safety. If you run out of fuel on a remote road, you're stranded. A diesel that runs dry "hava yapar" (air-lock in the fuel system) and may need professional bleeding. A half-full tank is your insurance against running out unexpectedly.

  4. Battery / starter protection. Running a car until the fuel gauge hits zero means your fuel pump is sucking the bottom of the tank where any sediment accumulates — you're feeding the engine dirty fuel and debris.

Exam trigger phrase:
- "Yakıt deposunu neden dolu bulundurmalıyız?" → Deposu dolu tutmak evaporasyonu, su yoğunlaşmasını ve kirli yakıt sorunlarını önler.

Practical rule: never let the fuel gauge drop below 1/4 full for regular driving. Fill up whenever it hits 1/4 — not when it hits empty.

Tip 3 — ⭐ NEW: Kaliteli yakıt kullanın (Use quality fuel)

Plain English: Not all fuel is equal. Cheap gas stations sometimes sell contaminated, low-octane, or watered-down fuel that can damage your engine and reduce fuel economy.

Why quality matters:
- Higher octane in gasoline means more resistance to "knock / vuruntu" (pre-ignition) — the engine runs more efficiently
- Cleaner fuel means less wear on bujiler, injectors, fuel filters
- Additives in premium fuels (detergents, corrosion inhibitors) keep the fuel system clean
- Watered-down fuel is a real problem in Turkey at unreliable stations — literally diluted with water → engine won't run properly

Rule: buy fuel from reputable branded stations (BP, Shell, Opet, Petrol Ofisi, Total, etc.) in Turkey. Avoid remote / unknown stations unless you have no other choice.

Exam trigger phrase:
- "Kaliteli yakıt kullanmanın faydası nedir?" → Motor verimi artar, yakıt tüketimi düşer, motor ömrü uzar.

Tip 4 — Uygun devirde kullanmak (RECAP — the 2000-3000 RPM rule)

Already covered in slide 3 (2000-3000 devir aralığı). Restated for emphasis.

Tip 5 — ⭐ NEW: 90 km/saat üzerine çıkma (Don't exceed 90 km/h)

Plain English: Aerodynamic drag grows quadratically with speed. A car at 90 km/h experiences much less drag than at 120 km/h, even though the speed difference is only 33%. The physics:

Drag force ∝ v²

That means:
- 60 km/h → baseline drag
- 90 km/h → 2.25× baseline (50% faster, 125% more drag)
- 120 km/h → 4× baseline (100% faster, 300% more drag)

At higher speeds, the engine is fighting against massively more air resistance, and fuel consumption scales accordingly. The fuel-efficient sweet spot for most Turkish passenger cars is around 80-90 km/h on the highway — that's where aerodynamic losses are still manageable and the engine is in a high gear at a comfortable RPM.

Beyond 90 km/h:
- Fuel consumption increases dramatically (a 120 km/h cruise can burn 30-50% more fuel than 90 km/h)
- Engine RPM rises, making it work harder
- Brake distances grow (kinetic energy scales with v²)
- Tire wear accelerates
- Turkish highway speed limits are often 110 or 120, but efficient ≠ maximum legal — legal and efficient are different concepts

Exam trigger phrase:
- "Ekonomik sürüş için azami hangi hızda seyretmek tavsiye edilir?" → 90 km/saat

Practical rule: on long highway trips, cruise at 85-90 km/h even if the speed limit is higher. You'll arrive only a few minutes later but save 20-30% on fuel.

The heuristic — identify the correct answer from sentence structure alone

This is the single highest-value exam-taking trick hoca gave all class 2. It works even if you don't know the specific facts.

"Araçtan kaynaklanan sıkıntı arabanın teknik özelliğinde olması gereken bir şeyin olmamasıyla ilgilidir. Yani mesela lastikler hava basıncı olması gereken de değildir. Ne bileyim işte distribütör kapağı vesair olması gereken de değildir gibi gibi. Olması gereken bir şey olmaması gereken durumdaysa hep olumsuz cümleler vardır. Konuyu bilmeseniz bile buradan soruyu yakalayabilirsiniz. Ya da sürücü yapması gereken bir şeyi yapmamıştır. Mesela bakımlarını yaptırmamıştır. Filtreleri değiştirmemiştir. Hani normal hızla gitmesi gerekiyordur. Yüksek hızda gitmiştir. Değil mi? Sürücünün uyması gereken bir şey vardır. Sürücü bunu yapmamıştır. İnanın şu dört slaytı okumasam bile soru gelse buradan hepiniz çok temiz mis gibi bu soruları kaçırmadan yaparsınız."

And later:

"Bu sefer de olumlu cümleleri dediğim gibi okuyorum... teknik özelliğinin aslında olması gereken değerde olması durumunda araç zaten yakıt tasarrufu [sağlar]."

Plain English — the pattern

On the exam, questions about fuel consumption have two possible directions:

  1. "What causes excess fuel consumption?" → answer is a NEGATIVE / problem statement
  2. "What saves fuel?" → answer is a POSITIVE / correct-state statement

The trick: even if you don't know the specific topic, you can identify which direction the question is going from the sentence structure of the answer options, and pick the matching one.

The pattern in detail

Pattern 1 — Questions about "fazla yakıt yakma nedeni" (why the car burns excess fuel)

Correct answers always have NEGATIVE / problem sentence structure:

Negative phrasing What it describes
"...olmaması" Something that should exist, but doesn't
"...bozuk olması" Something broken
"...ayarsız olması" Something not adjusted
"...tıkalı olması" Something clogged
"...az olması" Something below the correct level
"...bakımsız olması" Something not serviced
"...kaçırması" Something leaking/slipping (like debriyaj)

Example question:

"Aşağıdakilerden hangisi aracın fazla yakıt yakmasının sebebidir?"
A) Hava filtresinin temiz olması
B) Hava filtresinin tıkalı olması ← tıkalı olması = negative structure → CORRECT
C) Bujilerin yeni olması
D) Rölantinin doğru ayarlı olması

Options A, C, D all have positive sentence structure ("clean", "new", "correctly adjusted"). Only option B has negative structure ("tıkalı olması" = "being clogged"). B is the answer — and you can pick it without even knowing what a hava filtresi is.

Pattern 2 — Questions about "yakıt tasarrufu" (fuel saving)

Correct answers always have POSITIVE / correct-state sentence structure:

Positive phrasing What it describes
"...olması" Something that exists / is present
"...doğru ayarlı olması" Something correctly adjusted
"...temiz olması" Something clean
"...normal değerde olması" Something at the correct value
"...sıkı olmaması" Something not too tight (note: this is a "not" statement but describes a correct state — subtle case)

Example question:

"Aşağıdakilerden hangisi yakıt tasarrufunu sağlar?"
A) Bujilerin ayarsız olması
B) Hava filtresinin tıkalı olması
C) Lastiklerin hava basıncının normal değerde olması ← positive structure → CORRECT
D) Debriyajın kaçırması

Options A, B, D have negative structure ("ayarsız", "tıkalı", "kaçırması"). Only C has positive structure ("normal değerde olması" = "being at the normal value"). C is the answer.

The full decision tree

Reading the question:
│
├── Contains "fazla yakıt", "yakıt israfı", "yakıt sarfiyatı artar"
│   └── PICK the option with NEGATIVE structure (bozuk, tıkalı, ayarsız, olmaması)
│
├── Contains "yakıt tasarrufu", "ekonomik sürüş", "yakıtı azaltmak"
│   └── PICK the option with POSITIVE structure (temiz, doğru, normal değerde, düzenli)
│
└── Contains "sürücü hatası" / "sürücü kaynaklı"
    └── PICK the option that describes a BAD HABIT (ani hızlanma, düşük viteste, marşa fazla basma)

Why this works — the deeper pattern

Every fact you've learned about ekonomik sürüş is symmetric:

  • Hava filtresi tıkalı (wastes fuel) ↔ Hava filtresi temiz (saves fuel)
  • Lastik basıncı az (wastes fuel) ↔ Lastik basıncı normal (saves fuel)
  • Fren ayarı sıkı (wastes fuel) ↔ Fren ayarı uygun (saves fuel)
  • Düşük viteste seyretmek (wastes fuel) ↔ Uygun viteste seyretmek (saves fuel)

Every "correct state" has a mirror "broken state". Exam writers use the same 9 vehicle faults and 5 driver habits — just flipping them for positive vs negative questions. Once you know the pattern, you recognize the answer from the sentence direction alone.

Critical exam takeaway

Yakıt sarfiyatı soruları: olumsuz cümle yapısı → doğru cevap.
Yakıt tasarrufu soruları: olumlu cümle yapısı → doğru cevap.

6. Why this matters for the exam

The Ekonomik Araç Kullanma topic is a full exam subject with several reliable question patterns:

  1. "Aşağıdakilerden hangisi yakıt tüketimini artırır?" → pick any of the 9 vehicle-side faults OR the 5 driver-side habits from slides 1–2
  2. "Ekonomik sürüşte hangi devir aralığı tercih edilir?" → 2000-3000 devir
  3. "Yakıt tasarrufu için ne yapılmalıdır?" → recite any of the 8 items from slide 4
  4. "Jikle çekili unutulursa ne olur?" → Motor rich çalışır, yakıt tüketimi artar.
  5. "Düşük hava basınçlı lastiklerin yakıta etkisi nedir?" → Sürtünmeyi artırır, yakıt tüketimini artırır.
  6. "Sıkı fren ayarı yakıta nasıl etki eder?" → Balatalar aşınır, motor sürekli dirençle çalışır, yakıt tüketimi artar.
  7. "Uzun süre motoru stop etmek yakıta etki eder mi?" → Hayır, aksine restart daha çok yakıt harcar.

  10. "Marşa gereğinden fazla basmak ne yapar?" → Motoru boğar, yakıt ve akü israfı olur.
