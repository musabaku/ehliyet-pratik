Lastikler — Tires

Last updated: 2026-04-14 (class 2)
Slides covered: Tire sidewall markings, air pressure contact patches, maintenance & common faults (class 1); Lastik değişimi prosedürü — 10 steps (class 2 post-break); Lastik önemi recap slide

Note on placement: Tires are the last link of the drivetrain chain (see 08-güç-aktarma-organları.md), but they get tested as their own topic on the exam. This file contains all tire-specific content.

1. Overview

The tire (lastik) is the only part of the car that touches the road. Every bit of force — engine power, braking, steering, cornering grip — has to pass through the small "contact patch" where the tire meets the asphalt. That's why tire condition is one of the most heavily tested maintenance topics on the exam.

Three things matter most about a tire:
1. What it is — its specification (markings on the sidewall)
2. How inflated it is — air pressure (affects grip, wear, fuel economy)
3. Whether it's balanced and aligned — vibration, steering, even wear

Each of these has its own slide covered below.

What the slide showed

- "Lastiğin kabul edilebilir maksimum dış dönüşleri gösteren bilgiler..." (info showing the maximum allowable external rotations)

Clean English explanation — decoding a tire marking

Every tire has a code like 195/65 R 15 91 H printed on its sidewall. This code tells you the exact specification. Breaking it down:

Part of code What it means This example
195 Kesit genişliği — section width in mm 195 mm wide
/65 Seri (aspect ratio) — sidewall height as % of width Sidewall height = 65% of 195 mm
R Lastik tipi — construction type (Radyal = Radial) Radial
15 Jant çapı — rim diameter in inches Fits a 15" rim
91 Yük endeksi — load index (coded) 91 = 615 kg max per tire
H Hız sembolü — speed rating (coded) H = 210 km/h max

Key terms from the slide

  • Kesit genişliği (Section width) — how wide the tire is, measured straight across the tread, in millimeters (mm)
  • Lastiğin serisi / Kesit oranı (Aspect ratio / Series) — the height of the sidewall expressed as a percentage of the width. A "65 series" tire has a sidewall 65% as tall as the tread is wide. Low numbers = low-profile sporty tire; high numbers = taller/softer tire.
  • Lastik tipi — R (Radyal) — the construction method. Radyal (Radial) tires are the modern standard. The letter "R" right before the rim size means radial.
  • Jant çapı — the rim diameter the tire fits, measured in inches (inç). A "15" means the tire fits a 15-inch wheel.
  • Yük endeksi (Load index) — a coded number meaning max weight per tire. 91 = 615 kg. Higher index = higher weight capacity.
  • Hız sembolü (Speed symbol) — a letter code meaning max speed. H = 210 km/h. Common codes: T = 190, H = 210, V = 240, W = 270.
  • Tubeless (iç lastiksiz) — modern tires that hold air directly against the rim (no separate inner tube inside).

Critical exam facts

  • "R" means Radyal (radial) — if an exam option says "R = Rim" or "R = Road", it's wrong.
  • Kesit genişliği is in MM, jant çapı is in INCH — don't mix the units up.
  • Yük endeksi and hız sembolü are coded numbers/letters, not literal kg or km/h — they map to a table.
  • Lastiğin serisi (aspect ratio) is a percentage, not a millimeter measurement. This trips people up constantly.

3. SLIDE 2 — Hava Basıncına Göre Yerle Temas Etme Şekilleri (Contact Patch by Air Pressure)

What the slide showed

Three cross-sectional tire illustrations side by side, each with downward hatching showing how the tire touches the ground. Title:

Hava Basınçlarına Göre Lastiklerin Yerle Temas Etme Şekilleri
(The ways tires contact the ground according to air pressure.)

The three states:
- Doğru (Correct) — full, even contact across the full tread width
- Eksik (Under-inflated / low pressure) — the center of the tire lifts up; only the two edge sides touch the ground
- Fazla (Over-inflated / high pressure) — the edges lift up; only the center touches the ground

Clean English explanation

The air pressure inside a tire directly changes its shape when pressed against the road. Think of it like a balloon: low pressure = soft, the tire squishes and only its outer edges contact the road; high pressure = stiff, the tire bulges outward and only its middle contacts the road. Only at the correct pressure does the tire sit flat with its full tread contacting the asphalt.

State Turkish Contact pattern Consequence
Correct Doğru Full width touches the ground Even wear, best grip, best fuel economy
Under-inflated Eksik (az) Only the two edge sides touch Edges wear out fast; fuel consumption rises; vehicle loses energy
Over-inflated Fazla Only the middle touches Center wears out fast; steering vibrations; harsh ride

Critical exam facts

  • Eksik basınç → kenarlardan aşınma (low pressure → wear on the edges)
  • Fazla basınç → orta kısımdan aşınma (high pressure → wear in the middle/center)
  • Doğru basınç = eşit aşınma, düşük yakıt tüketimi, maksimum yol tutuşu (correct pressure = even wear, low fuel consumption, max grip)

Memory trick

  • Eksik (low) = Edges wear (think "E matches E")
  • Fazla (high) = middle wears (opposite of low — if not edges, then center)

4. SLIDE 3 — Lastiklerin Bakım ve Basit Arızaları (Tire Maintenance & Common Faults)

What the slide said

LASTİKLERİN BAKIM VE BASİT ARIZALARI

  • Lastik hava değeri tavsiye edilen değerden az ise; lastikler kenar kısımlarından aşınma gösterir, aracın enerji kaybı artar ve araçta sarfiyat oluşur.
  • Lastik hava değeri tavsiye edilen değerden fazla ise; lastikler orta kısmından aşınır ve direksiyonda titreşimler oluşur.
  • Lastiklerde balansızlık (dengesizlik) var ise,
  • Lastikler titreşim yapar, direksiyonda belirli kilometrelerde titreşim oluşur, direksiyon hakimiyeti zorlaşır, bağlantı somun ve cıvataları gevşer, ön takım ayarları çabuk bozulur, lastikler dengesiz aşınır.

Clean English translation

Tire maintenance & common faults:

  • If the tire air value is less than recommended: tires show wear on the edge sides, the vehicle's energy loss increases, and fuel consumption rises.
  • If the tire air value is more than recommended: tires wear in the middle section and vibrations occur in the steering wheel.
  • If there is imbalance in the tires:
  • Tires vibrate, vibration appears at certain speeds in the steering wheel, steering control becomes difficult, connection nuts and bolts loosen, front suspension alignment goes out of spec quickly, and tires wear unevenly.

Clean English explanation — the 3 common tire faults

Fault 1 — Hava az (Under-inflation)

If the tire pressure is lower than recommended:
- Kenar kısımlarından aşınma — wear on the edge sides (not the middle)
- Enerji kaybı artar — the vehicle loses more energy rolling (more rolling resistance)
- Sarfiyat oluşur — fuel consumption rises
- Also: softer handling, sluggish steering

Fault 2 — Hava fazla (Over-inflation)

If the tire pressure is higher than recommended:
- Orta kısmından aşınır — the center of the tread wears out
- Direksiyonda titreşimler oluşur — vibrations appear in the steering wheel
- Also: harsh ride, less grip, more likely to blow out on potholes

Fault 3 — Balansızlık / Dengesizlik (Imbalance)

If the tires are not properly balanced on the wheel:
- Lastikler titreşim yapar — the tires vibrate
- Direksiyonda belirli kilometrelerde titreşim oluşur — at certain speeds, steering wheel vibrates noticeably (typically at highway speeds)
- Direksiyon hakimiyeti zorlaşır — steering control becomes difficult
- Bağlantı somun ve cıvataları gevşer — connection nuts and bolts loosen over time from the vibration
- Ön takım ayarları çabuk bozulur — front suspension alignment (kamber, toe) goes out of spec quickly
- Lastikler dengesiz aşınır — tires wear unevenly (flat spots, cupping)

Critical exam facts (memorize these 3 cause-effect chains!)

Cause Effect (Turkish + English)
Az basınç (under-inflation / low pressure) → kenarlardan aşınma (wear on the edges) + enerji kaybı (energy loss / rolling resistance) + sarfiyat artışı (increased fuel consumption)
Fazla basınç (over-inflation / high pressure) → orta kısımdan aşınma (wear in the center / middle) + direksiyonda titreşim (steering wheel vibration)
Balansızlık / dengesizlik (imbalance) → titreşim (vibration) + hakimiyet zorluğu (loss of steering control) + cıvataların gevşemesi (loosening of bolts / nuts) + dengesiz aşınma (uneven wear) + ön takım ayarlarının bozulması (front suspension alignment going out of spec)

These three cause-effect mappings generate at least 3-4 exam questions between them.

4B. SLIDE 4 — Lastik Bakım Kuralları (Tire Care Rules)

What the slide said

Tire care rules:
- Isıdan dolayı şişen lastiklerin hava değeri indirilmemeli, olduğu basınçta bırakılmalıdır.
- Lastik hava değerleri her araca binerken kontrol edilmelidir.
- Kontrol esnasında lastiklerin soğuk olmasına dikkat edilmelidir.
- Araç üzerinde bulunan lastiklerin hepsi eşit değerde olmalıdır.
- Kar zincirleri çekici lastiklere takılmalıdır.
- Tekerlekler üzerinde bulunan bijonlar sıkma işlemi yapılmadan önce mutlaka kuru bir bezle silinmelidir.

Clean English translation

  1. Tires that have swollen/expanded due to heat should NOT have their air pressure reduced — leave them at their current pressure.
  2. Tire air pressure should be checked every time you get into the vehicle (before each trip).
  3. During the check, the tires must be cold — don't measure pressure on hot tires.
  4. All tires on the vehicle must be at equal (eşit) pressure.
  5. Snow chains must be mounted on the driving (çekici) tires — the wheels that receive engine power.
  6. Before tightening the lug nuts (bijon), they must be wiped with a dry cloth.

Clean English explanation — the 6 golden rules of tire care

Rule 1 — Don't release pressure from hot tires

"Isıdan dolayı şişen lastiklerin havası indirilmemeli, olduğu basınçta bırakılmalıdır."

When you drive, friction heats the tires and the air inside them expands. An expanded pressure reading is higher than the "cold" spec, but that's normal. If you release air to bring a hot tire back to the cold spec, then when the tire cools off later, the pressure will be too low — and you'll be driving on under-inflated tires.

The rule: Never let air out of hot tires. Leave them alone until they cool.

Rule 2 — Check pressure before every trip

"Lastik hava değerleri her araca binerken kontrol edilmelidir."

Not every week, not every month — every time you get into the car, you should visually check (or feel) that the tires are properly inflated. In practice this means a quick glance before you get in. Use a gauge regularly.

Rule 3 — Only measure pressure when the tires are cold

"Kontrol esnasında lastiklerin soğuk olmasına dikkat edilmelidir."

Because driving heats tires and raises their pressure, the only accurate reading is when the tire is at ambient temperature — typically first thing in the morning before driving, or after the car has sat for at least a few hours. This is directly connected to Rule 1.

Rule 4 — All four tires at equal pressure

"Araç üzerinde bulunan lastiklerin hepsi eşit değerde olmalıdır."

If one tire is at a different pressure than the others, the car will pull to one side, wear unevenly, and handle unpredictably. All four must match the manufacturer's spec.

Rule 5 — Snow chains on the driving (çekici) wheels

"Kar zincirleri çekici lastiklere takılmalıdır."

Snow chains (kar zinciri) give grip in snow. They must go on the çekici lastikler — the "driving" or "traction" tires — which are the wheels that actually receive engine power:
- Front-wheel drive (FWD) car → chains on the front tires
- Rear-wheel drive (RWD) car → chains on the rear tires
- 4WD → ideally all four, but minimum on the main driven axle

A chain on a non-driving wheel does nothing because that wheel isn't pushing the car forward.

Rule 6 — Wipe lug nuts dry before tightening

"Bijonlar sıkma işlemi yapılmadan önce mutlaka kuru bir bezle silinmelidir."

Bijon = lug nut/wheel bolt (the fasteners that hold the wheel onto the hub). Before tightening them during a tire change, wipe them clean and dry. Wet, oily, or dirty bijonlar give inaccurate torque readings and can loosen in service — potentially causing the wheel to come off while driving. This is a critical safety step.

Critical exam facts

  • Hot tires → never release air. Wait until they cool, then measure.
  • Pressure check = cold tires only, every time you drive.
  • All 4 tires at equal pressure.
  • Kar zinciri → çekici lastiklere (driving wheels) — NOT any four.
  • Bijon → kuru bezle silindikten sonra sıkılır (lug nuts are tightened AFTER being wiped dry).

4C. Hoca's Live Class Additions (2026-04-13)

Tire pressure is NOT a universal number

A student asked "lastiğe basacağımız hava en fazla otuz, doğru mudur?" (The max air pressure is 30, right?). Hoca's response:

"Araçtan araca farklılık gösteriyor. Otomobil mi? Motorsiklet mi? Kamyon mu? Traktör mü? Her aracın lastiğinin üzerinde yazar. Orada ne yazıyorsa herkes kendi aracının lastiğini o değerde basmak zorunda."

Translation: There is no universal "correct" tire pressure — it depends on the vehicle type AND the specific tire. A car, motorcycle, truck, and tractor all have different requirements. The correct value is printed on the tire sidewall and/or in your car's user manual (often on a sticker inside the driver's door jamb).

Exam answer approach: If an exam question asks "what should the tire pressure be?" the correct general answer is "araç üreticisinin tavsiye ettiği değerde" (at the value recommended by the vehicle manufacturer) — NOT a specific number like "30 PSI" or "32 PSI."

4D. Additional Exam Facts (from portal E-Kitap Subjects 2 & 5)

Our slide-based notes captured the basics, but the portal has additional exam-testable facts the hoca didn't dwell on. These are all drawn from the e-kitap curriculum and are fair game on the theory exam.

Tire construction types — definitions you'll be asked

Turkish English Meaning
Şambrelli lastik Tube-type tire (with inner tube) Has a separate iç lastik (inner tube) inside. Old-school construction.
Dubleks lastik Tubeless tire No inner tube — the tire seals directly to the rim. Modern standard.
Radyal lastik Radial tire Wide-base construction; the "R" in markings like 195/65 R 15. The modern standard for passenger cars.

⚠️ Exam trap: "Dubleks" is a Turkish loanword that means tubeless — don't confuse it with English "duplex." The exam may ask "Dubleks lastik ne demektir?" → answer: iç lastiği olmayan lastik (a tire without an inner tube).

Do NOT mix tire types on the same axle

"Ön lastiklerden birine dubleks birine de şambrelli lastik takılırsa bir tarafa çekme yapar."
(If one front tire is tubeless and the other is tube-type, the vehicle will pull to one side.)

Rule: Both front tires must be the same type (both tubeless OR both tube-type). Mixing creates uneven handling.

Front tires must always be newest

"Ön lastikler daima yeni olmalıdır. Ön lastiğin biri yeni biri eski olursa araç çekme yapar."
(The front tires must always be new. If one is new and the other is old, the car will pull to one side.)

Rule: In any tire rotation or replacement, the newest tires always go on the front. Mixing new + old on the front causes pulling.

Low pressure on ONE front tire → pulls to that side

"Ön lastiklerin birinde hava basıncı az olursa direksiyon az olan tarafa çekme yapar."
(If one front tire has low pressure, the steering pulls to the low-pressure side.)

Exam rule: When a car "çekme yapıyor" (pulls), first suspect is uneven front tire pressure.

Low pressure also affects BRAKING

Our existing notes listed these symptoms of low pressure: edge wear + energy loss + fuel consumption rise. The portal adds one more:

"Lastik hava basıncı az olursa frenleme iyi olmaz."
(If tire pressure is low, braking performance degrades.)

Complete list of low-pressure consequences:
1. Kenardan aşınma (edge wear)
2. Enerji kaybı (energy loss / rolling resistance)
3. Sarfiyat artar (fuel consumption rises)
4. Frenleme iyi olmaz (braking performance is poor) ← new from portal

Lastik diş derinliği (tread depth) — critical exam numbers

"Lastik diş derinliği 4 mm'nin aşağısına düşerse değiştirilir."
(If the tread depth falls below 4 mm, the tire must be replaced.)

Legal and practical thresholds:

Standard Minimum depth Notes
Legal minimum (by Turkish regulation) 1.6 mm Below this is a traffic violation
Summer tire replacement threshold 3 mm Below this, summer tires should be replaced
Winter tire replacement threshold 4 mm Winter tires need more depth for snow grip
General replacement recommendation 4 mm The portal's general rule for passenger tires

⚠️ Critical exam fact: The minimum legal tread depth is 1.6 mm. If you see "yasal asgari diş derinliği" as an exam term, the answer is 1.6 mm.

M+S Kış Lastiği — the legal winter tire mandate

"Yolcu ve eşya taşımalarında kullanılan araçların her yılın 1 Aralık ile 1 Nisan tarihleri arasında kış lastiği kullanmaları zorunludur."

Memorize this date range:

Kış lastiği kullanımı 1 Aralık – 1 Nisan tarihleri arasında ZORUNLUDUR (for passenger and cargo transport vehicles).

M+S on the sidewall = "Mud and Snow" marking — this is the official indicator of a winter-rated tire.

Kış lastiği özellikleri (Winter tire features) — why they're different

The portal specifically highlights:

  1. Daha fazla diş derinliği (deeper tread) — for better snow/slush displacement
  2. Daha az desen doluluk oranı (less "filled-in" tread pattern) — more gaps to eject snow, ice, water, mud
  3. Kılcal damarlı ve omuz blokları dişli yapı (capillary-style sipes + toothed shoulder blocks) — extra traction on snowy, slushy, muddy surfaces
  4. Özel karışım (special rubber compound) — doesn't harden in cold temperatures, maintains grip
  5. Yüksek silika oranı (high silica content) — preserves grip on wet surfaces

Lastik çeşitleri — 3 types

The portal lists three tire types (exam answers "lastik çeşitleri kaç tanedir? / nelerdir?"):

  1. Yaz lastikleri (summer tires) — rahatlık ve konfor (comfort-optimized, harder compound)
  2. Kış lastikleri (winter tires) — güvenli sürüş ve frenleme in winter conditions
  3. Dört mevsim lastikler (all-season tires) — compromise between the two

Lastik değişim prosedürü (Tire change procedure) — step-by-step

This is tested on the exam as a safety procedure. Full ordered sequence:

  1. Park on flat ground (düz zemin)
  2. Turn on hazard flashers (flaşör yakılır) to make the car visible
  3. Engage the handbrake (el freni çekilir)
  4. Turn off the engine (motor durdurulur)
  5. Place a wedge/chock (takoz) behind one of the wheels that stays on the ground, to prevent the car from rolling
  6. Retrieve spare tire (stepne) and jack (kriko) from their storage location
  7. Align the jack (kriko) correctly on the frame — wrong placement can damage the car
  8. Loosen the lug nuts (bijonları gevşet) with the lug wrench, before lifting the car (easier when the wheel can't spin)
  9. Raise the car with the jack
  10. Remove the hub cap (jant kapağı / göbek kapağı) if present
  11. Fully unscrew bijonlar and remove the old tire
  12. Mount the spare tire and hand-tighten the bijonlar into place
  13. Partial tighten with the wrench (don't fully torque yet)
  14. Lower the car slowly until all four wheels touch the ground
  15. Fully tighten the bijonlar now that the car is back on the ground
  16. After driving a few km, STOP and re-check that the bijonlar are still tight

⚠️ Exam-specific facts from this procedure:
- Motor durdurulmalı, el freni çekilmeli, tekerleklere takoz konulmalıdır (engine off, handbrake on, wheels chocked — 3 things before you even pick up the wrench)
- Bijonlar kuru bezle silinmelidir (before tightening — from hoca slide 4)
- Full torque AFTER the car is on the ground, not while it's in the air

Patinaj önleyiciler (Anti-skid devices) — 3 types

The portal lists 3 types of anti-skid aids for snow/ice conditions:

  1. Zincir (chains) — metal chains wrapped around the driving tires
  2. Kar çorabı (snow sock / textile snow cover) — fabric alternative to chains, easier to install
  3. Patinaj önleyici sprey (anti-skid spray) — a chemical spray applied to the tire surface for temporary grip

Rule from slide 4 still applies: All three must be applied to the çekici lastikler (driving wheels) — front wheels for FWD cars.

Lastik ömrü (Tire lifespan)

"Ortalama 4-5 yıl veya 40-50.000 km sonra aracınızın lastiklerini değiştirmelisiniz."

Rule of thumb: Replace tires after 4–5 years OR 40,000–50,000 km, whichever comes first. Rubber degrades with age even if you don't drive much.

Lastik üretim tarihi (Tire manufacturing date) — DOT code

Every tire has a DOT code on the sidewall. The portal explains how to read it:

"Son iki rakam üretim yılı, ilk iki rakamsa üretildiği haftadır. Örnek: '4612' = 2012 yılının 46. haftası."

Format: A 4-digit code where:
- First 2 digits = manufacturing week (1–52)
- Last 2 digits = manufacturing year (e.g., 12 = 2012, 24 = 2024)

Rule: "Alacağınız lastiklerin son 2 yıl içinde üretilmiş olması gerekir."
(Tires you buy should have been manufactured within the last 2 years.)

Why this matters: Rubber starts degrading on the shelf even before a tire is mounted on a car. Tires that have been sitting in a warehouse for 3+ years are already aged — don't buy them new.

Daily tire check — portal's günlük kontrol rule

"Aracın lastikleri günde en az bir kez kontrol edilmelidir."

Rule: Tires should be checked at least once a day. This reinforces the slide 4 rule ("her araca binerken kontrol edilmelidir" — check every time you get in the car). The portal formalizes it as a daily minimum.

This is part of the broader günlük araç kontrolleri (daily vehicle checks) checklist — a topic that deserves its own notes file once the hoca covers it (currently a gap per coverage-map.md).

4E. SLIDES (2026-04-14) — LASTİK DEĞİŞİMİ PROSEDÜRÜ (How to Change a Flat Tire)

What the slides showed

A 4-slide mini-chapter on roadside tire replacement:

  1. Intro: "Lastik Değişimi" title + photo of a car with a flat tire + cartoon of a man reading the manual + humorous caption "Patlak lastik cinsiyet tanımıyor" (a flat tire doesn't care about gender).
  2. 1. Adım + 2. Adım: Place the reflector triangle + take out tools from the trunk. İPUCU: wear gloves.
  3. Same slide + hoca's live annotations: hoca drew additional marks on the slide showing where the reflector triangle should go (distance from the car) and pointing out tool positions.
  4. 3. Adım + 4. Adım: Check the spare tire + loosen the bolts (vidalar) while the car is still on the ground.

Overview — why every driver must know this

Otomobil kullanan herkes, patlamış bir lastiğin nasıl değiştirileceğini bilmelidir. Şehir içinde, patlak bir lastik için çeşitli çözümler bulunabilir, fakat şehirler arası bir yolda, lastiğinizi tek başınıza değiştirmek zorunda kalabilirsiniz.

Every driver must know how to change a flat tire. In a city, you can call for help — a tow truck, tire repair shop, roadside assistance. But on an intercity highway, far from any town, you may have to change the tire yourself with no help available. Waiting for assistance in a rural area can take hours, and you may be blocking traffic or in an unsafe position. Self-reliance is critical.

The cartoon joke "Patlak lastik cinsiyet tanımıyor" (a flat tire doesn't discriminate by gender) is pointed at the stereotype that tire changes are "men's work" — in reality, every driver regardless of gender must know this procedure because a flat tire can happen to anyone, anywhere, anytime.

The 4 steps covered on these slides (Adım 1–4)

The full procedure has many more steps — see [portal reference in section 4B below] for the complete 12-step list. These slides cover the first 4 steps.

ADIM 1 — Reflektörü Yerleştirin (Place the Warning Triangle)

1. Adım — Reflektörü yerleştirin.
Step 1 — Place the reflector (warning triangle).

Icon: A red triangular warning sign (reflektör / ikaz üçgeni).

What this means

Before touching the car, you warn approaching traffic that you're stopped on the side of the road. This is done with the reflektör / ikaz üçgeni — a red-and-white triangular sign that folds out from a plastic case, usually stored in the trunk. It has reflective material that glows brightly when headlights hit it.

How far back to place the reflector (memorize the distances)

The Turkish traffic regulation specifies:

Road type Distance behind the car
Yerleşim birimi içi (urban / city) 30–50 metre
Yerleşim birimi dışı (rural / intercity highway) 150 metre

Why the distance matters: at highway speeds, a driver needs several seconds to see, recognize, and react to a stopped car. The reflector gives them that early warning. Too close → not enough reaction time → rear-end collision.

The drill

  1. Stop the car somewhere safe — off the road shoulder if possible
  2. Turn on the dörtlü (hazard flashers) immediately
  3. Get the reflektör from the trunk
  4. Walk 30 m (city) or 100 m (rural) BEHIND the car, carrying the triangle
  5. Unfold and place it on the road surface so it points toward approaching traffic
  6. Walk back to the car (always watching for oncoming traffic)
  7. Only NOW start working on the tire

Critical exam fact

Lastik değiştirmeden önce ilk iş: yerleşim birimi içinde 30–50 m, yerleşim birimi dışında 150 m uzağa reflektörü (ikaz üçgeni) yerleştirmektir.
Before changing a tire, the first thing to do is place the warning triangle — 30–50 m in the city, 150 m on the highway — behind the car.

Her araçta iki (2) işaretleme reflektörü bulundurmak zorunludur — one for the front, one for the back.
Every vehicle must carry two warning triangles — one for the front and one for the back.

ADIM 2 — Malzemeleri Çıkartın (Take Out the Tools)

2. Adım — Malzemeleri çıkartın. Gerekli malzemeleri bagajdan çıkarın. Kriko ve bijon anahtarı genellikle yedek lastiğin ortasındaki boşlukta bulunur.

Step 2 — Take out the tools. Get the necessary tools out of the trunk. The jack (kriko) and the lug wrench (bijon anahtarı) are usually found in the hollow center of the spare tire.

The tools you need

Turkish English What it does
Kriko Jack Lifts the car off the ground so you can remove the wheel
Bijon anahtarı Lug wrench / wheel brace Loosens and tightens the lug bolts (bijonlar) that hold the wheel on
Yedek lastik Spare tire The replacement wheel
Reflektör / ikaz üçgeni Warning triangle Already placed in step 1

Bonus items (recommended but not always provided):

Turkish English Why
Eldivenler Gloves Keep hands clean and protected
Fener / el feneri Flashlight For night-time changes
Takoz Wheel chock Block the other wheels so the car doesn't roll off the jack
Tahta parçası / tuğla Wood block / brick Improvised chock
Fosforlu yelek Hi-viz vest Makes you visible to traffic

Where the tools are stored

Most Turkish cars store the kriko and bijon anahtarı in the hollow center of the spare tire, which itself is in a well under the trunk floor. To access:

  1. Remove everything from the trunk (cargo, etc.)
  2. Lift the trunk floor carpet — reveals the spare wheel well
  3. Unscrew the hold-down bolt in the center of the spare tire (usually a wing nut)
  4. Lift the spare tire out (or tilt it)
  5. The kriko and bijon anahtarı are in the foam tool insert inside the spare wheel hub
  6. Some cars also store them in side compartments in the trunk

If you've never looked, find them at home BEFORE you have a flat. You don't want to be discovering the tool layout of your car for the first time on the side of a highway.

İPUCU (TIP) — Wear gloves

Lastik değiştirirken eldiven kullanarak, ellerinizin hem kirlenmesini hem de zarar görmesini önleyebilirsiniz.
When changing a tire, wearing gloves prevents your hands from getting dirty and from being injured.

Why gloves matter:
- Tires and wheels are filthy — black brake dust, road grime, oil, grease all over them
- The bijon anahtarı has sharp metal edges that can cut you when you slip
- You may need to lie down or kneel on hot or cold asphalt
- After changing a tire with bare hands, you'll be black up to your elbows

Cheap work gloves (mekanik eldiven / iş eldiveni) cost 10–20 TL and should be kept permanently in the trunk alongside the tools.

ADIM 3 — Yedek Lastiği Kontrol Edin (Check the Spare Tire)

3. Adım — Yedek lastiği kontrol edin. Yedek lastik çıkarın ve sıcak (şişkin/havalı) olup olmadığını kontrol edin.

Step 3 — Check the spare tire. Take out the spare and check whether it's pumped up / in good condition.

Why this step matters

A flat spare tire is useless. If you don't check it before starting the change, you might:

  1. Jack up the car
  2. Remove the flat tire
  3. Try to mount the spare
  4. Discover the spare is also flat or worn out
  5. Be stuck with NO working wheel and a partially disassembled car

To prevent this, every driver should check the spare tire periodically — at least once every few months — even if they haven't had a flat. Things to check on the spare:

  • Hava basıncı (air pressure) — is it at the correct pressure? Spares slowly lose air over months.
  • Diş derinliği (tread depth) — is it still legal (≥ 1.6 mm)?
  • Çatlak / bulging / cracks — has the rubber degraded from age?
  • Korozyon on the jant (wheel rim) — rust can cause leaks

If your spare is a "stepne" (space-saver / donut spare) — the small temporary tire — it has its own rules: max 80 km/h, max 80 km total distance, then replace with a real tire ASAP. Modern cars often ship with these instead of full-size spares.

ADIM 4 — Vidaları Gevşetin (Loosen the Lug Bolts — BEFORE Jacking Up!)

4. Adım — Vidaları gevşetin. Bijon anahtarını kullanarak vidaları gevşetin. Vidalar el ile gevşetemeyecek kadar sıkıysa, bijon anahtarına ayağınızla basarsınız.

Step 4 — Loosen the lug bolts. Using the lug wrench, loosen the bolts. If the bolts are too tight to loosen by hand, you can step on the wrench with your foot.

The critical timing rule

Vidalar (lug bolts) must be LOOSENED WHILE THE CAR IS STILL ON THE GROUND — not after you've jacked it up. This is the single most important rule of tire changing.

Why: when the wheel is on the ground, the tire's friction against the road holds the wheel still while you apply torque with the wrench. Without this friction, the wheel would just spin when you try to loosen the bolt.

If you jack up the car first, then try to loosen:
- You push on the bijon anahtarı → the wheel spins freely in the air → nothing happens
- You can't get the bolts off
- You have to lower the car back down, loosen on the ground, then re-jack up
- You wasted time and effort

Rule: loosen all bolts by ~1 turn while the car is grounded, then jack up, then finish removing them.

The "ayağınızla basarsınız" technique

Factory-torqued lug bolts are extremely tight — often tightened to 100+ Newton-meters. A normal person's arm strength is sometimes not enough to break them loose, especially if they've been on for a long time (corrosion can "freeze" them).

The trick: put the bijon anahtarı horizontally on the bolt so the handle sticks out to the side, then STEP ON THE HANDLE with your foot and put your bodyweight on it. Your leg muscles can generate 10× the force of your arm muscles.

Direction:
- Saat tersi (counter-clockwise) to loosen ("Lefty Loosey")
- Saat yönü (clockwise) to tighten ("Righty Tighty")

This is the standard for all right-hand threads (which is ~99% of all bolts on a car).

UYARI — Don't fully remove the bolts while the car is on the ground

UYARI: Tekerleğin yerinden çıkma ihtimaline karşı, kriko ile arabayı kaldırmadan vidaları tamamen sökmeyin.

WARNING: To prevent the wheel from falling off, do NOT fully remove the bolts before lifting the car with the jack.

Plain English: In step 4, you loosen the bolts — maybe 1 full turn each — but you don't remove them entirely. The bolts should still be in place, just no longer clamping the wheel tight.

Why: if you remove the bolts while the car is on the ground:
- The wheel can slip off the hub and fall over
- If the car shifts, the wheel could pinch your fingers
- You might damage the hub alignment

Only AFTER the car is jacked up should you fully remove the bolts and take the wheel off.

The correct sequence is always:
1. Place reflektör (step 1)
2. Get tools (step 2)
3. Check spare (step 3)
4. Loosen bolts on the ground (step 4)
5. Jack up the car
6. Fully remove bolts and wheel
7. Put on spare
8. Start bolts by hand
9. Lower the car partially (some weight on wheel)
10. Tighten bolts fully
11. Lower fully
12. Re-tighten after driving a few km

The star / cross pattern for tightening

When re-tightening the new tire, don't tighten bolts in a circle — tighten them in a star / cross pattern:

   1
   |
 4 + 3
   |
   2

With 5 bolts: 1 → 3 → 5 → 2 → 4 (skipping around). Gradual, even torque. This ensures the wheel seats flat against the hub without warping.

ADIM 5 — Krikoyu Yerleştirin (Place and Lift the Jack)

5. Adım — Krikoyu yerleştirin. Krikoyu arabanın patlak lastik tarafına yerleştirin ve kolunu çevirerek tekerleği çıkarabileceğiniz kadar yükseltin.

UYARI: Bu durumdayken arabaya yaslanmayın veya arabayı sallayacak bir hareket yapmayın.

Step 5 — Place the jack. Put the jack on the side of the car where the flat tire is, and turn the handle to lift the car just enough to remove the wheel.

WARNING: While the car is on the jack, do not lean on the car or make any movements that could rock it.

Where exactly to place the kriko

This is important and often missed: the kriko must be placed under a reinforced jacking point on the car's chassis, not just "anywhere under the car". Every car has 4 specific jacking points (one near each wheel), usually marked with a notch, arrow, or triangle on the sill (the body panel below the door).

If you jack up the car at a non-reinforced point:
- The body panel will bend / crumple under the jack's pressure
- You can damage thousands of lira of bodywork
- The jack can slip off the bent panel, dropping the car suddenly
- Severe injury risk

To find the jacking points on an unfamiliar car: check the kullanım kılavuzu (owner's manual) — it shows exactly where they are. They're usually right behind the front wheel and right in front of the rear wheel, about 10 cm inside the edge of the sill.

How much to lift

Just enough to clear the wheel — maybe 2–3 cm above the ground once the wheel is off. Over-lifting is dangerous because it increases the distance the car would fall if the jack slipped.

The UYARI — do not lean on or rock the car

While the car is on the jack, it's balanced on a single point (the jack) + the 3 other wheels. The jack is a thin column that can tip sideways if the car rocks. Do not:
- Lean your weight on the car
- Get into the car
- Let anyone sit in the car
- Shake or push the car
- Stand under the car
- Walk around it forcefully

One tip from the car → the jack tips over → the car crashes down onto the wheel hub, destroying it, and potentially onto anyone's limbs underneath.

Extra safety tip (not on slide but critical):

Place the removed flat tire UNDER the car, flat, next to the hub. If the jack fails, the car will fall onto the tire instead of onto the ground / onto you. The tire acts as a safety cushion and buys you time to get out of the way.

ADIM 6 — Tekerleği Çıkarın (Remove the Wheel)

6. Adım — Tekerleği çıkarın. Tüm vidaları sökün ve tekerleği yerinden çıkarın.

Step 6 — Remove the wheel. Take out all the bolts and remove the wheel from its position.

Details

Now that the car is lifted and stable:

  1. Finish removing each bolt — unscrew them the rest of the way (you already loosened them in step 4)
  2. Keep the bolts in a safe place — put them in your pocket, in the jant kapağı (hubcap), or line them up on the ground. Do NOT lose them — without all the bolts, you can't reattach the spare.
  3. Grip the flat tire with both hands on the tread (top and bottom of the outer edge)
  4. Pull the tire straight off the hub — sometimes it's stuck by rust and needs a firm tug or a kick from behind
  5. Set the flat tire aside — and if you want extra safety, slide it under the car as a crash cushion in case the jack fails

Why tires stick to the hub: over time, the hub center and the jant (wheel rim) develop light rust / corrosion that glues them together. A brand new tire comes off easily. A tire that's been on for 5 years may need a hard pull. If it won't come off, try rocking it left-right to break the corrosion bond, or tap the tire with a rubber mallet.

ADIM 7 — Yedek Lastiği Takın (Install the Spare Tire)

7. Adım — Yedek lastiği takın. Önce yedek lastiği, sonra da vidaları yerlerine yerleştirin. Vidaları, üzerlerinden bir kaç tur geçerek sırayla çevirin.

Step 7 — Install the spare tire. First put the spare on, then put the bolts back in. Turn the bolts in a few turns each, in sequence.

Details

  1. Lift the spare tire and align its holes with the hub studs
  2. Push the spare onto the hub — it should slide flat against the hub face
  3. Start the bolts by hand — finger-tighten each bolt a few turns until they all catch
  4. Tighten each bolt 1–2 turns in sequence — go around in a star / cross pattern (not in a circle) so you pull the wheel evenly onto the hub

Star pattern with 4 bolts: 1 → 3 → 2 → 4 (opposites first)
Star pattern with 5 bolts: 1 → 3 → 5 → 2 → 4 (skip every other)

Why the star pattern: if you tighten bolts in a circle, the wheel gets pulled crooked onto the hub (the first bolt pulls its edge flat, the next bolt fights that, etc.). Star pattern keeps the wheel aligned with the hub face.

Don't fully tighten yet — just snug. Full tightening comes after lowering the car in step 8.

ADIM 8 — Krikoyu İndirin (Lower the Jack + Final Tightening)

8. Adım — Krikoyu indirin. Krikoyu indirip yerinden çıkarın ve vidaları son bir kez kuvvetlice sıkın.

Step 8 — Lower the jack. Lower the jack and remove it, then tighten the bolts one last time, firmly.

Details

  1. Slowly turn the jack handle in the reverse direction to lower the car
  2. Stop when the spare tire just touches the ground — at this point, the tire has friction again but the car is mostly still on the jack
  3. Tighten each bolt fully in star pattern — now you can use the bijon anahtarı + your leg if needed (see step 4 technique). The tire-ground friction holds the wheel still while you apply torque.
  4. Fully lower the car onto all 4 wheels
  5. Remove the jack and store it in the trunk
  6. One more round of tightening — once the car is fully on the ground, do a final star-pattern torque-down to make sure everything is at maximum tightness
  7. Check each bolt by pushing on the bijon anahtarı — if none of them move, they're tight enough

Why this 2-phase tightening: partially-lowered → initial star tighten → fully lowered → final star tighten. This is the professional sequence that ensures the wheel is flat against the hub at all bolt-load levels.

ADIM 9 — Malzemeleri Toplayın (Pack Up the Tools)

9. Adım — Malzemeleri toplayın. Tüm malzemeleri düzenli bir şekilde yerlerine kaldırın.

Step 9 — Pack up the tools. Put all the materials back in their places neatly.

Details

  1. Kriko goes back in the spare wheel well
  2. Bijon anahtarı goes back alongside the kriko
  3. Flat tire goes into the spare wheel well (replacing the spare, which is now on the car)
  4. Close up the trunk — trunk floor carpet back in place, any wing nuts re-tightened
  5. Walk back to the reflektör (30 m city / 100 m rural) and pick it up — don't leave it on the road
  6. Fold it up and put it back in the trunk
  7. Turn off the dörtlü (hazard flashers)
  8. Do one final walk-around — make sure nothing was left on the ground, under the car, or on the hood

Why this step matters: if you leave your kriko or bijon anahtarı on the road shoulder, they're gone forever. And if you don't pick up the reflektör, it will fly into a car's windshield on the next gust of wind. Leave the road as you found it.

ADIM 10 — Patlak Lastiği Tamir Ettirin (Get the Flat Repaired)

10. Adım — Patlak lastiği tamir ettirin. Lastiğinizin her an tekrar patlayabileceğini düşünerek, patlak lastiği bir an önce tamir ettirin. Daha sonra, lastiklerin yerini tekrar değiştirin.

Step 10 — Get the flat tire repaired. Remembering that your spare could also flat at any time, get the punctured tire repaired as soon as possible. Afterward, put the tires back in their original positions.

Why this is critical

After installing the spare, you are driving without a backup. If you get a second flat — and it does happen — you'll be stranded with no options. Rule: repair or replace the flat tire within 1–2 days.

The "stepne" (donut spare) issue

If your spare is a stepne (space-saver / donut spare) — the smaller temporary tire:
- Max 80 km/h speed limit — do not exceed
- Max 80 km total distance — go straight to a tire shop
- Different diameter than your regular tires → affects handling, ABS, traction control
- Don't drive it as a permanent tire

Restoring the normal tire positions

Daha sonra, lastiklerin yerini tekrar değiştirin.
Afterward, put the tires back in their original positions.

When you get the flat fixed at the tire shop (lastik tamircisi), they'll patch the hole or replace the tire. Then you should:
1. Remove the spare from the wheel position it's currently on
2. Re-install the repaired tire in its original position
3. Put the spare back in the trunk

This keeps the wheel balance and tire rotation consistent across the car.

Bonus: have the shop check the other 3 tires while you're there — if one went flat, the others might be low or showing wear.

Addition 4 — Hoca's honest confession about lug bolt reality

Addition 5 — Factory lug bolts are extremely tight — a man standing on the wrench may not be enough

"Keşke küçüğünü tuttuğu gibi rahat bu vidalar sökülebilse. Adam üzerine çıkmış pakuz [bijon anahtarı] sökmeye çalışıyor, sökemiyor. Çünkü çok sert bu vidalar. Makinalar sıktırıyor çünkü bunu."

Plain English: Factory lug bolts are tightened by pneumatic torque guns (makinalar) at the tire shop to extremely high torque values. Sometimes even a grown man standing on the bijon anahtarı with his full body weight cannot loosen them.

Implications for roadside tire changes:

  1. Don't over-tighten your own lug bolts when re-installing — hand-tighten + foot-leverage is plenty. You don't need machine-gun torque.
  2. If you can't loosen the bolts on a factory-installed wheel, you may be stuck on the side of the road. This is a real scenario — call for roadside assistance or a tow.
  3. Test your own car at home — try loosening a wheel bolt in your driveway to verify you CAN do it before you need to do it for real.
  4. Some cars have "locking" lug bolts with a special key pattern — you need the matching key (usually in the glove box or trunk tool kit). If you don't have the key, you can't get the wheel off.

The practical advice: know your car's tools, practice at home, and if the bolts are impossibly tight, don't hurt yourself — call a tow truck. Your safety is worth more than the cost of roadside assistance.

Hoca: "Senin araban arka çekişli olamaz ki, öyle bir şey yok. Üretilen bütün arabaların hepsinde çekiş gücü önden. Sadece dört çeker arabalar var. Onlar da dört çarpı dört. Arkada tekerin olduğu bir araba yok. Öyle üretilmiş bir araba markası yok. Ürettiğinde, hepsinde ön tekerleklerde çekiş gücü var."

Hoca's position:
- All production cars are FWD (front-wheel drive)
- 4WD/AWD cars exist (4×4) but they drive all 4 wheels
- No RWD cars are being made

Reality check: this is not strictly true. Rear-wheel drive is common on:
- Luxury sedans (BMW 5-series, 7-series, Mercedes E-class, S-class)
- Sports cars (Porsche 911, Toyota Supra, Mazda MX-5)
- Pickup trucks (Ford Ranger, Toyota Hilux)
- Commercial vans (some)
- Performance cars in general

BUT for the Turkish B-class exam and common Turkish city cars, hoca's framing is exam-correct:
- Typical Turkish city cars (Renault Clio, Fiat Egea, Opel Corsa, Hyundai i20, Tofaş Şahin/Doğan, Ford Fiesta, etc.) are almost all FWD
- The exam question "kar zinciri hangi tekerleklere takılır?" has one right answer: ön tekerlekler (because the exam assumes a modern FWD city car)
- If the exam doesn't specify the drive type, assume FWD and answer "ön tekerlekler"

For your own safety in real life: check your car's manual to know if it's FWD, RWD, or 4WD. The answer depends on the car.

Exam rule (ignoring reality): always answer ön tekerlekler for snow chain placement unless the question explicitly says "arka çekişli araç" or "4×4 araç".

4G. SLIDE (2026-04-14) — LASTİĞİN ÖNEMİ (The Importance of Tires) — Recap Emphasis Slide

What the slide showed

A full-slide text block in blue letters with a cartoon of a man shouting into a megaphone (💡 representing "attention!") and a red-yellow triangular warning sign with "!" (uyarı). The text emphasizes that tires are the only physical link between the car and the road, and therefore the single most important component for vehicle control.

Verbatim Turkish → English

Lastikler otomobilin tüm teknik özelliklerini yere aktaran önemli parçalardır. Otomobiller fizik kurallarına göre hareket eder ve durur. Otomobillerin bu hareketleri sırasında yerle temasını sağlayan tek unsur lastiklerdir. Otomobilin kontrol edilebilmesinde lastiğin önemi göz ardı edilemeyecek kadar büyüktür.

Tires are the critical parts that transfer all of a car's technical characteristics to the ground. Cars move and stop according to the laws of physics. During these movements, the ONLY element that connects the car to the ground is the tires. The importance of tires in being able to control the car is too great to be ignored.

Clean English explanation — why hoca showed this slide

This slide is a closing emphasis on the lastik chapter. It's not adding new information — it's driving home the central concept that lies behind everything taught in this chapter: the tire is the ONLY thing touching the road.

Think about what that really means:

  • Engine power → produced by the motor, routed through the drivetrain (vites kutusu → şaft → diferansiyel → aks) → delivered to the tire contact patch → pushes the car forward. If the tire fails, the power goes nowhere.
  • Braking force → generated by the fren sistemi (balata pressing on disc/drum) → transmitted to the wheel → delivered to the tire contact patch → slows the car. If the tire fails, the brakes can't stop the car.
  • Steering force → generated by the direksiyon + ön düzen → routed through the rot başları → delivered to the front wheel hubs → delivered to the tire contact patch → changes the car's direction. If the tire fails, the car can't be steered.
  • Cornering grip → determined entirely by the tire's grip against the road. If the tire fails, the car slides off the road in a curve.

Every single control input from the driver has to pass through the tire contact patch. There is nothing else. The entire car balances on 4 small oval areas (each about the size of a postcard) where rubber meets asphalt.

Why "fizik kurallarına göre"

Otomobiller fizik kurallarına göre hareket eder ve durur.

Plain English: cars don't move because of "will" or "design" — they move because of physical forces, and those forces are constrained by the laws of physics. The key laws at play:

Physics principle How it affects driving
Newton's 1st law (inertia) A moving car wants to keep moving — brakes have to generate enough force to stop it
Newton's 2nd law (F = ma) The deceleration depends on the brake force divided by the car's mass — heavier cars need more braking force
Friction (μ × N) All driving forces are limited by the friction coefficient (μ) between tire and road multiplied by the normal force (weight on the tire)
Kinetic energy (½mv²) Doubling speed quadruples the energy that needs to be dissipated to stop — this is why high speeds have dramatically longer stopping distances

The tire's job: provide as much friction (grip) as possible so the driver can push the physical limits of the car without exceeding them. A good tire gives you more friction = more grip = more control. A worn / under-inflated / wrong tire gives you less friction = less grip = less control = crash risk.

The "göz ardı edilemeyecek" (cannot be ignored) phrasing

The slide ends with "göz ardı edilemeyecek kadar büyüktür" — literally "[its importance] is too great to be overlooked". This is emphatic Turkish for "you absolutely must take tires seriously".

What this looks like in practice:

  • Check tire pressure every week, or at least every fill-up
  • Inspect tires visually every day (part of günlük kontroller)
  • Replace tires when the tread depth approaches the legal minimum (1.6 mm) — don't wait for a blowout
  • Match tires to the season — kış lastiği in winter, yaz lastiği in summer
  • Rotate tires periodically for even wear
  • Balance + align (rot + balans ayarı) to prevent uneven wear
  • Replace in pairs at minimum — never mix brand new tires with worn ones on the same axle

All of these care rules trace back to this single slide's point: the tire is the single most critical safety component on the car, and neglecting it is effectively giving up on controlling the car.

The cross-system tie-in

This slide also connects to:

  • 11-süspansiyon-sistemi.md — the süspansiyon's job is to keep the tire in contact with the road during bumps. Worn amortisörs = tire bounces off the road = no grip.
  • 09-fren-sistemi.md — brakes can only slow the wheel as fast as the tire-road friction allows. Worn tires = ABS kicks in earlier and stopping distance grows.
  • 10-direksiyon-ön-düzen-sistemi.md — steering works via tire-road friction. No grip → no steering.
  • 08-güç-aktarma-organları.md — all engine power has to pass through the tire contact patch. Slippery road → engine power just spins the drive wheels without moving the car.

Every system feeds into the tire. The tire is the funnel at the end of every control chain.

Critical exam fact

Lastik, otomobilin yerle temasını sağlayan tek unsurdur. Otomobilin kontrolü tamamen lastiklere bağlıdır.
The tire is the ONLY element that connects the car to the ground. The control of the car is entirely dependent on the tires.
