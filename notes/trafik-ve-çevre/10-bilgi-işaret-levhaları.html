Bilgi İşaret Levhaları — Informational Signs + Otoyol + Duraklama/Park + Yol Numaraları

Last updated: 2026-04-15
Slides covered: 7 slides from 2026-04-15 class — settlement (meskun mahal) signs, geographic signs, distance signs, facility signs (hospital, pedestrian zone, yaya geçidi), priority road signs, emergency phone numbers, otoyol signs, parking/stopping signs, and road numbering system

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

Bilgi işaret levhaları are the third main category of trafik işaret levhaları (after tehlike uyarı and trafik tanzim). They do not warn or command — they inform. They tell you where you are, what's nearby, and what services are available.

Universal visual signature

Property Rule
Shape Dikdörtgen (rectangle) or kare (square)
Colour Varies by sub-type — mavi (blue) for most info, yeşil (green) for otoyol/distance, kahverengi (brown) for geographic/touristic
Meaning Information — no obligation, no prohibition, just guidance

B-10c Meskun Mahal Sonu Levhası (Köy/Belde) End of settlement — village Red line through "SARIYER"

Critical exam fact

Meskun mahal = yerleşim yeri. When you pass a meskun mahal sign, speed limits change (typically 50 km/h inside built-up areas vs 90 km/h outside). When you pass the sonu (end) sign (the one with the red line), the higher speed limit resumes.

The exam asks: "Kırmızı çizgiyle üstü çizilmiş mavi yerleşim yeri işareti ne anlama gelir?" → Meskun mahalin sona erdiğini bildirir — you are now outside the settlement.

Hoca's live clarification (2026-04-15) — nüfus/rakım no longer shown:
"Buna nüfus ve rakım yazılmıyor. Sadece il merkezinin ismi yazıyor."
Current-generation meskun mahal signs only show the name of the settlement — population and elevation are no longer printed. The slide showed old-format signs with nüfus/rakım for teaching, but the real road signs have been simplified.

Speed limits by road type (Türkiye'de geçerli hız sınırları):

Road type Limit
Yerleşim yeri içi (built-up) 50 km/h
İki yönlü yol (two-lane) 90 km/h
Bölünmüş yol (divided) 110 km/h
Otoyol (motorway) 120 km/h

Hoca noted that some otoyol sections have been raised to 130 km/h in certain locations.

Critical exam nuance

  • Green signs = otoyol / mesafe. Blue signs = general bilgi. Brown signs = coğrafi/turizm. The colour coding is tested: "Yeşil levhalar ne tür bilgi verir?" → Otoyol ve mesafe bilgisi.

5. SLIDE 4 — Priority Road · Emergency Services · Institutions

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
B-17 Öncelikli Olan Yön Priority direction Arrow sign showing which direction has right of way at a narrow passage
B-30 Anayol Priority road (main road) Yellow diamond — you are on the priority road; side roads yield to you
B-30s Anayol Sonu End of priority road Yellow diamond with black bar — your priority ends
B-46 Jandarma (156) Gendarmerie — phone 156 Emergency contact for rural areas
B-41 Polis Police station Blue sign with "Polis"
B-42 Yangın Tehlikesi (177) Fire danger — phone 177 Fire emergency phone
B-23 112 Acil Yardım 112 Emergency The universal emergency number in Turkey
B-44 Kaymakamlık / Türk Darphane Government office / institution General institutional sign

Critical exam facts

B-30 (yellow diamond) = ANAYOL. This is one of the most-tested signs. When you are on the anayol, vehicles on tali yollar (side roads) must yield to you. When you see B-30s (diamond with bar), your anayol priority ends — at the next intersection, normal rules apply (sağdan gelen = right-of-way).

Yellow diamond shape = priority. No other sign uses this shape/colour combo. If the exam shows a yellow diamond, the answer involves "anayol" or "öncelikli yol."

Emergency numbers to memorize:
- 112 — Acil yardım (universal emergency)
- 155 — Polis
- 156 — Jandarma (rural/military police)
- 177 — Orman yangını (forest fire)
- 110 — İtfaiye (fire department)

Hoca's live addition (2026-04-15) — say "bir-bir-iki" not "yüz on iki":
"Eskiden yüz on iki diyorduk ama bunu değiştirmişler, bir-bir-iki demek gerekiyor. Neden? Çünkü küçük çocuklar ona kadar saymayı biliyor ama yüz on ikiyi diyemez. Onun için bir-bir-iki demek gerekiyor. Çocuklara da öyle öğretmek gerekiyor."

The official way to say the emergency number is now "bir-bir-iki" (1-1-2), not "yüz on iki" (112). Small children can say single digits but not "one hundred and twelve." Teach it as three separate digits.

Critical exam fact — Kaçış Rampası

Kaçış rampası (escape ramp): a long uphill gravel strip built on steep descents for vehicles whose brakes have failed (fren arızası). The slide defines it:

"Fren arızası nedeni ile oluşabilecek can ve mal kayıplı kazaların önüne geçmek için yapılmıştır, fren kontrolünü yitirmiş ağır araçların trafik koridorundan ayrılarak izole edilmesi ve aracın kontrollü bir şekilde durdurulmasını temin eden yapılardır."

(Built to prevent fatal/costly accidents caused by brake failure; it isolates runaway heavy vehicles from the traffic corridor and brings them to a controlled stop.)

These are posted on mountain descents (tehlikeli eğim / iniş). The signs come in a sequence: 500 m → ramp approach → ramp itself.

7. SLIDE 6 — Duraklama ve Park Etme İşaret Levhaları

The 7 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
P-1 Park Etmek Yasaktır No parking Red-bordered blue circle with single red diagonal slash over P
P-2 Duraklama ve Park Etme Yasaktır No stopping or parking Double red diagonal slash over blue circle — both stopping AND parking forbidden
P-3a Park Yeri Parking area White P on blue square — parking is available
P-7a Kapalı Park Yeri Covered / indoor parking P with a roof icon
P-7b Park Yeri + Metro Park and ride (Metro) P + Metro icon — park here and take metro
P-7c Park Yeri (belveder/yabancinlar için) Parking for specific users P with a sub-panel indicating who may park

Critical exam nuances

  • P-1 (single slash) = park yasak, duraklama serbest. You CAN stop briefly (duraklama — under 5 minutes for loading/unloading), but you CANNOT park (leave your vehicle).
  • P-2 (double slash) = duraklama ve park BOTH yasak. You cannot stop at all — not even briefly.

This is the classic durma / duraklama / park etme three-way trap from 01-temel-kavramlar.md:
- Durma = forced stop (traffic light, police) — always allowed
- Duraklama = voluntary brief stop (≤5 min, loading/unloading, dropping off passengers)
- Park etme = leaving the vehicle standing

P-1 bans park but allows duraklama. P-2 bans both.

8. SLIDE 7 — Otoyol İşaretleri

The 6 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
OT-1 Otoyol Başlangıcı ve Yasakları Motorway start + prohibitions Green sign showing motorway icon + list of banned vehicles/actions
OT-2 Otoyol Belgesi Motorway symbol Green oval with motorway icon
OT-3 Otoyol Hız Sınırları (40/120) Motorway speed limits Asgari 40 — Azami 120 km/h
OT-4 Otoyol Yaklaşım Levhası Motorway approach sign Green distance sign showing motorway ahead
OT-5 Otoyol Sonu Motorway end Green sign with red bar — motorway rules no longer apply
OT-6 Otoyol Çıkış Levhası Motorway exit sign Green sign showing exit name, destinations, distances

Critical exam facts

Otoyol speed limits:
- Azami (max): 120 km/h (for otomobil — trucks/buses may have lower limits)
- Asgari (min): 40 km/h — if your vehicle cannot sustain 40 km/h, you cannot use the otoyol

Vehicles BANNED from otoyol: pedestrians, bicycles, mopeds, tractors, animal-drawn carts, any vehicle that cannot reach 40 km/h. The exam loves: "Otoyolda en düşük hız sınırı kaçtır?" → 40 km/h.

Otoyol sign colour = yeşil (green). Regular bilgi signs = mavi (blue). If you see a green sign on the exam, it's either an otoyol sign or a distance sign.

The 5 number types

Code Format Colour Turkish English Example
O.1 Green Otoyol Numarası Motorway number O.1 = İstanbul–Ankara otoyolu
E.90 Green Uluslararası Yol Numarası International road number E.90 = European route
D.400 Blue Devlet Yolu Numarası State road number D.400 = a national highway
06.80 Orange İl Yolu Numarası Provincial road number 06 = Ankara il kodu
K.16 White Kavşak Numarası Junction number K.16 = junction ID

Exam question on maps

On a map, shapes encode road type:
- A = Otoyol (oval shape)
- B = Demir yolu (railway — dashed line)
- C = Devlet yolu (shield shape)
- D = Uluslararası yol (rectangular)

"Haritalarda gösterilen şekillerden D100 hangisine aittir?" — check the shape.

10. Why this matters for the exam

  1. Meskun mahal = speed limit change: entering → 50 km/h; exiting (red line through name) → 90 km/h (two-lane) / 110 km/h (divided) / 120 km/h (otoyol).
  2. Anayol (yellow diamond): tali yoldan gelenler yol verir.
  3. Otoyol asgari hız: 40 km/h minimum — anything below that cannot enter.
  4. Duraklama vs park (P-1 vs P-2): single slash = park yasak only; double slash = both yasak.
  5. Kaçış rampası = fren arızası için.
  6. Emergency numbers: 112, 155, 156, 177, 110.
  7. Green sign = otoyol/mesafe. Blue = general bilgi. Brown = coğrafi/turizm.

11. Memorize These

Meskun mahal levhası = yerleşim yeri başlıyor → hız sınırı düşer (50 km/h).
Meskun mahal sonu (kırmızı çizgili) = yerleşim yeri bitiyor → hız sınırı yükselir.

Anayol = sarı baklava (diamond). Tali yoldan gelenler sana yol verir.
Anayol sonu = sarı baklava + siyah çizgi. Önceliğin bitti, normal kurallar geçerli.

Otoyol: asgari 40, azami 120. 40 altı araçlar otoyola giremez.

P-1 (tek çizgi) = park yasak, duraklama serbest.
P-2 (çift çizgi) = duraklama ve park ikisi de yasak.

Kaçış rampası = fren arızasında ağır araçlar için acil çıkış (dağ inişlerinde).

Acil numaralar: 112 (genel), 155 (polis), 156 (jandarma), 177 (orman yangını), 110 (itfaiye).
