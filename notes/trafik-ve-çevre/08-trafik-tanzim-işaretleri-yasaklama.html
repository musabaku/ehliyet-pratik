Trafik Tanzim İşaretleri — Yasaklama & Kısıtlama (Regulation & Prohibition Signs)

Last updated: 2026-04-15
Slides covered: 4 slides from 2026-04-15 class — the full Turkish catalog of "trafik tanzim" signs (prohibitions, limits, mandatory movements, and end-of-restriction markers), codes TT-1 through TT-34
Hierarchy rank: 3 (Trafik işaret levhaları) — overrides road markings, loses to traffic lights and to a live officer. See 05-trafik-işaretleri-öncelik.md.

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

Trafik tanzim işaretleri — literally "traffic-regulation signs" — are the command category of sign boards. The banner above every slide in this group puts it perfectly:

"Çeşitli yasaklama ve kısıtlamalar hakkında bilgi veren işaretlerdir."
(Signs that inform about various prohibitions and restrictions.)

Unlike tehlike uyarı signs (which only warn you about a hazard), tanzim signs order you to do — or not do — something. Disobeying them is a direct KTK violation. They are the rulebook of the road, made visible.

The universal visual signature

Tanzim signs come in three distinct shapes, each with its own meaning:

Shape Meaning Example
Ters üçgen (inverted triangle) — red border, white interior Yield / "Yol Ver" TT-1 YOL VER
Sekizgen (octagon) — solid red Stop / "Dur" — the only sign that can never be confused with another TT-2 DUR
Daire (circle) — red border around white interior with black symbol Prohibition / limit / restriction end Almost all other TT signs

Why the two exceptions matter: DUR and YOL VER are intentionally given unique shapes so that drivers can still recognize them from the back or in low visibility (snow, fog, bad lighting) — you don't need to read them to know what they mean, the silhouette is enough. This is a classic exam question: "Hangi iki işaretin arkadan bakıldığında bile anlamı anlaşılır?" → DUR (sekizgen) ve YOL VER (ters üçgen).

Red circle vs red circle with slash

  • Plain red-bordered circle with a symbol inside = this vehicle / action is forbidden.
  • Red-bordered circle with a diagonal slash over the symbol = same meaning, but the slash is used when the symbol is already a composite (e.g. "no U-turn," "no left turn").
  • Black diagonal slash over a white circle = end of restriction.

Action when you see any tanzim sign

  1. Read the pictogram (what's restricted / ordered).
  2. Read any numbers (a weight, width, height, length, or speed value).
  3. Comply immediately — tanzim signs take effect from the moment you pass them and remain in force until an "end of restriction" sign (TT-32, TT-33, or TT-34).

2. SLIDE 1 — The Foundational 8 (Yol Ver, Dur, Entry Prohibitions)

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
TT-1 Yol Ver Yield Inverted red triangle. Slow down and yield to vehicles on the priority road. Do not stop unless necessary.
TT-2 Dur Stop Red octagon. Mandatory full stop — wheels must come to rest — then proceed only when the way is clear.
TT-3 Karşıdan Gelene Yol Ver Yield to oncoming traffic A narrow section ahead — oncoming traffic has priority, you must wait
TT-4 Girişi Olmayan Yol No entry Solid red circle with white horizontal bar — you may not enter this road at all
TT-5 Taşıt Trafiğine Kapalı Yol Road closed to all vehicles Plain red-bordered empty circle — no motor vehicles AND no bicycles, horse carts, etc. — the road is completely closed to all traffic
TT-6 Motosiklet Hariç Motorlu Taşıt Trafiğine Kapalı Yol Closed to motor vehicles except motorcycles Car icon — motorcycles are still allowed
TT-7 Motosiklet Giremez Motorcycles may not enter Motorcycle icon — motorcycles specifically prohibited
TT-8 Bisiklet Giremez Bicycles may not enter Bicycle icon — bicycles specifically prohibited

Critical exam nuances

  • DUR vs YOL VER — the key verb difference:
  • DUR → mutlak duruş (full stop mandatory, even if the road is clear)
  • YOL VER → yavaşla, hakkı olana geç, gerekirse dur (slow down, yield, stop only if needed)

The exam will test this directly: "DUR ve YOL VER arasındaki fark nedir?" — DUR always requires a full stop; YOL VER only requires stopping if a higher-priority vehicle is approaching.

  • TT-4 (no entry) vs TT-5 (road closed): TT-4 is a one-way violation sign — you are about to enter a street from the wrong end. TT-5 means the road itself is completely closed — even to pedestrians in some contexts, and to all vehicles regardless of type.

  • TT-6's "hariç" trick: the word "hariç" = "except". TT-6 means "closed to motor vehicles except motorcycles" — so motorcycles are the one type of motor vehicle that may enter. This reverses what students expect. A typical exam trap: "Aşağıdaki işaretin bulunduğu yola hangi araç girebilir?" → Motosiklet.

  • Flashing red traffic light = TT-2 DUR — see 06-ışıklı-trafik-işaretleri.md. The exam loves to bridge these two topics.

  • Flashing yellow traffic light = TT-1 YOL VER — same source.

3. SLIDE 2 — Dangerous Cargo + Dimension Limits (TT-16 → TT-22)

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
TT-16a Belirli Miktarlardan Fazla Patlayıcı ve Parlayıcı Madde Taşıyan Taşıt Giremez No vehicles carrying excessive explosive / flammable material Blast/fire icon — fuel tankers, fireworks, gas over a threshold may not enter
TT-16b Tehlikeli Madde Taşıyan Taşıt Giremez No dangerous-goods vehicles Orange placard icon — ADR-class hazmat may not enter
TT-17 Belirli Miktarlardan Fazla Su Kirletici Madde Taşıyan Taşıt Giremez No vehicles carrying excessive water-polluting material Typically posted near water reservoirs, dam catchment areas
TT-18 Motorlu Taşıt Giremez No motor vehicles Car+motorcycle icon — no engine-powered vehicles at all; bicycles and horse carts may still enter
TT-19 Taşıt Giremez No vehicles at all Car+motorcycle+animal-cart icon — nothing on wheels may enter
TT-20 Genişliği ... Metreden Fazla Olan Taşıt Giremez No vehicles wider than X metres 2.30 m on the slide — a width limit
TT-21 Yüksekliği ... Metreden Fazla Olan Taşıt Giremez No vehicles taller than X metres 3.50 m on the slide — a height limit, typically posted before bridges/tunnels
TT-22 Uzunluğu ... Metreden Fazla Olan Taşıt Giremez No vehicles longer than X metres 10 m on the slide — a length limit

Critical exam nuances

  • TT-18 vs TT-19 — "motorlu taşıt" vs "taşıt":
  • TT-18 (motorlu taşıt giremez) = engine-powered vehicles forbidden; non-motor vehicles (bicycles, horse carts, handcarts) are still allowed.
  • TT-19 (taşıt giremez) = all wheeled vehicles forbidden, motor or not.

This ties directly to 03-araç-tanımları.md — "taşıt" vs "motorlu taşıt" is a classic two-term distinction the exam exploits.

  • TT-20/21/22 = the "gabari" family: these signs enforce the gabari rules from 04-gabari-ve-ağırlık.md — genişlik (width), yükseklik (height), uzunluk (length). Canonical exam numbers:
  • 2 m / 3 m — typical width limits on narrow urban streets
  • 3.50 m / 4.5 m — typical height limits at bridges and tunnels

  • 10 m — typical length limit on winding rural roads

  • TT-16a vs TT-16b: TT-16a is specifically about patlayıcı/parlayıcı (explosive/flammable); TT-16b is the broader tehlikeli madde (dangerous goods) category. In the real exam, both may appear as "no hazmat vehicles" with slightly different answer choices — read the icon carefully.

  • TT-17 (water-polluting material) is rare in practice but it's a favourite exam trap because students don't recognize the icon (a tanker with water droplets underneath).

4. SLIDE 3 — Weight Limits + Turn / Overtake Prohibitions (TT-23 → TT-28)

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
TT-23 Dingil Başına ... Tondan Fazla Yük Düşen Taşıt Giremez No vehicles with per-axle load exceeding X tons 6 t on the slide — per-axle weight limit (protects bridges/pavement)
TT-24 Yüklü Ağırlığı ... Tondan Fazla Olan Taşıt Giremez No vehicles with total laden weight exceeding X tons 7 t on the slide — total gross weight limit
TT-25 Öndeki Taşıt ... Metreden Daha Yakın Takip Edilemez Minimum following distance — do not follow closer than X metres 70 m on the slide — minimum takip mesafesi (following distance)
TT-26a Sağa Dönülmez No right turn Right-turn arrow with diagonal slash
TT-26b Sola Dönülmez No left turn Left-turn arrow with diagonal slash
TT-26c U Dönüşü Yapılmaz No U-turn U-arrow with diagonal slash
TT-27 Öndeki Taşıtı Geçmek Yasaktır No overtaking Two-car icon with one car in red
TT-28 Kamyonlar İçin Öndeki Taşıtı Geçmek Yasaktır No overtaking for trucks Truck + car icon — only trucks are forbidden from overtaking; cars may still overtake

Critical exam nuances

  • TT-23 (dingil başına) vs TT-24 (yüklü ağırlık) — the key distinction:
  • Dingil başına yük (axle load) is the weight pressing on each axle — a 14-ton truck with 2 axles typically puts 7 tons on each axle. This matters for bridge deck loading and pavement damage.
  • Yüklü ağırlık (laden weight) is the total weight of the vehicle plus its load. This matters for bridge capacity.

The same truck might be allowed past TT-24 (7 t total) but forbidden past TT-23 (6 t/axle) — or vice versa. Know the difference cold. See 04-gabari-ve-ağırlık.md for the full terminology.

  • TT-25 (minimum following distance) is different from the general speed-based 2-second rule. This sign enforces a hard metric (70 m in the slide). It's typically posted on tunnels, bridges, and mountain roads where sudden braking by the car in front could cascade into pile-ups.

  • TT-26 family — no-turn signs:

  • The slash direction visually matches the forbidden direction.

  • U dönüşü yasağı (TT-26c) is the single most-tested of the three — students often miss that you cannot make a U-turn where there is no explicit sign forbidding it if there are other rules in force (e.g., on divided highways, at traffic lights in certain configurations).

  • TT-27 vs TT-28 — "who is forbidden":

  • TT-27: no vehicle may overtake. Everyone.
  • TT-28: only trucks may not overtake. A regular car can still overtake if safe.

Classic trap: "TT-28 işareti hangi araçlar için geçerlidir?" → Kamyonlar için. Regular cars can still overtake.

5. SLIDE 4 — Speed / Horn / Customs / End-of-Restriction (TT-29 → TT-34)

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
TT-29 Azami Hız Sınırlaması Maximum speed limit 50 on the slide — maximum speed (km/h) from this point forward
TT-29b Okul Bölgesi Azami Hız Sınırı School-zone maximum speed 30 on the slide — rectangular sign specifically for school zones
TT-30 Sesli İkaz Cihazlarının Kullanımı Yasaktır No horn use Horn/trumpet icon with slash — typically near hospitals, schools, residential areas at night
TT-31 Gümrük (Durmadan Geçmek Yasaktır) Customs — mandatory stop Red border, "GÜMRÜK / DOUANE" text — full stop required at border checkpoints
TT-32 Bütün Yasaklama ve Kısıtlamaların Sonu End of all prohibitions and restrictions Diagonal black bar over white circle — all restrictions set by earlier tanzim signs are now lifted
TT-33 Hız Sınırlaması Sonu End of speed limit Same slashed-circle style, with the previous speed limit shown struck through
TT-34a Geçme Yasağı Sonu End of no-overtaking restriction Slashed-circle with the two-car icon
TT-34b Kamyonlar İçin Geçme Yasağı Sonu End of no-overtaking for trucks Slashed-circle with the truck+car icon

Critical exam nuances

  • Azami vs asgari hız: azami = maximum, asgari = minimum. TT-29 is a maximum speed limit — going faster is the violation. There is a separate sign (not on this slide) for minimum speed.

  • TT-29b okul bölgesi: school zones have a dedicated rectangular sign (different shape from the standard circular TT-29) because school-zone limits are enforced more strictly and students must recognize them faster. The combined "OKUL BÖLGESİ" text + "30" value is a classic exam trap.

  • TT-30 (no horn) matters for the practical exam (direksiyon sınavı): if you honk near a hospital sign during the test, you fail. In real life, the fine is small, but the exam is unforgiving. Exam prompt: "Sesli ikaz cihazlarının kullanımının yasak olduğu yerlerde ne yapmalısınız?" → Çalmamalısınız.

  • TT-31 gümrük = mandatory stop. Even if the road looks clear, even if there's no officer in sight, you must stop at a gümrük sign. This is a two-for-one question on the exam: it asks both "bu işaretin anlamı nedir?" and "durmadan geçerseniz ne olur?" → yasaktır / ceza.

  • TT-32 is the universal "end of everything" sign — when you see it, every earlier tanzim restriction is canceled. TT-33 and TT-34 are specific end-of-restriction signs (only for speed, only for overtaking). TT-32 beats both — it ends all earlier restrictions at once.

  • Visual cue for "end of" signs: a diagonal bar across a white or light circle, often with the previously-restricted symbol shown grey/crossed out. If you see a circle with a diagonal slash, it is removing a prior rule, not creating a new one.

6. Why this matters for the exam

  1. DUR vs YOL VER — shape cue: "Hangi işaretler arkadan bakıldığında dahi anlamlıdır?" → DUR (sekizgen) ve YOL VER (ters üçgen).
  2. DUR action: "DUR işaretinde ne yapmalıyız?" → Mutlak duruş yapmalıyız — full stop required, even if the road is clear.
  3. YOL VER action: "YOL VER işaretinde ne yapmalıyız?" → Yavaşlayıp geçiş hakkını olana vermeliyiz — slow down and yield, stop only if necessary.
  4. Hariç trap (TT-6): "Motosiklet hariç motorlu taşıt trafiğine kapalı yol işaretinde hangi araç girebilir?" → Motosiklet.
  5. Motorlu taşıt vs taşıt (TT-18 vs TT-19): TT-18 lets bicycles in; TT-19 does not.
  6. Gabari limits (TT-20/21/22): know genişlik / yükseklik / uzunluk.
  7. Axle vs total weight (TT-23 vs TT-24): dingil başına yük = per-axle, yüklü ağırlık = total.
  8. U-turn and left/right prohibitions (TT-26): the slash direction matches the forbidden maneuver.
  9. Overtaking (TT-27 vs TT-28): TT-27 = nobody overtakes; TT-28 = trucks only.
  10. Speed limit (TT-29 + TT-29b): azami = maximum. School zones use a rectangular variant.
  11. Horn prohibition (TT-30): sesli ikaz cihazı = horn. Common near hospitals, schools.
  12. Customs mandatory stop (TT-31): "Gümrük işaretinde ne yapmalıyız?" → Durmadan geçmek yasaktır — mandatory stop.
  13. End-of-restriction signs (TT-32/33/34): TT-32 = all restrictions end; TT-33 = only speed ends; TT-34 = only overtaking ends.
  14. Red circle with slash = restriction / end of restriction; red solid circle = absolute prohibition.
  15. Hierarchy reminder: tanzim signs are rank 3 — they beat road markings but lose to traffic lights and police. See 05-trafik-işaretleri-öncelik.md.

7. Memorize These

DUR = sekizgen (octagon). YOL VER = ters üçgen (inverted triangle). These two shapes are unique so drivers can recognize them even from behind.

DUR işareti → mutlak duruş. Yol açık olsa bile tekerlekler tam duracak, sonra emniyetli olduğuna emin ol, sonra hareket et.
YOL VER işareti → yavaşla ve geçiş hakkı olana ver. Gerekirse dur, gerekmezse durmadan geç.

TT-6 "motosiklet hariç motorlu taşıt giremez" → motosikletler girebilir (hariç = except).
TT-18 "motorlu taşıt giremez" → bisiklet ve at arabası girebilir.
TT-19 "taşıt giremez" → hiçbir taşıt giremez.

Dingil başına yük = per-axle load. Yüklü ağırlık = total laden weight. İkisi farklı kısıtlamalardır.

TT-27 geçmek yasaktır → kimse geçemez.
TT-28 kamyonlar için geçmek yasaktır → sadece kamyonlar geçemez, otomobiller geçebilir.

Azami hız = maximum speed. Asgari hız = minimum speed.

TT-31 gümrük → durmadan geçmek yasaktır, mutlak duruş zorunludur.

Yasaklama sonu işaretleri:
- TT-32 = bütün yasaklamaların sonu (all restrictions end)
- TT-33 = sadece hız sınırlamasının sonu
- TT-34a/b = sadece geçme yasağının sonu (a = hepsine, b = sadece kamyonlara)

Daire (circle) + kırmızı kenar + siyah sembol = yasaklama işareti.
Daire + diyagonal kırmızı çizgi = aynı, ama vurgulu (no U-turn vb).
Daire + siyah diyagonal çizgi = yasaklamanın sonu.
