Trafik Tanzim İşaretleri — Emir (Mandatory / Blue Signs)

Last updated: 2026-04-15
Slides covered: 4 slides from 2026-04-15 class — blue mandatory direction signs, pedestrian/cyclist/horse signs, minimum speed, snow chains, shared paths
Hierarchy rank: 3 (Trafik işaret levhaları)

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

The emir (mandatory) signs are the second half of the Trafik Tanzim family. Where yasaklama signs (file 08) tell you what you must not do, emir signs tell you what you must do. Their universal visual signature:

Property Rule
Shape Daire (circle)
Colour Mavi zemin (blue background), beyaz sembol (white symbol)
Meaning Mecburi (mandatory) — you MUST follow the indicated direction / rule

Exam mnemonic: Kırmızı daire = yasak (no!). Mavi daire = mecburi (must!). Colour = intent.

2. SLIDE 1 — Mandatory Direction Signs (TT-35 → TT-40)

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
TT-35a Sola Mecburi Yön Mandatory left You must turn left
TT-35b İleri Mecburi Yön Mandatory straight ahead You must go straight
TT-35c İleri ve Sağa Mecburi Yön Mandatory straight or right You must go straight or right — no left turn
TT-35d İleri ve Sola Mecburi Yön Mandatory straight or left You must go straight or left — no right turn
TT-35f Sağa ve Sola Mecburi Yön Mandatory right or left You must turn right or left — no going straight
TT-36a İleriden Sağa Mecburi Yön Mandatory right ahead Must turn right at the next point
TT-38b İleriden Sola Mecburi Yön Mandatory left ahead Must turn left at the next point
TT-40a Kavşak Dönüş Yönü Roundabout direction Mandatory direction of circulation in a roundabout (counterclockwise in Turkey)

Critical exam nuances

  • Mandatory direction signs implicitly prohibit other directions. TT-35c (straight or right) = no left turn allowed. The exam tests this indirectly: "Bu işaretin bulunduğu kavşakta sola dönülebilir mi?" → Hayır.
  • TT-40a (roundabout direction) — Turkey drives on the right, so roundabouts go counterclockwise (saat yönünün tersine). The blue circular arrow confirms this. If the exam shows a roundabout sign, vehicles already inside have priority.

3. SLIDE 2 — Pedestrian, Cyclist, and Horse Signs

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
TT-35d Yaya ve Bisikletliler Tarafından Kullanılabilen Yol Path shared by pedestrians and cyclists Mixed use — pedestrians and cyclists share the same path
TT-36 Atlı/At Yarışçıları Mecburi Yön Horse riders — mandatory direction Horse riders must follow this path
TT-37 Bisiklet Mecburi Yol Mandatory bicycle path Cyclists must use this path — riding on the main road is forbidden
TT-38 Yaya Mecburi Yol Mandatory pedestrian path Pedestrians must use this path
TT-39 Motorlu Taşıt Trafiğine Kapalı — Bisiklet/Yaya Yolu Road closed to motors — ped/bike path Only bicycles and pedestrians allowed
TT-40 Mecburi Bisiklet Yolu Mandatory cycle lane Cyclists must ride here, not on the main carriageway
TT-41 Yaya Bölgesi Pedestrian zone Area designated only for pedestrians — no vehicles
TT-42 Yaya Bölgesi Sonu End of pedestrian zone Pedestrian-only zone ends — vehicles may re-enter

Critical exam nuance

  • Blue circle with both pedestrian + bicycle icon = shared path. Blue circle with a dividing line between pedestrian and bicycle icons = separate paths (each group uses their own side). This distinction is the exam's favourite test on this slide.

4. SLIDE 3 — Minimum Speed · Snow Chains · Hazmat Direction

The 5 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
TT-41a Mecburi Asgari Hız (30) Mandatory minimum speed You must drive at least 30 km/h — no crawling. Common on motorways
TT-41b Mecburi Asgari Hız Sonu (30) End of minimum speed The minimum-speed requirement is lifted
TT-42 Zorunlu Takılma Zincirleri Mandatory snow chains You must fit snow chains (kar zinciri) before continuing. Posted on mountain passes in winter
TT-42b Tehlikeli Madde Taşıyan Araçlar İçin Mecburi Yön Mandatory route for hazmat vehicles Hazmat vehicles must follow this direction
TT-45a,b,c Tehlikeli Madde Taşıyan Araçlar İçin Mecburi Yön (variants) Hazmat mandatory direction (straight/left/right) Specific directional variants for hazmat

Critical exam nuances

  • Azami hız (TT-29, red circle) vs asgari hız (TT-41a, blue circle):
  • Azami (red) = maximum — going faster is forbidden
  • Asgari (blue) = minimum — going slower is forbidden

The colour encodes the rule: red = restriction, blue = requirement. If a sign shows "30" in a red circle, the max speed is 30. If it shows "30" in a blue circle, the min speed is 30. The exam always tests this contrast.

  • Kar zinciri = çekici lastiklere takılır (snow chains go on the drive wheels). For modern front-wheel-drive (FWD) cars = front wheels. This links back to the Araç Tekniği module — see 08b-lastikler.md.

5. SLIDE 4 — Shared Path & End-of-Path Signs (TT-44)

The 4 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
TT-44a Yayalar ve Bisikletliler Tarafından Kullanılabilen Yol Shared pedestrian/cyclist path Mixed — both use the same space
TT-44b Yayalar ve Bisikletliler İçin Ayrı Kullanılabilen Yol Separate pedestrian/cyclist path Divided — each has their own side (usually marked by a dividing line on the sign)
TT-44c Yayalar ve Bisikletliler Tarafından Kullanılabilen Yol Sonu End of shared path
TT-44d Yayalar ve Bisikletliler İçin Ayrı Kullanılabilen Yol Sonu End of separate path

Critical exam fact

Shared vs separate — THE visual cue: if the pedestrian icon and bicycle icon are side by side with no line between them = shared. If there is a vertical line dividing them = separate paths. Exam: "Bu iki işaret arasındaki fark nedir?" → one is shared, the other separates pedestrians from cyclists.

6. Why this matters for the exam

  1. Red circle = yasak, blue circle = mecburi: "Mavi zeminli daire şeklindeki işaretler ne anlama gelir?" → Mecburi (mandatory) — uyulması zorunlu.
  2. Azami vs asgari hız: "Kırmızı daire içinde 50 yazıyorsa ne anlama gelir?" → Azami hız 50 km/h. "Mavi daire içinde 30 yazıyorsa?" → Asgari hız 30 km/h.
  3. Shared vs separate path: dividing line = separate.
  4. Roundabout direction (TT-40a): counterclockwise in Turkey.
  5. Snow chains (TT-42): mandatory on marked roads; go on drive wheels.
  6. Mandatory direction signs implicitly prohibit other turns.

7. Memorize These

Kırmızı daire = YASAK (prohibition).
Mavi daire = MECBURİ (mandatory).

Azami hız = maximum speed → kırmızı daire.
Asgari hız = minimum speed → mavi daire.

Yaya + bisiklet arasında çizgi var = ayrı yol. Çizgi yok = ortak yol.

Kar zinciri → çekici lastiklere (ön tekerlekler, FWD araçlarda).

Mecburi yön işareti = gösterilen yön dışına dönüş yapılamaz.
