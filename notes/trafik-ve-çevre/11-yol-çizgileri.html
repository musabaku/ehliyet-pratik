Yol Çizgileri — Road Markings (Rank 4)

Last updated: 2026-04-15
Slides covered: 7 slides from 2026-04-15 class — kesik çizgi, devamlı çizgi, çift devamlı, combined markings, yaya geçidi markings, road-surface symbols, park yeri çizgileri
Hierarchy rank: 4 (Yol çizgileri) — the lowest priority source. Overridden by sign boards (3), traffic lights (2), and police (1). See 05-trafik-işaretleri-öncelik.md.

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

Yol çizgileri (road markings) are painted directly on the road surface. They are rank 4 in the hierarchy — the baseline rules when nothing higher overrides them. They control lane discipline, overtaking, pedestrian crossings, and parking.

The fundamental rule

Line type Can you cross it? Can you overtake across it?
Kesik çizgi (dashed) Evet — you may cross Evet — you may overtake (with care)
Devamlı çizgi (solid) Hayır — you may NOT cross Hayır — no overtaking
Çift devamlı çizgi (double solid) Hayır — absolutely not Hayır — bölünmüş yol separator

2. SLIDE 1 — Kesik Yol Çizgisi (Dashed Line)

What the slide showed

A road photo with a dashed centreline, plus a diagram showing a car overtaking across a dashed line.

Kesik yol çizgisi: Bu çizgi boyunca kurallara uymak şartıyla öndeki araç geçilebilir.
(Dashed road line: along this line, you may overtake the vehicle ahead provided you follow the rules.)

Clean English explanation

A dashed (broken) centreline means the lane boundary is permeable — you may cross it to overtake, provided:
1. The road ahead is clear and visible for a safe distance.
2. There is no oncoming traffic that would require you to abort.
3. You are not in a no-overtaking zone (TT-27 sign, bridge, tunnel, curve, hill crest).
4. You signal and complete the manoeuvre safely.

Critical exam fact

Kesik = geçilebilir. This is the simplest rule and the exam uses it as the baseline: "Kesik yol çizgisi ne anlama gelir?" → Öndeki araç geçilebilir (kurallara uymak şartıyla).

3. SLIDE 2 — Devamlı Yol Çizgisi (Solid Line)

What the slide showed

A road with a solid white centreline, plus a diagram showing a car with a red X — you cannot cross.

Devamlı yol çizgisi: Bu çizgi boyunca hiçbir sebeple sol şeride geçilemez ve üzerinden gidilemez.
(Solid road line: under no circumstances may you cross to the left lane or drive over this line.)

Critical exam fact

Devamlı = geçilemez. No exceptions. Even if the vehicle in front is going 10 km/h, you cannot cross a solid line to overtake. You must follow until the line becomes dashed. "Devamlı çizgi boyunca öndeki araç geçilebilir mi?" → Hayır, hiçbir sebeple geçilemez.

4. SLIDE 3 — Yan Yana İki Devamlı Yol Çizgisi (Double Solid Line)

What the slide showed

A road with two parallel solid lines, plus a diagram showing a car with a red X.

Yan yana iki devamlı yol çizgisi: Yolu, bölünmüş yol durumuna getirmiştir. Hiçbir sebeple çizgilerin soluna geçilemez.
(Two side-by-side solid lines: creates a divided-road condition. Under no circumstances may you cross to the left of the lines.)

Critical exam fact

Çift devamlı = bölünmüş yol. The double solid line functions as an invisible median — it divides the road into two completely separate carriageways. Even if there is no physical refüj (median island), the double solid line makes it a bölünmüş yol legally. This matters because bölünmüş yol has different speed limits (110 km/h max outside settlement) vs tek yönlü yol (90 km/h).

5. SLIDE 4 — Combined Line Types (4 configurations)

The 4 configurations

Configuration Name Rule
Kesik çizgi (dashed only) Kesik Çizgi Öndeki araç geçilebilir
Devamlı çizgi (solid only) Devamlı Çizgi Öndeki araç geçilemez, şerit değiştirilemez
Kesik + Devamlı yan yana Kesik ve Devamlı Çizgi The side with the kesik (dashed) line may cross; the side with the devamlı (solid) line may NOT cross. Your side determines your right.
Hatched / taralı alan Hatched Zone (taralı alan) Genişleme alanı — şerit sayısı artıyorsa veya azalıyorsa, geçiş alanı. Taralı alana girilmez.

Critical exam fact — the combined (kesik + devamlı) line

Which side are YOU on? If the dashed line is on YOUR side of the road, you may cross to overtake. If the solid line is on your side, you may not. The other lane's driver has the mirror-image rule.

"Yan yana kesik ve devamlı çizgi varsa, hangi taraftaki sürücü geçiş yapabilir?" → Kesik çizgi tarafındaki sürücü.

Taralı alan (hatched zone)

Taralı alan (hatched/chevron-marked area) = girilmez (do not enter). These are painted buffers — often at merge points, lane reductions, or before obstacles. They are NOT a lane. Driving on them is a violation.

6. SLIDE 5 — Yaya Geçidi Çizgileri · Yavaşlama Uyarı · Engelli Park

The 4 markings

Marking Turkish English What it means
Zebra çizgileri (solid bars) Yaya Geçidi Pedestrian crossing (solid) Thick white bars across the road — pedestrians have priority here
Kesik çizgili yaya geçidi Yaya Geçidi (kesik) Pedestrian crossing (dashed) Dashed bars across the road — less common variant
Yavaşlama uyarı çizgileri Yavaşlama Uyarı Çizgileri Slow-down warning lines Ladder-like lines painted before hazards to prompt braking
Engelli aracı park yeri Engelli Aracı Park Yeri Disabled-vehicle parking space Wheelchair symbol painted on the ground — reserved for disabled drivers

Critical exam fact

Yaya geçidinde yaya her zaman önceliklidir. If a pedestrian is on or about to step onto a yaya geçidi, the driver must stop and yield. This applies even if there is no traffic light — the painted marking alone creates the obligation.

7. SLIDE 6 — Road-Surface Symbols (Yol Üzeri Sembolleri)

The 5 painted symbols

Symbol Turkish English What it means
Inverted triangle Yol Ver Yield (painted on road) Reinforces the TT-1 YOL VER sign — yield ahead
DUR text Dur Stop (painted on road) Reinforces the TT-2 DUR sign — full stop ahead
50 number Azami Hız Speed limit (painted) Reinforces the speed limit sign — 50 km/h here
Bicycle icon Bisiklet Yolu Bicycle lane This lane is for bicycles only
Diagonal stripes Taralı Alana Girilmez Hatched area — do not enter Buffer zone — not a lane

Critical exam fact

Road-surface symbols are rank 4 — they do not override sign boards (rank 3) or higher. They reinforce what a sign already says. If a sign says 30 km/h and the road says 50 km/h, the sign wins (rank 3 > rank 4).

8. SLIDE 7 — Park Yeri Çizgileri ve Park Yasağı Çizgileri

What the slide showed

A photo of a parking lot with white-lined spaces, plus a diagram showing angled and parallel parking configurations with correct/incorrect positioning.

Park Yeri Çizgileri: Araçların duracakları yerleri belirlemek amacıyla park yerlerine çizilen çizgilerdir.
(Parking-space lines: lines drawn in parking areas to define where vehicles should stand.)

Park Yasağı Çizgileri: Park etmenin yasaklandığı kesimlerde bordür taşlarına veya kaplama üzerine çizilen çizgilerdir.
(No-parking lines: lines drawn on kerb stones or the road surface in sections where parking is forbidden.)

Critical exam fact

Sarı-siyah bordür çizgisi = park yasak. Yellow-and-black painted kerbs mean parking is forbidden at that location. This is the ground-level equivalent of the P-1 (no parking) sign.

9. Why this matters for the exam

  1. Kesik = geçilebilir. Devamlı = geçilemez. The single most fundamental road-marking rule.
  2. Çift devamlı = bölünmüş yol. Speed limits change.
  3. Combined line — your side determines your right. Dashed side may cross; solid side may not.
  4. Taralı alan = girilmez. Hatched zones are not lanes.
  5. Road-surface symbols reinforce signs but do NOT override them (rank 4 < rank 3).
  6. Yaya geçidi = yaya öncelikli. Always yield to pedestrians at a crossing.
  7. Yellow-black kerb = park yasak.

10. Memorize These

Kesik çizgi → geçilebilir (dashed = may cross).
Devamlı çizgi → geçilemez (solid = may NOT cross).
Çift devamlı çizgi → bölünmüş yol (double solid = divided road).

Kesik + devamlı yan yana: kesik tarafındaki sürücü geçebilir, devamlı tarafındaki geçemez.

Taralı alan → girilmez (hatched = do not enter).

Yol çizgileri = rank 4 (en düşük öncelik). Levha (rank 3) ile çelişirse levha kazanır.

Yaya geçidinde yayalar her zaman önceliklidir.

Sarı-siyah bordür = park yasağı.
