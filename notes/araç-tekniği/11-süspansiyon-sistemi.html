Süspansiyon Sistemi — Suspension System (+ Şase)

Last updated: 2026-04-14 (class 2)
Covered in class: Önemli dipnotlar + arka aks diyagramı (slide 1), Görev + parçalar + tekerlek diyagramı (slide 2), Araç üzerinde süspansiyon animasyonu (slide 3), Şase nedir (slide 4), Tam araç iskelet diyagramı (slide 5), Karoser nedir (slide 6), Günümüz otomobil yapısı — çelik türleri (slide 7)

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

Süspansiyon Sistemi: Araç hareket halinde iken yerden gelebilecek sarsıntı ve darbeleri üzerine alarak şasiye iletilmesini engelleyen sistemdir.
(The suspension system takes on the shocks and impacts coming from the road while the vehicle is in motion, and prevents them from being transmitted to the chassis (şase).)

Plain English: when a wheel hits a bump, the suspension absorbs that impact before it can travel up into the car's body. Its two jobs are:

  1. Konfor / comfort — keep the ride smooth for passengers
  2. Yol tutuş / road grip — keep all four tires in constant contact with the road so the brakes, steering, and traction systems can actually work

Without suspension, every bump would be transmitted straight into the chassis and the cabin — the car would bounce uncontrollably, tires would momentarily lift off the road (losing grip for braking and steering), and mechanical parts plus passengers would absorb every impact.

The system's enemy is vertical shock; its solution is a spring + damper pair at every wheel.

  2. A labeled line drawing of a rear suspension showing the parts: "U" CİVATASI, YAY KÜPESİ, AMORTİSÖR, DİNGİL, KELEPÇE, YAY GÖZÜ.

Önemli Dipnotlar — 2 facts to memorize

Dipnot 1 — What controls the spring's oscillation

Yayların salınımını kontrol altına alan parça amortisördür.
The part that controls the oscillation of the springs is the amortisör (shock absorber).

Plain English: A spring alone is useless. If you hit a bump with only a spring, the spring will compress, then rebound, then compress again, then rebound — the car will bounce for many seconds after the bump. The amortisör (shock absorber, literally "absorber") is a hydraulic damper that sits next to the spring and kills the oscillation — it turns each bounce into a single smooth up-down motion.

Exam angle: "Süspansiyondaki yayların salınımını kontrol eden parça hangisidir?" → Amortisör.

The spring absorbs the bump; the amortisör tames the spring.

Dipnot 2 — Blown shock = chassis noise on bumpy roads

Amortisör patlamışsa engebeli yolda şasiden ses gelir.
If a shock absorber has blown / burst (patlamış), you'll hear a noise coming from the chassis (şase) on bumpy roads.

Plain English: A blown amortisör no longer dampens the spring. The spring bounces freely, the wheel hits its travel limit, and metal parts clank against each other — producing the characteristic "şasiden ses geliyor" (noise from the chassis). You'll often see a secondary symptom too: a yağ kaçağı (oil leak) visible on the outside of the shock body, because "patlamış" often means the seal has failed and hydraulic fluid has leaked out.

Exam angle: "Engebeli yolda şasiden ses geliyorsa hangi parça arızalıdır?" → Amortisör patlamıştır.

Yay küpesi Spring shackle Hinged link at one end of the leaf spring, allows the spring to flex and change length

Why leaf springs on the rear axle of older/commercial vehicles: leaf springs can carry very heavy loads, they're cheap, durable, and dual-purpose (they also help locate the axle laterally). Modern passenger cars use helezon yay (coil springs) instead for better comfort.

Görev (verbatim) — Turkish → English

Suspension System: The system that takes on shocks and impacts from the road surface while the vehicle is moving, and prevents them from being transmitted to the chassis.

Parçaların isimleri — Parts

Turkish English What it does
Yaylar (helezon yaylar) Springs (coil springs) Absorb the initial shock — compress when the wheel hits a bump, extend when it drops into a dip. Helezon yay = coil spring (the spiral steel spring used on modern passenger cars).
Amortisör Shock absorber Damps (slows down) the spring's oscillation so the car doesn't bounce repeatedly. Hydraulic.
Makaslar Leaf springs Stacked curved metal strips used as a single combined spring element. Found on trucks, commercial vehicles, and the rear axles of older pickups. Makas and yaprak yay are the same thing.

Parts visible in the diagram

Label (TR) English Role
Amortisör ve Yay Shock absorber + coil spring (combined strut) The whole spring-damper unit at the wheel
Direksiyon Bağlantısı Steering linkage attachment Where the rot başı connects to this wheel hub — ties the steering and suspension together
Alt Süspansiyon Kolu Lower suspension arm / lower control arm (salıncak) Holds the wheel hub to the chassis, lets it swing up and down
Şasi Chassis The car's skeleton — suspension mounts to it, but shocks must not reach it

Clean English explanation — how it all works together

When the front wheel rolls into a pothole or over a bump:

  1. The wheel moves up (or down).
  2. That motion pushes the alt süspansiyon kolu (lower control arm) to pivot around its mounting point on the chassis.
  3. The helezon yay (coil spring) between the control arm and the chassis compresses, absorbing the impact energy as spring tension.
  4. Immediately the spring wants to rebound — it pushes back and tries to bounce.
  5. The amortisör (shock absorber, mounted next to or inside the spring) resists that bounce with hydraulic damping — each up-down motion pushes oil through a tiny valve, turning kinetic energy into heat.
  6. The wheel returns to its neutral position smoothly, without bouncing, and the chassis barely feels the bump at all.

The direksiyon bağlantısı reminds you that the steering and suspension share hardware — this is why "süspansiyon arızası" and "ön düzen arızası" symptoms overlap (see 10-direksiyon-ön-düzen-sistemi.md).

Critical exam fact

Süspansiyonun 3 temel parçası: yay (helezon yay), amortisör, makaslar.
The 3 fundamental parts of the suspension: spring (coil spring), shock absorber, leaf spring.

4. SLIDE 3 — Animasyon: Süspansiyon iş başında (Animation: Suspension in action)

What the slide showed

An animated illustration: a silver Peugeot 308 hatchback at the top (real car image), then below it a cutaway cartoon side view of the same car in bright green, showing two purple strut assemblies clearly visible inside the wheel arches — one at each axle. The car is driving over a wavy orange road surface, and the strut units can be seen compressing and extending as the wheels follow the bumps, while the car body (green) stays relatively level above. A play/pause button at the bottom controls the animation.

What the animation demonstrates

  • The road surface is uneven (engebeli yol) — the orange wavy line represents bumps, dips, potholes, and undulations that a car encounters on real roads.
  • The wheels follow the road contour exactly — they go up into bumps and drop into dips, staying in constant contact with the ground.
  • The strut units (yay + amortisör, purple) absorb that up-down motion — you can see them compressing on bumps and extending on dips.
  • The car body (yeşil kısım) stays nearly level — the chassis barely moves even as the wheels do all the work below.

That third point is the whole purpose of the system: the wheels dance up and down, but the cabin stays steady. This is what "süspansiyonun şasiye sarsıntı iletmemesi" looks like in motion.

Why hoca shows this animation

It's the easiest way to internalize the phrase "darbeleri üzerine alarak şasiye iletmesini engeller". Without seeing it in motion, students tend to confuse "süspansiyon" with "rot/balans/ön düzen" — but the animation makes it obvious that süspansiyon is specifically about vertical motion absorption, not steering or alignment.

Critical exam fact

Süspansiyon, wheel motion ile chassis motion'ı birbirinden ayırır: tekerlekler yol ile temasta kalırken kabin sabit kalır.
Suspension decouples wheel motion from chassis motion: wheels stay in contact with the road while the cabin stays stable.

5. SLIDE 4 — ŞASE NEDİR (What is the Chassis)

What the slide showed

A full-slide text block in large red + blue letters, no image. The title ŞASE NEDİR is in red, and the body paragraph explains what a chassis is and why it's the most important safety component in the car.

Verbatim Turkish → English

ŞASE NEDİR
Şase arabanın iskelet sistemidir. Şase arabanın tüm parçalarının monte edildiği en büyük bölümüdür. Şase bir arabanın güvenliğinin baş unsurudur. Şase, araba şirketlerinin milyonlarca dolar para döktüğü en önemli güvenlik bölümüdür. Kaza anında çarpma şiddetini göğüsleyen, gerekli kırılma noktalarından kırılarak, kaza şiddetini en aza indiren bölümdür.

WHAT IS THE CHASSIS
The chassis is the skeleton system of the car. It is the largest section onto which all the car's parts are mounted. The chassis is the primary safety element of a car. The chassis is the most important safety component that car companies pour millions of dollars into. In an accident, it is the part that takes on the force of the impact, and by breaking at its designed crumple points, it minimizes the severity of the crash.

Clean English explanation

The şase is the car's steel backbone — every other component (engine, transmission, suspension, body panels, interior, wheels) is bolted to it. Two key ideas on the slide:

Idea 1 — "Şase = iskelet sistemi"

Think of the chassis as the skeleton that everything else hangs off. Without it, you'd have a pile of parts with nothing to hold them in the right relative positions. The shape of the chassis defines the shape of the car.

Idea 2 — Controlled crumple zones (the big safety concept)

Kaza anında çarpma şiddetini göğüsleyen, gerekli kırılma noktalarından kırılarak, kaza şiddetini en aza indiren bölümdür.

Modern chassis design is not about being "as rigid as possible". It's about being rigid where it needs to be (around the passenger compartment / kabin) and deliberately weak in specific places (at the front and rear crumple zones). When the car hits something, the front of the chassis is engineered to fold and crush in a controlled way at pre-planned kırılma noktaları (breakpoints / crumple points). This crumpling does 2 critical things:

  1. Absorbs collision energy — turning it into metal deformation instead of letting it reach the passengers.
  2. Extends the time of the impact — a longer crumple time means lower peak deceleration, which means much less force on the human body.

The passenger cabin (yolcu hücresi / safety cell) itself stays rigid and intact, while the front and rear zones sacrifice themselves. This is called kontrollü darbe emici yapı or passive darbe emici sistem — and it's one of the pasif güvenlik sistemleri (passive safety systems) you already memorized in 09-fren-sistemi.md.

Connection to süspansiyon (why şase is on this slide)

The entire süspansiyon sistem's purpose is framed in terms of şase — "sarsıntıları şasiye iletmesini engeller". So before moving on, hoca defines what şase actually is. The flow of the lesson is:

  • Süspansiyon protects şase from road shocks (slides 1-3)
  • Şase is the skeleton + the primary safety element (slide 4)
  • Therefore: if süspansiyon fails (e.g., amortisör patlar), impacts reach şase → noise + safety degradation

Critical exam facts

Şase = arabanın iskelet sistemidir.
Chassis = the skeleton system of the car.

Şase, aracın güvenliğinin baş unsurudur (primary safety element).
Chassis is the primary safety element of the vehicle.

Kaza anında şase, belirlenmiş kırılma noktalarından kırılarak çarpma şiddetini azaltır.
In an accident, the chassis breaks at designed crumple points to reduce impact severity.

5B. SLIDE 5 — Tam Araç İskelet Diyagramı (Full-Car Skeleton View)

What the slide showed

A 3D cutaway illustration of a complete car with the body panels rendered semi-transparent, so you can see the internal frame and all 4 wheels with their suspension assemblies. Four labels point to the key parts:

Label (TR) English Color in diagram
Alt Süspansiyon Kolu Lower suspension arm / control arm Blue
Amortisör Shock absorber Green (cylindrical, one at each wheel — all 4 visible)
Şasi Chassis Orange (the long frame rails + cross members forming the skeleton)
Direksiyon Bağlantısı Steering linkage Yellow (running from the steering rack to the front wheels)

Clean English explanation

This slide ties together everything from süspansiyon, direksiyon, and şase into one picture. Key observations:

  1. There are 4 amortisörs — one at every wheel (not just the front). Each wheel has its own independent spring-damper unit so each wheel can absorb shocks independently.
  2. The şasi (orange frame) runs the full length of the car — it's the skeleton that the engine, seats, body panels, and every suspension arm bolt onto.
  3. Alt süspansiyon kolu (blue) connects each wheel hub to the chassis — this is the pivoting arm that lets the wheel move up and down while the chassis stays level.
  4. Direksiyon bağlantısı (yellow) runs from the center of the car to the front wheels only — because only the front wheels steer. The rear wheels have suspension but no steering linkage.

Why this matters — the "one system, shared hardware" insight

Looking at this diagram, you can see that süspansiyon and direksiyon live in the same physical space and attach to the same hardware (the wheel hub). That's why:

  • A worn rot başı (direksiyon arızası) and a blown amortisör (süspansiyon arızası) can produce very similar symptoms (noise, loose feel, poor handling)
  • Ön düzen ayarı (front-end alignment) checks rot AND suspension angles together
  • Damage to one system often stresses the other

Critical exam fact

Her tekerlekte ayrı bir amortisör vardır — toplam 4 amortisör.
Every wheel has its own shock absorber — 4 shock absorbers total.

5C. SLIDE 6 — KAROSER NEDİR (What is the Body)

What the slide showed

A full-slide text block (red title, blue body text) defining karoser (car body).

Verbatim Turkish → English

Karoser nedir?
Motorlu kara vasıtalarının, yolcu ve yükleri taşıyan kısmı. Ön çamurluk, motor kapağı ve ön ızgara, karoserden değildir. Karoser, otomobilin satışında rol oynayan en önemli faktördür.

What is the body (karoser)?
The part of motorized land vehicles that carries passengers and cargo. The front fender, hood (motor cover), and front grille are NOT part of the karoser. The karoser is the most important factor in selling a car.

Clean English explanation

Karoser is the Turkish term for the car body — the outer enclosure that contains the passenger cabin and the cargo area. Importantly:

  • Karoser = yolcu + yük bölümü → the part that encloses people and cargo
  • NOT karoser: ön çamurluk (front fender), motor kapağı (hood / bonnet), ön ızgara (front grille). These are separate body panels attached to the chassis, not part of the karoser proper.

The reason this matters on the exam: there's a trap question that lists several body parts and asks "which of these is NOT part of the karoser?" — the answer is one of the three excluded parts (çamurluk, motor kapağı, or ızgara).

Karoser vs Şase vs Kaporta — the 3-way distinction

This is a common confusion and the exam loves it. Here's the clean breakdown:

Turkish English What it is
Şase Chassis The skeleton / frame that all parts bolt to. Primary safety element.
Karoser Body (passenger/cargo enclosure) The part of the body that carries passengers and cargo — excludes front-end panels like çamurluk, motor kapağı, ön ızgara.
Kaporta Body panels (sheet metal) The metal outer cover of the car — hood (kaput), doors (kapılar), fenders, etc. All the external sheet-metal panels collectively.

Mental model:
- Şase = skeleton
- Kaporta = skin
- Karoser = the passenger/cargo compartment specifically

"Satışta rol oynayan en önemli faktör"

The slide ends by saying karoser is the most important factor in selling a car. This is the aesthetic angle — because the karoser is what the customer actually sees and sits in, its design, finish quality, paint, and comfort are what make or break a sale. Hoca may mention this as a throwaway fact, but it's not usually exam-tested directly.

Critical exam fact

Karoser: yolcu ve yükleri taşıyan kısımdır. Ön çamurluk, motor kapağı ve ön ızgara karoserden değildir.
The karoser is the part that carries passengers and cargo. The front fender, hood, and front grille are NOT part of the karoser.

5D. SLIDE 7 — GÜNÜMÜZ OTOMOBİL YAPISI (Modern Car Body Construction)

What the slide showed

Title: Günümüz Otomobil Yapısı (Modern Automobile Structure). A cutaway illustration of a modern car with its body shell exposed, color-coded by material. 5 different colored sections show 5 different materials used in different parts of the car:

Label (TR) English Color Typical location in a car
Çok Yüksek Mukavemetli Çelik Ultra High Strength Steel (UHSS) Orange/gold Roof, A/B/C pillars, door beams — the passenger safety cage
Yüksek Mukavemetli Alüminyum High Strength Aluminum Silver/grey Front end structure, bumper beams — lightweight front crash structures
Geleneksel Çelik Traditional (mild) Steel Light grey Rear end, non-critical body panels
Bor Çelik Boron Steel Red B-pillar reinforcement, door impact beams, rocker panels — the strongest possible areas
Yüksek Mukavemetli Çelik High Strength Steel (HSS) Blue Floor pan, lower structure — main load-bearing areas

Clean English explanation

Modern cars are not made of a single material. Instead, each section of the body uses the material best suited for its job:

1. Bor Çelik (Boron Steel) — the strongest

Used where you want maximum strength with minimum thickness. Typically found in the B-pillar (the vertical post between the front and rear doors), door impact beams, and rocker panels (under the doors). Boron steel is heat-treated to extreme hardness — it's several times stronger than regular steel. This is the material that saves your life in a side-impact collision.

2. Çok Yüksek Mukavemetli Çelik (Ultra High Strength Steel)

The passenger safety cage — the roof, A-pillars (windshield posts), B-pillars, C-pillars (rear window posts), and the floor cross-members around the seats. This is the rigid cell that must NOT deform in a crash — the space where the passengers sit has to remain intact while everything outside it crumples.

3. Yüksek Mukavemetli Çelik (High Strength Steel)

Used for the main load-bearing structures that need strength but also some capability to absorb energy. Floor pan, lower body, structural cross-members.

4. Yüksek Mukavemetli Alüminyum (High Strength Aluminum)

Used in the front end and for bumper beams. Aluminum is lighter than steel but still strong enough for crash absorption — reducing front-end weight improves fuel economy and handling.

5. Geleneksel Çelik (Traditional Steel)

The normal, cheaper mild steel used for areas that don't need special strength — rear body panels, non-critical outer sheet metal, etc.

The "mixed materials" principle

This slide teaches a modern engineering concept that's increasingly asked on the exam: cars are built with different materials in different zones to get the best combination of safety (strong where you need it) and efficiency (light where you can afford to be). Key takeaways:

  1. The passenger cell is reinforced with the strongest materials (UHSS + boron steel) — the goal is zero deformation around the people.
  2. The crumple zones (front and rear) use materials and structures designed to deform in a controlled way to absorb crash energy (tying back to slide 4's "kırılma noktalarından kırılarak" concept).
  3. Weight reduction via aluminum improves fuel economy, handling, and braking distance.

Connection to şase (slide 4)

Slides 4, 6, and 7 together tell a coherent story about the car's structural design:

  • Slide 4: şase is the skeleton and the primary safety element — it must break at designed crumple points to absorb impact energy.
  • Slide 6: karoser is the passenger/cargo enclosure (a subset of the overall body structure).
  • Slide 7: modern şase + karoser construction uses multiple materials — the strongest (bor çelik, UHSS) around the passengers, lighter and more deformable materials at the crumple zones.

Critical exam facts

Günümüz otomobilleri tek bir malzemeden yapılmaz — farklı bölgelerde farklı mukavemette çelik ve alüminyum kullanılır.
Modern automobiles are not made from a single material — different strength grades of steel and aluminum are used in different zones.

En güçlü malzeme bor çeliktir — yolcu güvenlik hücresinin kritik noktalarında (B-direği, kapı takviyeleri) kullanılır.
The strongest material is boron steel — used in the critical points of the passenger safety cell (B-pillar, door reinforcements).

5E. Hoca's Live Class Additions (2026-04-14) — Süspansiyon & Şase

Addition 1 — Practical car-buying tip: tap the hood to test build quality

Hoca gave a real-world car-shopping analogy while explaining the "Günümüz Otomobil Yapısı" slide:

"Belki içinizde ileride araba almayı düşünenler vardır. Günlük hayatta bazı arabanın kaputunu şöyle kapatıyorsun. Ses geliyor. Ağır bir araba. İyi. Bazısına şöyle bir dokunuyorsun. Plastik içine göçüyor. İşte Allah korusun ileride olabilecek bir olumsuzluğu düşünüp kendini de sevdiklerini de o aracın içinde düşündüğün ne? Sen o plastik olan aracama binip ilerlemek istersin? Yoksa bu şekilde eğilmeyecek, görünmeyecek, Allah korusun çarpışma olsa da güvenlik olarak daha konfor sağlayacak bir aracın git, içinde gitmeyi mi isteyeceksin? Buna işte dikkat etmek gerekiyor. Yani kullanılan malzemelere de bakmak gerekiyor."

Plain English: When shopping for a used car, hoca says a practical quality test is to tap the hood (kaput) or press on the body panels:

  • Heavy, solid "thud" → real metal, thick panels, well-built car
  • Light plastic sound + the panel flexes inward under your fingers → thin sheet metal or plastic cladding, cheap build quality

Why this matters: in a collision, the stronger-built car (more boron steel, UHSS, thicker panels — see slide 7) will protect the passengers better. A car whose hood flexes inward under finger pressure is a car whose crumple zones may not perform as designed.

The underlying principle (tying back to the şase + karoser + material slides 4, 6, 7):

Arabanın güvenliği malzemesinden başlar.
A car's safety starts with its materials.

This isn't an exam point per se, but it's a life advice hoca wanted you to take away: when you eventually buy your own car, don't just look at the price and the model — physically touch the car's panels and get a feel for how solid it is. The same money can buy you a "ağır araba" (solid, safe car) or a "plastik araba" (flimsy, unsafe car) depending on the manufacturer's build quality.

Addition 2 — Reinforcing the "süspansiyon absorbs road shocks for the chassis" concept with a live mental model

Hoca explained the süspansiyon görev using a different angle:

"Amortisör dediğim şey tekerleğin hemen böyle üst tarafında. Araç çukura girdi. Ne bileyim bir kasise giriyorsunuz, çıkıyorsunuz. Salalım yapmasını sağlıyor. Aracın daha rahat hareket etmesini sağlıyor."

Plain English: Hoca framed the amortisör as the component that sits right above the wheel and allows the wheel to follow bumps and dips in the road independently of the chassis. When you:

  • Enter a çukur (pothole) → the wheel drops → the spring extends → the amortisör slows the extension → the chassis barely moves
  • Drive over a kasis (speed bump / raised crossing) → the wheel rises → the spring compresses → the amortisör dampens the bounce → the chassis stays level

The word "salınım" (oscillation) means the up-down bouncing motion. Amortisör = salınımın kontrolü (control of oscillation). Already captured in slide 1, but hoca added the kasis example as a real-world trigger — because kasisler (speed bumps) are everywhere in Turkish residential streets, and if your süspansiyon is bad, kasisler are where you'll notice it first.

Addition 3 — Amortisör patladı → "şaseye böyle değiyor gibi ses gelir" (the metallic contact sound)

Hoca elaborated on the slide 1 fault ("Amortisör patlamışsa engebeli yolda şasiden ses gelir"):

"Amortisör patladı diyelim ki ön taraf. Bu şeye böyle değiyor gibi ses gelir araçtan. İşte şase'den ses geliyor."

Plain English: When an amortisör is blown, the spring can compress fully and the metal parts of the suspension come into direct contact with the şase — producing a hard "clank" or metallic banging noise from under the car. It's not just "a noise" in general — it's specifically the sound of metal touching metal where the süspansiyon's damper used to absorb that impact.

Diagnostic rule:
- Bumpy road + clanking / banging noise from one corner of the car = that amortisör is patlak
- The other corners are still fine
- Drive it to the service but don't keep driving for long — each bump is now hitting the şase directly and stressing it

6. Why this matters for the exam

Typical exam questions on süspansiyon + şase:

  1. "Süspansiyon sisteminin görevi nedir?" → Yerden gelen sarsıntı ve darbeleri şasiye iletmeyen sistemdir.
  2. "Yayların salınımını kontrol eden parça hangisidir?" → Amortisör
  3. "Engebeli yolda şasiden ses geliyorsa hangi parça arızalıdır?" → Amortisör patlaktır
  4. "Süspansiyonun parçaları nelerdir?" → Yay (helezon yay), amortisör, makaslar
  5. "Şase nedir?" → Arabanın iskelet sistemidir / en önemli güvenlik bölümüdür.
  6. "Kaza anında şasinin görevi nedir?" → Çarpma şiddetini emmek, kırılma noktalarından kırılarak kaza şiddetini en aza indirmek.
  7. "Makas ne demektir?" → Yaprak yay / leaf spring
  8. "Araç üzerindeki konforun sağlanması hangi sistem sayesindedir?" → Süspansiyon sistemi

The rot / balans / süspansiyon confusion (clean separation)

Symptom System Fault
Car pulls to one side Ön düzen (direksiyon) Rot ayarı bozuk
Steering wheel vibrates at speed Ön düzen (lastik) Balans ayarı bozuk
Play / slop in the steering wheel Direksiyon Rot başı arızalı
Tires wear on inside/outside edges Ön düzen Rot ayarı / kamber bozuk
Noise from chassis on bumpy roads Süspansiyon Amortisör patlak
Excessive bouncing after a bump Süspansiyon Amortisör zayıf / arızalı

Süspansiyon is about vertical motion. Ön düzen / direksiyon is about horizontal (left-right) motion.

7. Memorize These

Süspansiyonun görevi: Yerden gelen sarsıntı ve darbeleri üzerine alarak şasiye iletmesini engeller.
Suspension's purpose: takes on road shocks and prevents them from reaching the chassis.

Yayların salınımını kontrol altına alan parça amortisördür.
The part that controls spring oscillation is the shock absorber.

Amortisör patlamışsa engebeli yolda şasiden ses gelir.
A blown shock absorber causes chassis noise on bumpy roads.

Süspansiyon parçaları: yaylar (helezon yay), amortisör, makaslar (yaprak yay).
Suspension parts: springs (coil springs), shock absorber, leaf springs.

Şase, arabanın iskelet sistemidir ve aracın güvenliğinin baş unsurudur.
The chassis is the car's skeleton and the primary element of vehicle safety.

Kaza anında şase, kırılma noktalarından kırılarak çarpma şiddetini en aza indirir.
In an accident, the chassis breaks at crumple points to minimize impact severity.
