Aydınlatma ve İkaz Sistemi — Lighting & Warning System

Last updated: 2026-04-14 (class 2)
Slides covered: Görev + 8 parts, Arızaları (fault diagnosis flow), Göstergeler sistemi (dashboard), 4 critical warning icons, composite icons, sinyal/far indicators (class 1); Göstergelerin 3 renk kuralı, Aydınlatma sisteminin görevi, Uzun far + 100m + mavi, Far ayarı + 25/100m + sinyal/sigorta, Fren lambası + müşir, Geri vites lambası + paslanma, Römork elektrik + gres yağı, Göstergeler recap (yağ/şarj/hararet/yakıt/fren), Hararet → klima kapat + kalorifer aç, Motor arıza lambası (sarı check engine), Fren balatası aşınma göstergesi, Kızdırma bujisi (diesel), Farlar açık uyarı lambası (class 2 post-break)

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

Aydınlatma ve ikaz sistemi: Güvenli bir sürüş için yolun aydınlatılması ve sinyallerin hareketlerinin bildirilmesini sağlar.
(For safe driving, it provides illumination of the road and indication of signal movements.)

This system has two jobs in one:

  1. Aydınlatma (lighting) — lights up the road so you can see (headlights, fog lights)
  2. İkaz (warning/signaling) — lets others see you and understand your intentions (brake lights, turn signals, reverse light, horn, hazard lights)

Both jobs share the same 8 components.

Connection to şarj sistemi

Four components are shared with the şarj sistemi: akü, kontak anahtarı, sigorta, kablolar. These are the general electrical-wiring parts of the car, sometimes grouped under "elektrik tesisatı" (electrical installation). The difference is that şarj sistemi generates the electricity while aydınlatma-ikaz sistemi consumes it.

3. SLIDE 2 — Arızaları (Fault Diagnosis Flow)

What the slide said

Arızaları:
- Lambalardan biri geçmiş ise yenisi ile değiştirilir.
- Aynı hattaki lambaların hiçbiri yanmıyorsa sigorta ve kabloları kontrol edilir.
- Fren lambası sağlam olduğu halde yanmıyorsa fren müşürü arızalıdır.
- Geri vites lambası sağlam olduğu halde yanmıyorsa geri vites müşürü arızalıdır.

Clean English translation

  1. "If one of the lamps has burned out, it should be replaced with a new one."
  2. "If none of the lamps on the same circuit are lighting, the fuse and cables should be checked."
  3. "If the brake lamp is intact but still doesn't light, the brake switch (fren müşürü) is faulty."
  4. "If the reverse lamp is intact but still doesn't light, the reverse gear switch (geri vites müşürü) is faulty."

How to diagnose a dead light — decision tree

Light isn't working?
│
├─ One single lamp dead?
│   └─▶ Bulb burned out → REPLACE BULB (ampul değiştirilir)
│
├─ Multiple lamps on the same circuit dead?
│   └─▶ CHECK FUSE + WIRING (sigorta + kablolar kontrol edilir)
│
├─ Brake light alone dead + bulb is intact?
│   └─▶ FREN MÜŞÜRÜ (brake switch) arızalıdır
│
└─ Reverse light alone dead + bulb is intact?
    └─▶ GERİ VİTES MÜŞÜRÜ (reverse gear switch) arızalıdır

Key concept — "müşür" (switch/sender)

A müşür is a small switch/sensor that detects a driver action and turns on a light in response:

  • Fren müşürü — detects when you press the brake pedal → turns on the brake light
  • Geri vites müşürü — detects when you shift into reverse → turns on the reverse light

If the müşür is broken, the bulb is physically fine but nothing ever tells it "turn on now." That's why the slide uses the phrase "sağlam olduğu halde yanmıyorsa" — "even though it is intact, if it doesn't light."

Classic exam questions from this slide

  1. "Fren lambası yanmıyor ve ampulü sağlam. Arıza nerededir?"
    → Fren müşüründe (in the brake switch)
  2. "Geri vites lambası yanmıyor, ampulü sağlam. Arıza nerededir?"
    → Geri vites müşüründe
  3. "Aynı hattaki tüm lambalar yanmıyorsa ilk kontrol edilecek şey?"
    → Sigorta ve kablolar
  4. "Bir lamba yanmıyor, diğerleri çalışıyor. Ne yapılır?"
    → Ampul yenisiyle değiştirilir

⚠️ CRITICAL EXAM RULE — the "3 red light" rule

If ANY of these three red lights turns on while driving, you must stop the car immediately to prevent permanent engine damage:

  1. 🔴 Yağ basınç lambası (oil pressure)
  2. 🔴 Şarj / akü lambası (charging/battery)
  3. 🔴 Hararet lambası (engine temperature)

Ignoring any of these can destroy the engine within minutes. This is one of the single most common exam questions in the Araç Tekniği section.

4D. Hoca's Live Class Addition — Yarım Kontak (Half-Ignition) in the Driving Test

This is a practical/exam concept that isn't on the slides but comes up on the actual driving test (direksiyon sınavı):

"Komisyon diyecek ki: yarım kontak yap, bekle. Yani sen anahtarını ikinci tur şuraya çevirdiğinde aracın çalışacak. Yarım kontakta seni bekletiyor. Niye bekletiyor? Göstergeni çalıştırabiliyorsun çünkü yarım kontaktayken. Sana göstergeden sorular soruyor — bu ışıkların anlamlarını."

What is "Yarım Kontak"?

Yarım kontak ("half-ignition") = the ON / Accessory position of the ignition switch. It's the position the key sits in between OFF and START. In this position:

  • The car's electrical accessories are powered on (dashboard, gauges, radio, lights)
  • The engine is NOT running (you haven't turned all the way to the marş position yet)
  • All the warning lights on the dashboard light up so the driver can see them

Why the examiner asks for it

During your driving test (direksiyon sınavı), before you start the car, the examiner (komisyon) will say:

"Yarım kontak yap, bekle."
(Make half-ignition, wait.)

Then they'll point at warning light icons on your dashboard and ask you to identify them:
- "Bu ne?" (What's this?) → şarj lambası / yağ lambası / hararet lambası / etc.
- "Bu ne işe yarıyor?" (What does this do?)
- "Sağa sinyal ver." (Give a right turn signal.)
- "Sola sinyal ver." (Give a left turn signal.)

You must know every single warning light on the dashboard — including the three red ones from the "3 red light rule," plus the yellow ones (fuel, door-open, etc.), the blue one (uzun far), and the green ones (sinyal, kısa far).

Rule to memorize

Yarım kontak = engine off, dashboard ON. This is when the examiner tests your knowledge of dashboard warning lights before you start the engine.

This is why the göstergeler content (slides 4, 4A-4C and the 3-red-light rule) aren't just an exam topic — they're also a practical test requirement. You have to identify each warning light by sight, in Turkish, on the day of the driving test.

Hoca's key quote

"Bu dersi sadece e-sınavda sınavı geçeyim, on tane soruyu çözeyim gibi düşünmeyin lütfen. Araç kullanırken bu bilgiler çok çok işinize yarayacak."
(Don't think of this lesson as just "pass the e-exam by solving 10 questions." When you actually drive, this information will be very useful.)

4E. SLIDE (2026-04-14) — GÖSTERGELER: The 3-Color Rule

What the slide showed

Title in red: "GÖSTERGELER…!!" (Indicators…!!). Below, a 3-rule color-coded list explaining what it means when a dashboard indicator light is off, red, or yellow — a simple traffic-light-style rule for interpreting any warning lamp on any car.

Verbatim Turkish → English

GÖSTERGELER…!!

1 — SÖNMÜŞ İSE: ARIZALI HERHANGİ BİR SİSTEMİMİZ YOK DEMEKTİR

2 — KIRMIZI İSE: O SİSTEM ARIZALI DEMEKTİR. SERVİSE GİDİLMELİ, ARAÇ O HALDE SÜRÜLMEMELİDİR.

3 — SARI İSE: UYARI IŞIĞIDIR. ACİL DEĞİLSE EN KISA ZAMANDA SERVİSE GİDİLMESİ GEREKTİĞİNİ BİLDİRİR.

INDICATORS…!!

1 — IF OFF (SÖNMÜŞ): Nothing in our systems is faulty.

2 — IF RED (KIRMIZI): That system is faulty. You must go to a service center, and the car should not be driven in this condition.

3 — IF YELLOW (SARI): It is a warning light. If not an emergency, it informs you that you need to go to a service center as soon as possible.

Clean English explanation — the universal 3-color rule

This is one of the most exam-tested pieces of dashboard knowledge because it's a simple rule you can apply to any warning light on any car without memorizing each specific icon. The traffic-light logic:

Renk / Color Meaning Action Priority
Sönmüş (off) System is operating normally — no fault detected Keep driving Normal
Sarı (yellow/amber) Uyarı — warning. There's something that needs attention but it's not urgent Go to service en kısa zamanda (as soon as possible) Medium
Kırmızı (red) Arıza — fault. Something is broken or dangerous right now Stop driving, go to service immediately Urgent

The analogy: traffic lights work the same way — red = stop, yellow = slow down / warning, green/off = go. Your dashboard lights use the same color code.

Rule 1 — "Sönmüş ise" (If it's off)

If a warning light is not illuminated, the system it represents is working correctly. This is why, after you turn the key to ignition (position 2, see 12-güvenli-araç-kullanma.md slide 11), all the warning lamps light up for a brief self-test — and then they all go out once the engine starts. All-off = all systems nominal.

Rule 2 — "Kırmızı ise" (If it's red)

A red warning light means the car should not be driven. Red indicators are reserved for faults that:
- Pose an immediate safety risk (brake failure, steering failure)
- Will cause immediate engine/mechanical damage if you keep driving (low oil pressure, overheating, charge failure, broken timing belt)
- Prevent the car from being controlled properly (airbag failure, ABS failure)

The 3 red lights you already know (see slide 4):

  1. Yağ ikazı (oil pressure warning) — RED — stop immediately, engine will seize
  2. Şarj ikazı (charge / battery warning) — RED — alternator failure, engine will die when battery drains
  3. Hararet ikazı (temperature warning) — RED — engine overheating, stop and let it cool

If any red light is on → pull over safely + stop engine + do not drive until repaired.

Rule 3 — "Sarı ise" (If it's yellow)

A yellow / amber warning light is a softer warning. The system is degraded or needs attention, but it's not an immediate danger. You can continue driving carefully, but you should visit a service station as soon as possible.

Common yellow warnings:
- Check engine / motor arıza lambası — engine management has detected a fault (often a sensor or emissions issue)
- ABS uyarı lambası — ABS is disabled; normal brakes still work, but the anti-lock assist is off
- Yakıt seviyesi düşük — low fuel warning
- Lastik basıncı uyarı (TPMS) — tire pressure is low
- Ön cam yıkama suyu — washer fluid is empty
- Emniyet kemeri hatırlatma — seatbelt reminder (yellow on many cars; sometimes red with sound)

The action: "Acil değilse en kısa zamanda servise gidilmesi gerektiğini bildirir" → if it's not urgent, go to service soon — don't ignore it, but you don't need to pull over immediately.

How this rule works with the 3-red-light system

The 3-red-light rule from slide 4 of this file (yağ, şarj, hararet) is a special case of the universal rule:

  • All three are RED → all three follow the "kırmızı ise" rule → stop immediately, don't drive

You already memorized those three specifically because they're the ones that kill your engine fastest if ignored. But the universal 3-color rule gives you a fallback for any other light you might see on an unfamiliar dashboard: if it's red, stop; if it's yellow, visit service soon; if it's off, everything is fine.

Exam question patterns

This slide maps directly to several high-frequency exam questions:

  1. "Gösterge panelindeki kırmızı renkli uyarı ışığı ne anlama gelir?"
    → O sistemin arızalı olduğunu, aracın kullanılmaması gerektiğini bildirir.

  2. "Sarı renkli uyarı ışığı yandığında sürücü ne yapmalıdır?"
    → Acil değilse en kısa zamanda servise gitmelidir.

  3. "Göstergelerin tamamı söndüğünde bu ne anlama gelir?"
    → Arızalı bir sistem yoktur (tüm sistemler normal çalışıyor).

  4. "Sürüş sırasında kırmızı şarj ikazı yandı, ne yapılmalıdır?"
    → Araç durdurulmalı, motor kapatılmalı, yetkili servise götürülmelidir. (Kırmızı = arızalı → sürülmemeli.)

Critical exam fact

Göstergelerin 3-renk kuralı:
- Sönmüş → sistem normal
- Sarı → uyarı, en kısa zamanda servise
- Kırmızı → arıza, araç sürülmemeli, derhal servise

The 3-color rule for dashboard lights:
- Off → system normal
- Yellow → warning, go to service ASAP
- Red → fault, car must not be driven, go to service immediately

4G. Hoca's Live Class Additions (2026-04-14) — Göstergeler & Yarım Kontak Exam Test

Addition 1 — The "yarım kontak" (half-ignition) sınav testi: exact committee questions

We already know from class 1 (section 4D of this file) that the committee stops you at yarım kontak and quizzes you on the dashboard. Hoca gave more detail in class 2 about exactly what the committee asks:

"İlk çevirdiğimde bazı elektronik aksamları ve aydınlatmaları devreye sokacağız ve burada komisyon sana 'yarım kontak, çevir, dur' diyor. Çünkü burada şu göstergesi, bu göstergesi, sağa sinyal ver, sola sinyal ver, ayağının altındaki pedalları sayar mısın, uzun farlarını açar mısın, kısa farlarını açar mısın, iç aydınlatma nerede gibi araçla ilgili arabayı tanıyor musunuz diye sorular soracağım der."

Plain English: At the driving exam, the committee will tell you to turn the ignition key to the yarım kontak (half-ignition / position 1 or 2) position — NOT to full start. While the key is in half-ignition, all the dashboard warning lamps are lit up, all the lighting controls are powered, but the engine is NOT running. Then the committee will fire a barrage of "find and show me" questions while you sit there. They want to see if you actually know the car you're about to drive.

Known exam questions at the yarım kontak test:

  1. "Şu ikaz/gösterge lambası ne anlama gelir?" — "What does this warning/indicator icon mean?" (they point to one on the dashboard)
  2. "Sağa sinyal ver" — "Signal right" (you must operate the sinyal kolu to the right)
  3. "Sola sinyal ver" — "Signal left"
  4. "Ayağının altındaki pedalları say" — "Name the pedals under your feet" (answer: soldan sağa debriyaj, fren, gaz)
  5. "Uzun farları aç" — "Turn on the high beams"

Sinyal lambası sık yanıp sönüyorsa lambaların biri yanmıyordur.

Plain English: When you turn on your left or right turn signal, it should blink at a normal, steady rhythm (about once per second — tik-tok, tik-tok). If instead it blinks unusually fast / rapidly (tiktiktik-tiktiktik), that's a fault indicator — specifically, it means one of the two signal bulbs (front or rear) is burned out / not lighting up.

Why this happens (the electrical reason): the signal flasher is designed to flash at a fixed rate based on the combined electrical load of two bulbs (one front + one rear, both flashing together). When one bulb burns out, the total load drops in half, and the flasher runs faster to compensate. This rapid blinking is the car's way of telling you without a warning light that a bulb is blown.

Exam trigger phrase:

- "Sinyali açtığımda normalden hızlı yanıp sönmesi ne anlama gelir?" → Lambalardan biri arızalıdır, değiştirilmelidir. (One of the bulbs is faulty and must be replaced.)

What to do: go around the car with the signal on — left side front/rear, then right side front/rear — and see which bulb is not lighting up. Replace that bulb and the blink rate returns to normal.

Fact 5 — SİGORTA: always replace with the same amperage

Sigorta attığı zaman mutlaka AYNI AMPER sigorta ile değiştirilmelidir…!!!!!!

Plain English: A sigorta (fuse) is a deliberately weak electrical connection designed to "blow" (break) when too much current flows through it — protecting the rest of the circuit from burning up. Each fuse is rated for a specific amper (amperage) — like 5A, 10A, 15A, 20A, 25A — and is color-coded on modern cars.

The rule: when a sigorta atar (the fuse blows), the replacement fuse must be the same amperage as the original. Never:

  • Higher amperage ❌ — the circuit can now carry more current than it was designed for, and something else in the circuit will burn up instead (wiring, switch, or the actual device). This has started many car fires.
  • Lower amperage ❌ — the new fuse will immediately blow again as soon as you use the circuit, because normal current exceeds the new fuse's rating.
  • No fuse / wire trick ❌ — bypassing the fuse with a piece of wire removes the protection entirely. Never do this. This is the #1 cause of electrical fires in older cars.

The correct reaction to a blown fuse:

  1. Identify which circuit the blown fuse protects (radio? horn? signals? brake lights?)
  2. Replace it with the exact same amperage from your spare fuse kit (stored in the glove box or fuse panel door)
  3. If it immediately blows again → there's a short circuit somewhere in that wiring, stop using that circuit and go to an electrician to find and fix the short. Don't keep replacing fuses.

Exam trigger phrase:
- "Sigorta atar ise nasıl bir sigorta ile değiştirilir?" → Aynı amperdeki sigorta ile değiştirilir.
- "Sigortayı bir telle değiştirmek caiz midir?" → Hayır, çok tehlikelidir. Yangına sebep olur.

- No turn signals visible → following traffic doesn't know you're turning

⭐ NEW: The klima + kalorifer emergency trick

This is NOT in the class 1 notes and is a high-value practical exam fact:

Motorun hararetlendiğinde: klima açık ise kapatılır, kalorifer sıcak konumda çalıştırılır.

Why this works — the physics:

  1. Klima (A/C) uses the engine to compress refrigerant → this adds load to the engine → makes the engine work harder → generates more heat. When overheating, turning off the A/C removes that extra load.

  2. Kalorifer (heater/heat) works by circulating hot coolant from the engine through a small radiator inside the cabin → a fan blows cabin air through it → the air gets warm and the coolant gets cooler. Turning the heater to FULL HOT + FULL FAN essentially adds a second radiator to the cooling system, using the passenger cabin to dump heat out of the engine.

Result: the engine coolant temperature drops slightly because:
- Less load (no A/C drawing power)
- More heat dissipation (cabin acts as extra radiator)

It's uncomfortable for the passengers (the cabin gets very hot), but it buys you time to pull over safely when the engine is overheating.

Exam trigger phrase:

Clean English explanation — glow plugs on diesel engines

This is a DIESEL-SPECIFIC topic. It does NOT apply to benzinli (gasoline) cars. Let's walk through why diesel engines need glow plugs:

Background: how diesels ignite fuel

As covered in 01-yakıt-sistemi.md:
- Benzinli motorlarda (gasoline engines): fuel ignites from a spark plug (buji) — the spark provides the heat
- Dizel motorlarda (diesel engines): fuel ignites from compressed, superheated air — no spark plug needed; the air in the cylinder is compressed so hard it becomes hot enough to ignite the injected mazot

The cold-weather problem with diesels

On cold mornings (below ~5°C), the air entering the diesel cylinder is already very cold. Even after being compressed, it may not reach the temperature needed to ignite the mazot — or the engine will run very rough / smoke heavily / refuse to start.

The solution: kızdırma bujisi (glow plug)

A kızdırma bujisi (glow plug) is a small electrical heating element inside each cylinder of a diesel engine. When you turn the key to position 2 (kontak açık) on a cold diesel, the glow plugs turn on and heat up red-hot inside the cylinders for about 5–15 seconds. This pre-heats the air inside the cylinder so that when you then crank the starter, compression is enough to ignite the fuel.

The dashboard indicator — critical procedure

The kızdırma bujisi lambası is a dashboard warning light that lights up when the glow plugs are active. The procedure for starting a cold diesel:

  1. Turn the key to kontak açık (position 2)
  2. The kızdırma bujisi lambası lights up — glow plugs are heating
  3. WAIT for the light to go out (5–15 seconds depending on how cold)
  4. NOW crank the starter — the engine will start smoothly
  5. If you try to crank while the glow plug light is still on, the engine will start hard or refuse to start

The exam rule:

Dizel motorlu araçlarda marşa basmadan önce kızdırma bujisi lambasının sönmesi beklenir.
In diesel-engined vehicles, you must wait for the glow plug light to go off before pressing the starter.

Gasoline vs diesel start procedure — contrast

Step Benzinli (gasoline) Dizel (diesel)
1 Key to kontak açık Key to kontak açık
2 (Nothing to wait for) WAIT for kızdırma bujisi lamba söner
3 Crank starter Crank starter
4 Engine starts Engine starts

The key difference: diesel drivers must watch the glow plug light and wait. Gasoline drivers can go straight from kontak to crank.

Icon description

The icon looks like two connected coils/loops — a visual representation of the glow plug's heating coil element. It's sometimes called the "pretzel" or "glasses" icon informally.

Critical exam fact

Key safety and practical rules (not explicitly on slide but critical)

1. Don't touch the glass of the new bulb with bare fingers

Halojen (halogen) ampulleri are extremely sensitive to finger grease. The tiny amount of oil from your skin, when the bulb heats up, can create a hot spot that causes the glass to expand unevenly and crack / shatter the bulb — often within hours of installation.

Always handle new bulbs by the metal base or wear gloves. If you accidentally touch the glass, clean it with alcohol before installing.

2. Replace bulbs in pairs

If one far ampulü has burned out, the other one is likely nearing its end of life too (both have been running the same hours). Replace them as a pair so:
- Both headlights have equal brightness
- You don't have to do this job twice in a few weeks
- The light pattern on the road is symmetric

3. Check the correct ampul type

Different cars use different bulb types:
- H1 — single filament
- H4 — dual filament (kısa + uzun far in one bulb)
- H7 — single filament, very common in modern cars
- HB3 / HB4 — used on some Japanese cars
- LED / Xenon — modern types, often not user-replaceable

Check the kullanım kılavuzu or the markings on the old bulb to find the correct replacement type.

4. Spare bulbs stored in the car

Many cars (and it's a legal requirement in some EU countries) come with a yedek ampul kiti (spare bulb kit) stored in the trunk or under a seat. This kit contains one of each bulb type used on the car (headlight, tail, signal, plate, interior). Know where your spare bulb kit is — you might need it on the road.

Why the "fiş → lastik → ampul" order matters

The order of removal (plug first, then boot, then bulb) exists because:

  1. Pulling the plug first disconnects electrical power — safety first. The bulb can't accidentally light up while you're touching it.
  2. Removing the rubber boot second gives you access to the bulb's spring clip or twist base.
  3. Removing the bulb last is self-explanatory.

Re-installation is the mirror order (bulb → boot → plug) so that:

  1. The bulb is seated first (needs to be fully inserted before anything else)
  2. The rubber boot seals against moisture (must go on before the electrical plug for water-tight protection)
  3. The plug is connected last — power returns only after everything else is in place
