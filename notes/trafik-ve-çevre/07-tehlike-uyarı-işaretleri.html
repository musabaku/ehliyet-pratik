Tehlike Uyarı İşaretleri — Danger Warning Signs

Last updated: 2026-04-15
Slides covered: 5 slides from 2026-04-15 class — the full catalog of Turkish "tehlike uyarı" (red-bordered triangle) signs, codes T-1 through T-49
Hierarchy rank: 3 (Trafik işaret levhaları) — overrides road markings, loses to traffic lights and to a live officer. See 05-trafik-işaretleri-öncelik.md for the full hierarchy.

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

Tehlike uyarı işaretleri — danger warning signs — are the first of the three main categories of trafik işaret levhaları (sign boards). They warn drivers about conditions ahead so that they have time to slow down, raise attention, and prepare. They do not give commands the way a yasaklama (prohibition) or emir (order) sign does. Their message is always "something dangerous is coming — be ready."

The universal visual signature — learn this once

Every tehlike uyarı sign shares the same three properties, and the exam loves to test them:

Property Rule
Shape Eşkenar üçgen (equilateral triangle)
Tip direction Tepe noktası yukarı (point facing up) — except one exception: the YOL VER sign, which is a tehlike uyarı-style triangle but with the tip pointing down (and it's technically a yield sign, not a warning)
Colour Kırmızı kenar (red border), beyaz zemin (white background), siyah sembol (black symbol)

Critical exam mnemonic: If you see a red-bordered triangle with the tip pointing up, it is a tehlike uyarı işareti — its job is to warn, not to command.

How far ahead are they placed?

  • Yerleşim yeri dışında (outside built-up areas): placed 150–250 m before the hazard.
  • Yerleşim yeri içinde (inside built-up areas): placed 50–150 m before the hazard.
  • Otoyollarda: 250–500 m before the hazard.

This distance rule is a classic exam question — memorize at least the first two.

Action when you see any tehlike uyarı sign

  1. Yavaşla — reduce speed.
  2. Dikkati artır — raise attention; scan the road ahead.
  3. Gerekli ise dur — stop if the situation requires.
  4. Sinyal verme/işaret verme hazırla — prepare to signal if the hazard requires maneuvering.

2. SLIDE 1 — Virajlar · Tehlikeli Eğim · Daralan Kaplama

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
T-1a Sağa Tehlikeli Viraj Dangerous bend to the right A single sharp bend curving right ahead
T-1b Sola Tehlikeli Viraj Dangerous bend to the left A single sharp bend curving left ahead
T-2a Sağa Tehlikeli Devamlı Virajlar Continuous dangerous bends, first to the right A sequence of bends starting with a right turn
T-2b Sola Tehlikeli Devamlı Virajlar Continuous dangerous bends, first to the left A sequence of bends starting with a left turn
T-3a Tehlikeli Eğim (İniş) — %10 Dangerous gradient (downhill) A steep descent of 10% ahead
T-3b Tehlikeli Eğim (Çıkış) — %10 Dangerous gradient (uphill) A steep climb of 10% ahead
T-4a Her İki Taraftan Daralan Kaplama Road narrows on both sides The lane narrows from both edges
T-4b Sağdan Daralan Kaplama Road narrows on the right The lane narrows only from the right side

Key exam nuances for this slide

  • "Tehlikeli viraj" vs "devamlı virajlar" — single bend vs multiple in series. The first bend's direction is what determines "a" (sağa) or "b" (sola) on T-2.
  • %10 in eğim signs — the number shows the steepness (10% = 10 m vertical drop per 100 m of horizontal road). Exam questions will ask "ne anlama gelir?" → "ahead there is a steep descent/ascent".
  • Iniş vs çıkış distinction — both T-3a and T-3b use the same triangular sign shape; the difference is the arrow direction on the hill icon (downward = iniş, upward = çıkış).
  • Daralan kaplama traps — T-4a narrows from both sides; T-4b narrows only from the right. Watch for a variant T-4c that narrows from the left. The action is the same: slow down, stay central, be ready to yield to oncoming traffic.

3. SLIDE 2 — Pedestrians · Animals · Work Zones · Devices · Environment

The 8 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
T-12 Okul Geçidi School crossing ahead Children crossing — reduce speed immediately; stop if needed
T-13 Bisiklet Geçebilir Bicycles may cross Bicycle-crossing zone ahead
T-14a Ehli Hayvanlar Geçebilir Domestic animals may cross Livestock/farm animals (cattle, sheep) may be on the road
T-14b Vahşi Hayvanlar Geçebilir Wild animals may cross Deer / wild animals may be on the road
T-15 Yolda Çalışma Road work ahead Work zone — yellow background instead of white!
T-16 Işıklı İşaret Cihazı Traffic-light device ahead Traffic light ahead — brake-ready, especially useful on fast/rural roads
T-17 Havaalanı — Havalimanı (Alçak Uçuş) Airport — low-flying aircraft Low-flying planes may cross overhead; unexpected noise/shadow hazard
T-18 Yandan Rüzgar Side wind Strong crosswind area — grip steering firmly

Critical exam nuances for this slide

  • T-15 is yellow! All other tehlike uyarı signs have a white background. The yolda çalışma sign uses yellow to signal temporary hazards. This is a classic exam trap: which tehlike uyarı sign has a yellow background? → Yolda çalışma (T-15).
  • Ehli vs vahşi hayvanlar — T-14a shows a cow (farm animal), T-14b shows a deer (wild). Both mean "animals may be on the road," but the exam will specifically ask for the distinction.
  • Okul geçidi (T-12) vs okul geçidi çizgisi — the sign is rank 3; the painted ground markings are rank 4. If you see both, the sign wins — but in practice they reinforce each other.
  • Havaalanı (alçak uçuş) sounds weird but it's a real sign. Low-flying aircraft can distract drivers or startle horses/livestock — the warning is about driver reaction, not collision with planes.

4. SLIDE 3 — Traffic Situations · Kavşaks · Demiryolu Geçidi

The 12 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
T-19 İki Yönlü Trafik Two-way traffic ahead One-way road becomes two-way — oncoming traffic will appear
T-20 Dikkat Attention / general hazard Generic "something dangerous" sign — used when no specific sign fits. Single red "!" in a triangle
T-21 Kontrolsüz Kavşak Uncontrolled intersection A cross-intersection with no signs, no lights — right-of-way rules apply
T-22a Ana Yol — Tali Yol Kavşağı Main road / minor road intersection You are on the ana yol; side roads will join
T-23a Sağdan Ana Yola Giriş Minor road joins main road from the right A tali yol will feed into your lane from the right
T-23b Soldan Ana Yola Giriş Minor road joins main road from the left Same, but from the left
T-24 Dönel Kavşak Roundabout A traffic circle ahead — yield rules apply
T-25 Kontrollü Demiryolu Geçidi Controlled railway crossing Railway crossing with barriers/gates
T-26 Kontrolsüz Demiryolu Geçidi Uncontrolled railway crossing Railway crossing without barriers — you must stop, look, listen
T-27a Kontrolsüz Demiryolu Geçidi (Tek Hat) Uncontrolled railway crossing — single track Single-rail crossing, no barriers
T-27b Kontrolsüz Demiryolu Geçidi (Çift Hat) Uncontrolled railway crossing — double track Two parallel tracks, no barriers — much higher risk
T-28a,b Demiryolu Hemzemin Geçit Yaklaşımı (Sağ/Sol) Railway crossing approach bars Vertical red/white striped bars showing how close the railway is

Critical exam nuances for this slide

  • T-20 "DİKKAT" is the universal warning: a single red exclamation mark in a triangle. Used where a specific warning sign doesn't fit. Exam question: "üçgen içinde ünlem işareti ne anlama gelir?" → Genel tehlike uyarısı — dikkat edilmesi gereken bir durum var.
  • Kontrollü (T-25) vs kontrolsüz (T-26/T-27) demiryolu geçidi — kontrollü has bariyer (gate); the symbol is a vertical fence-like icon. Kontrolsüz has no barrier; the symbol is a stylized locomotive (T-26) or an X (T-27).
  • Tek hat vs çift hat (T-27a/b) — the difference is a single red X vs a doubled/crossed X. Exam questions will test: "çift hatlı demiryolu geçidine kaç metre kala levha konulur?" — the placement distance rule still applies.
  • Kontrolsüz kavşak (T-21) — right-hand rule: at an uncontrolled intersection, the vehicle on the right has priority. This is a universal Turkish traffic law, and the T-21 sign just reminds you that you're entering such an intersection. Memorize this — it comes up constantly.
  • Dönel kavşak (T-24) — roundabout priority: vehicles already in the roundabout have priority; entering vehicles yield. This matches international convention.

5. SLIDE 4 — Demiryolu Approach Bars · Köprü · Viraj Yön Levhaları · Ek Levhalar

The 13 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
T-29a,b Demiryolu Hemzemin Geçit Yaklaşımı 200 m (Sağ/Sol) Railway crossing approach — 200 m 200 m before the railway, with diagonal red/white stripes
T-30a,b Demiryolu Hemzemin Geçit Yaklaşımı 100 m (Sağ/Sol) Railway crossing approach — 100 m 100 m before the railway — fewer stripes
T-31a,b Köprü Başı Levhası (Sağ/Sol) Bridge-start marker Black-and-white striped vertical bar showing the beginning of a bridge
T-32 Engel İşareti Obstacle marker Yellow-and-black striped horizontal bar marking a roadside obstacle
T-33a Tehlikeli Viraj Yön Levhası (single chevron) Dangerous-bend direction marker A single red chevron pointing the direction of the bend — placed inside the curve
T-33b Tehlikeli Viraj Yön Levhası (multiple chevrons) Dangerous bend — multiple chevrons 4 chevrons in series — a sharper, longer bend
T-33c Tehlikeli Viraj Yön Levhası (intermediate) Intermediate version Between T-33a and T-33b — moderate bend
T-33d,e,f Onarım Yaklaşım Levhası (Sağ/Sol) Road-repair approach markers Red/white diagonal stripes signaling a repair zone approach
T-34a,b Refüj Başı Ek Levhası (Sağ/Sol) Median-island start marker Yellow/black chevron pointing to a divider/median island ahead
T-35 Dönüş Adası Ek Levhası Turn-island marker Yellow/black upward chevron indicating a turn island
T-36 Düşük Banket Low shoulder The road shoulder is lower than the lane — don't drift onto it
T-37 Gizli Buzlanma Hidden icing / black ice Black-ice warning — typically on bridges and shaded stretches

Critical exam nuances for this slide

  • Demiryolu approach-bar progression: T-28 → T-29 → T-30. The number of diagonal stripes tells you the distance:
  • 3 stripes → ~300 m from railway
  • 2 stripes → ~200 m from railway
  • 1 stripe → ~100 m from railway

So if you see a railway-approach bar with one stripe, you are only 100 m from the tracks — slow down immediately.

  • T-33 chevron signs are placed ON the bend itself, not before it — their job is to show which direction the bend goes when a driver is already inside a sharp curve at night or in fog. Red chevrons on white = tehlikeli viraj yön levhası (dangerous bend direction marker).

  • T-36 düşük banket (low shoulder) is a hidden killer — if your right wheel drops off the lane onto a shoulder that's 5–10 cm lower, you can lose control while trying to get back on. The sign warns you not to drift off.

  • T-37 gizli buzlanma (black ice) — the Turkish term "gizli" means "hidden" because black ice is invisible. The sign uses a snowflake symbol in a red triangle. You'll see it on bridges (wind exposure), shaded forest stretches, and northern Turkish highways in winter.

6. SLIDE 5 — Trafik Sıkışıklığı · Tramvay Kavşağı

The 2 signs

Code Turkish English What it means
T-30 Trafik Sıkışıklığı Traffic congestion / jam A cluster of cars in a triangle — warns of a queue/jam ahead. Common on highways before toll plazas or accidents
T-49 Tramvay Hattı ile Oluşan Kavşak Intersection with a tram line A tram icon in a triangle — a tram line crosses the road ahead; trams have priority

Critical exam nuances

  • T-30 trafik sıkışıklığı — the action is specifically reduce speed and prepare to stop; rear-end collisions at the back of traffic queues are one of the most common highway accidents, so this sign exists to warn you early enough to brake smoothly.
  • T-49 tramvay hattı — trams always have priority over cars at intersections where a tram crosses. The tram cannot swerve or brake as fast as a car can, so it gets right of way. Exam question: "tramvay hattı ile oluşan kavşakta kim geçiş hakkına sahiptir?" → Tramvay.
  • Note on T-30 numbering: the same "T-30" code appears twice in this slide set — once for "demiryolu geçit yaklaşımı 100 m" in Slide 4 and once for "trafik sıkışıklığı" here. The slide code is just a teaching reference, not a strict catalog ID — don't let the overlap confuse you.

7. Why this matters for the exam

  1. Universal shape/colour rule: "Tehlike uyarı işaretlerinin şekli ve rengi nedir?" → Kırmızı kenarlı, beyaz zeminli, tepesi yukarı bakan eşkenar üçgen.
  2. Yellow exception: "Hangi tehlike uyarı işareti sarı zemin üzerine yapılır?" → Yolda çalışma (T-15).
  3. Placement distances: "Yerleşim yeri dışında tehlike uyarı işaretleri kaç metre önceden konulur?" → 150–250 metre. "Yerleşim yeri içinde?" → 50–150 metre. "Otoyolda?" → 250–500 metre.
  4. T-20 dikkat: "Üçgen içinde sadece ünlem işareti varsa ne anlama gelir?" → Genel tehlike — dikkat.
  5. Kontrolsüz kavşak rule: "Kontrolsüz kavşakta kim geçiş hakkına sahiptir?" → Sağdaki araç (the vehicle on the right).
  6. Dönel kavşak rule: "Dönel kavşakta kim geçiş hakkına sahiptir?" → Kavşak içindeki araç (the vehicle already in the roundabout).
  7. Tramvay önceliği: "Tramvay hattı ile oluşan kavşakta kim geçiş hakkına sahiptir?" → Tramvay.
  8. Kontrollü vs kontrolsüz demiryolu geçidi: kontrollü = bariyerli; kontrolsüz = bariyersiz.
  9. Demiryolu hemzemin geçit yaklaşımı stripes: 3 stripes ≈ 300 m, 2 stripes ≈ 200 m, 1 stripe ≈ 100 m.
  10. Tehlikeli eğim %10: "%10 eğim ne anlama gelir?" → 100 metrede 10 metrelik yükseklik farkı.
  11. İki yönlü trafik (T-19): warns that a one-way section is ending and oncoming traffic will begin.
  12. Yandan rüzgar (T-18): action = direksiyonu sıkı tut, hızı azalt (grip the wheel firmly, slow down).
  13. Düşük banket (T-36): don't drift onto the shoulder — a step-down risk.
  14. Gizli buzlanma (T-37): black ice — especially on bridges and shaded areas in winter.

8. Memorize These

Tehlike uyarı işareti = kırmızı kenarlı + beyaz zeminli + siyah sembollü + eşkenar üçgen + tepesi yukarı.

Placement distance:
- Yerleşim yeri içinde → 50–150 m
- Yerleşim yeri dışında → 150–250 m
- Otoyolda → 250–500 m

Yellow-background exception: Yolda Çalışma (T-15) — the only tehlike uyarı sign on a yellow background (because it's temporary).

Kontrolsüz kavşakta → sağdaki aracın geçiş hakkı vardır.
Dönel kavşakta → kavşak içindeki aracın geçiş hakkı vardır.
Tramvay hattı ile oluşan kavşakta → tramvayın geçiş hakkı vardır.

Kontrollü demiryolu geçidi = bariyerli. Kontrolsüz demiryolu geçidi = bariyersiz.

Hemzemin geçit yaklaşımı şeritleri: 3 şerit ≈ 300 m, 2 şerit ≈ 200 m, 1 şerit ≈ 100 m.

%10 tehlikeli eğim → 100 metrelik yatay yolda 10 metrelik yükseklik farkı.

Ünlem işareti (T-20) = genel tehlike / dikkat.
