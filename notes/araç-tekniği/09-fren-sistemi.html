Fren Sistemi — Brake System

Last updated: 2026-04-14 (class 2)
Covered in class: Görev + çalışma prensibi (slide 1), Parçaları ve görevleri (slide 2), Fren sistemi arızaları (slide 3), Önemli dipnotlar (slide 4)

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

Fren Sistemi: Hareket halindeki aracı önce yavaşlatıp sonra durduran sistemdir.
(The brake system first slows and then stops a moving vehicle.)

Plain English: when you press the brake pedal, all four wheels brake at the same time (4 tekerlek birden durur) — not just the front or back. The system's job is two-step:

  1. Yavaşlatma — slow the car down (initial press)
  2. Durdurma — bring it to a full stop (continued press)

On modern cars the brake system is hydraulic (hidrolik) — you press a pedal, that push is carried as pressure through fluid-filled pipes to each wheel.

Don't confuse the brake system with the handbrake-only role — although el freni (handbrake) is part of the wider brake system, its job is to keep a parked car from rolling, not to slow a moving car down. The main exam definition above is for ayak freni (foot brake / service brake).

2. SLIDE 1 — Fren Sistemi: Görev + Çalışma Prensibi

What the slide showed

A labeled diagram of a car viewed from above. The four brake discs at each wheel are highlighted in red, and the central hydraulic master cylinder is shown in the middle of the chassis with hydraulic lines running out to each wheel. Two boxes of text next to the diagram:

  1. Fren Sistemi — görev tanımı (the purpose definition)
  2. Çalışma Prensibi — how it works

Verbatim Turkish → English

Fren Sistemi: Hareket halindeki aracı önce yavaşlatıp sonra durduran sistemdir. Frene basıldığı zaman 4 tekerlek birden durur.
Brake System: The system that first slows and then stops a moving vehicle. When the brake is pressed, all 4 wheels brake simultaneously.

Çalışma Prensibi: Fren pedalına basıldığında hidrolik borularda iletilerek balatalar, disk ya da kampanaları sıkıştırır ve frenleme gerçekleşir.
Working Principle: When the brake pedal is pressed, the force is transmitted through hydraulic lines; the balata (brake pads) squeeze the disk (disc) or kampana (drum), and braking takes place.

Clean English explanation

The brake system converts pedal force (your leg) into friction at each wheel that turns the car's kinetic energy into heat, slowing the vehicle. It does this in 4 steps:

  1. You press the fren pedalı (brake pedal).
  2. The pedal pushes a piston inside the ana merkez (master cylinder), which compresses fren yağı / hidrolik yağ (brake fluid) inside the lines.
  3. That pressure travels through hidrolik borular (hydraulic lines) to each of the 4 wheels — because liquid is nearly incompressible, the pressure reaches all 4 wheels at the same time and with the same force (that's why 4 tekerlek birden durur).
  4. At each wheel, the pressure pushes the balata (brake pad) against a spinning disk (disc brake) or kampana (drum brake). The resulting friction slows the wheel, which slows the car.

Disc vs drum (disk vs kampana)

Disk fren (disc brake) Kampana fren (drum brake)
Where used today Front wheels (always), often all 4 Rear wheels on older/cheaper cars
Shape Flat metal disc spinning with the wheel Hollow drum with pads inside
Cooling Open to air → cools faster, better for hard use Enclosed → heats up more
Exam note "Disk" = modern standard "Kampana" = older / rear-wheel only

Critical exam fact

Fren basıldığı zaman 4 tekerlek birden durur.
(When you brake, all 4 wheels stop together.)

This is an exam trap — if a question asks "which wheels brake when you press the pedal?", the answer is all 4, simultaneously, not "front only" or "rear only". Only the el freni (handbrake) acts on a subset (usually just the rear wheels).

Verbatim Turkish → English (the 5 parça isimleri)

Turkish (slide) English What it is
Fren balatası Brake pad / brake shoe The friction material that is pressed against the disc or drum when you brake. It's the part that physically wears out over time and needs replacing.
Kampana Brake drum The hollow metal drum at the wheel that the balata presses against from the inside. Used on older cars and rear wheels.
Westing haus Westinghouse (air-brake / brake booster system) Named after inventor George Westinghouse. In the Turkish driving curriculum it refers to the hava freni / fren güçlendirici system — a vacuum or compressed-air assist that multiplies the driver's pedal force so heavy vehicles can brake effectively. On cars it's the brake booster (servo); on trucks/buses it's the full air-brake system.
Rekorlar Pipe unions / fittings The threaded connector fittings where hydraulic brake lines join the master cylinder and wheel cylinders. They seal the pressurized fluid in the system.

Clean English explanation — what each part actually does

1. Fren balatası (brake pad / shoe)
The friction block. When the hydraulic pressure pushes on a piston in the wheel, that piston pushes the balata against the spinning disk or kampana. The friction slows the wheel. Balatas are a consumable — they wear down and must be replaced at periyodik bakım. Worn balatas are what produce the ince ses (thin squealing sound) that means "get your brakes checked."

2. Kampana (brake drum)
A hollow metal drum that bolts to the wheel and spins with it. Inside the drum, two curved balatas (shoes) push outward against the drum's inner surface when you brake. Drum brakes are older technology — today they're mostly found on the rear wheels of cheaper/older cars. The front wheels are almost always disk brakes because discs cool better under heavy use.

3. Westing haus (Westinghouse / brake booster)
Named after George Westinghouse, who invented the air brake for trains in 1869. In modern cars, Westinghouse on the ehliyet curriculum refers to the brake booster / fren güçlendirici — a device that uses engine vacuum to multiply the force of your foot on the pedal. Without it, you'd need to stand on the pedal to stop the car. On large commercial vehicles (camyon, otobüs) it becomes a full compressed-air brake system where brake force is delivered by compressed air instead of hydraulic fluid. If your car's engine dies while driving, you'll notice the brake pedal becomes very hard — that's because the Westinghouse booster lost its vacuum assist.

4. Rekorlar (hydraulic pipe unions)
The word rekor in Turkish mechanical vocabulary means a threaded pipe-union fitting — the metal connector where two hydraulic lines or a line and a component (master cylinder, wheel cylinder) are joined. They have to be absolutely leak-proof, because any air or fluid leak in the brake hydraulics means pressure drops and the brakes fail. A loose rekor is one of the ways air can get into the system, which is why bleeding the brakes ("fren havası alma") is needed after any work on the lines.

5. El freni (handbrake / parking brake)
Separate mechanism from the main hydraulic brakes. Usually operated by a hand lever between the seats (or a button on newer cars). It acts on the rear wheels only, via a steel cable (not hydraulic fluid). Its job is to keep the car from rolling when parked — especially on a slope. It's not designed for slowing a moving car. Rule of thumb: before getting out of the car, always pull the el freni; before driving off, always release it.

Critical exam fact

Fren sisteminin parçaları: fren balatası, kampana, Westinghouse, rekorlar, el freni.
(The parts of the brake system: brake pad, drum, Westinghouse booster, pipe unions, handbrake.)

The trap here is Westinghouse — it looks like a foreign word on a Turkish exam and many students don't recognize it. If you see "Westinghouse" in a multiple-choice list of brake-system parts, it is correct — it refers to the brake booster (or the full air-brake system on heavy vehicles).

2C. SLIDE 3 — Fren Sistemi Arızaları (Brake System Faults)

What the slide showed

Title: FREN SİSTEMİ — Fren Sistemi Arızaları. A numbered list of 4 classic brake faults, each describing a cause and its symptom. These are all high-yield exam traps — the exam almost always includes at least one "frene bastım ama…" scenario question.

The 4 faults — Turkish → English

Fault 1 — Handbrake left engaged

El freni çekik unutulursa balatalar ısınır, yanar ve bir müddet sonra frenler tutmaz, araç rahat harekete geçemez.
If the handbrake is left engaged (forgotten in the "up" position), the brake pads heat up, burn, and after a while the brakes stop gripping; the car can't move freely.

Plain English: Forgetting to release the handbrake is the #1 rookie mistake. The rear balatas stay clamped against the drum/disc while the car tries to drive, friction generates intense heat, the balatas overheat and start to smoke/burn, and eventually they glaze over and lose their grip entirely (frenler tutmaz). The car feels sluggish and won't accelerate normally (araç rahat harekete geçemez) — often accompanied by a burning smell and sometimes visible smoke from the rear wheels.

Fault 2 — Air in the hydraulic system

Sistem hava yaparsa fren tutmaz (fren pedalı yumuşar).
If air gets into the system, the brake fails to hold (the brake pedal becomes soft / spongy).

Plain English: Brake fluid is incompressible, but air is not. If air bubbles enter the hydraulic lines (from a leak, from fluid running too low, or after opening the lines during service), pressing the pedal first compresses the air instead of pushing the fluid — so the pedal sinks softly toward the floor (pedal yumuşar) and not enough pressure reaches the wheels. Fren tutmaz = the brakes don't bite. Fix: fren havası alma (bleeding the brakes) to expel the air.

Exam trap: if a question says "fren pedalı yumuşak/süngerli" (spongy pedal), the cause is hava (air in the system).

Fault 3 — Incorrect brake adjustment

Fren ayarları yanlış yapıldıysa araç çekme yapar, sıkıysa balatalar aşınır, fazla yakıt sarf eder.
If the brake adjustments are set wrong, the car pulls to one side; if they're set too tight, the balatas wear out and the car uses extra fuel.

Plain English: This fault has two sub-cases:
- Uneven adjustment (one wheel braking more than the others) → when you press the brake, the car pulls / drifts to one side (araç çekme yapar). That's because one wheel is grabbing harder than its opposite, and the car yaws toward the harder-braking wheel.
- Over-tight adjustment (balatas slightly pressing even when pedal is released) → the balatas are always rubbing against the disc/drum, so they wear out fast and the engine has to work against the constant drag, which increases fuel consumption (fazla yakıt sarf eder).

Exam traps:
- "Brakes pulling the car sideways" → fren ayarı yanlış / dengesiz
- "Balatas wearing out faster than normal + extra fuel use" → fren ayarı sıkı

Fault 4 — Cold-weather handbrake freeze

Soğuk havalarda el freni çekilirse balatalar donarak yapışır.
In cold weather, if the handbrake is engaged, the balatas can freeze and stick (to the drum/disc).

Plain English: In sub-zero weather, any moisture between the balata and the drum/disc can freeze overnight, gluing them together. When you try to drive off in the morning, the rear wheels won't turn freely — sometimes the balata even rips chunks off when forced. Practical advice hoca will probably give: in very cold winter conditions, don't use the handbrake when parking — instead leave the car in first gear (düz vites) or in P (otomatik vites), and use a wheel chock (takoz) on a slope.

Why the 4 faults cluster on el freni and hidrolik

Three of the four faults (1, 3, 4) involve el freni or ayak freni mechanical adjustment, and only one (2) is hydraulic. That tells you what the exam loves: the consequences of handbrake misuse and the difference between a soft pedal (air) and a pulling car (wrong adjustment).

Critical exam fact

Fren pedalı yumuşaksa → sistemde hava vardır.
Soft/spongy brake pedal → there is air in the system.

El freni çekik unutulursa → balatalar yanar, fren tutmaz olur.
Forgetting the handbrake on → balatas burn and brakes fail.

Fren ayarı bozuksa → araç çekme yapar (yana kayar).
Wrong brake adjustment → car pulls to one side.

2D. SLIDE 4 — "Önemli Dipnotlar" (Important Footnotes)

What the slide showed

Title: FREN SİSTEMİ — "Önemli Dipnotlar". A numbered list of short, single-sentence facts about brake-system behavior, covering hydraulic, vacuum, and air-brake variations. These are high-yield one-liners — each sentence = one possible exam question.

The 6 dipnotlar — Turkish → English

1. Daily hydraulic-fluid check

Hidrolik yağa bakım günlük yapılmalıdır.
Hydraulic brake fluid should be checked daily.

Plain English: Brake fluid is on the günlük kontroller (daily checks) list — you should verify the fluid level every day before driving. This is already in the portal's "Aracın Günlük Kontrolleri" list (fren hidrolik yağı kontrolü).

Exam angle: if a question asks "hangi kontrol her gün yapılır?", fren hidrolik yağı kontrolü is a valid answer.

2. Westinghouse + engine stop = no brakes

Westinghouse'lu araçlarda motor stop edilirse fren tutmaz.
In vehicles with a Westinghouse booster, if the engine stops, the brakes don't hold.

Plain English: The Westinghouse system (brake booster / hava freni) needs the engine to produce vacuum or compressed air for the brake assist to work. If the engine dies while driving, the assist disappears — the pedal becomes very hard to press and braking force drops to almost nothing. You can still brake, but you need much more leg force, and the stopping distance grows dramatically.

Exam trap: "Motoru stop eden Westinghouse'lu aracın freni tutmaz" — this looks extreme but it's the textbook answer.

Practical rule: never turn off the engine while rolling downhill. Keep the engine running → keep the brake booster alive.

3. Air-brake compressor not filling → leak at the fittings

Havalı tip frenlerde kompresör dolmuyorsa hava rekorlarında kaçak vardır.
In air-type brakes, if the compressor is not filling up, there is a leak at the air-line fittings (rekorlar).

Plain English: On trucks/buses with full air brakes (havalı fren), the compressor builds up the air pressure that powers the brakes. If that pressure refuses to reach the target level, the most common cause is a leak at one of the rekorlar — the threaded fittings where the air lines join components. You can usually hear it hissing.

Exam angle: "Havalı frenlerde kompresör dolmuyor. Nedeni nedir?" → Rekorlarda hava kaçağı vardır.

4. Vacuum system fault → pedal becomes hard

Vakum sisteminin arızasında fren pedalı sertleşir.
When the vacuum (booster) system fails, the brake pedal becomes hard.

Plain English: The vakum servo (vacuum brake booster) on a normal passenger car uses engine-intake vacuum to multiply pedal force. When it's working, the pedal feels normal and responsive. When it fails (broken vacuum hose, bad diaphragm, engine off), the assist is gone — the pedal goes rock hard, and braking takes much more leg force.

Exam rule-of-thumb:
- Yumuşak / süngerli pedal (soft, spongy) → hava sistemde (air in the hydraulic lines) — see slide 3, fault 2
- Sert pedal (hard, rock-stiff) → vakum sistem arızası (brake booster failure)

Memorize this pair — they are opposite symptoms with opposite causes, and the exam loves this contrast.

5. Brake retention / no fluid leak at the unions

Frenin sabitlenmesi, rekorlarından hidroliğin akmamasına bağlıdır.
Reliable braking depends on the hydraulic fluid not leaking from the rekorlar (pipe unions).

Plain English: For the brakes to hold pressure consistently, every rekor (fitting) in the hydraulic line must be leak-tight. Even a tiny drip at a union means pressure will bleed off during braking — the pedal slowly sinks, and the brakes feel weak and unreliable. If you see brake fluid under the car at a wheel or under the master cylinder, that's an immediate stop-driving fault.

Exam angle: "Fren sisteminde hidrolik kaçağının en sık yeri neresidir?" → rekorlar (the pipe unions).

6. Disc brakes cool faster (advantage over drums)

Diskli fren sisteminin avantajı, balata ve disk yüzeyinin daha kolay soğumasını sağlamasıdır.
The advantage of disc brakes is that the balata and disc surfaces cool more easily (than drum brakes).

Plain English: A disk (disc brake) is exposed to open air — air flows across both sides of the spinning disc and across the balata, carrying heat away quickly. A kampana (drum brake) is enclosed; its balatas are trapped inside the drum with very little airflow, so heat builds up and causes fade (brakes lose grip when hot). This is why modern cars use disc brakes on the front wheels (where most of the braking happens) and often on all four wheels.

Exam angle: "Diskli fren sisteminin en önemli avantajı nedir?" → Daha kolay / hızlı soğumasıdır (they cool down more easily / faster).

Critical exam facts — the "sertleşir vs yumuşar" pair

Fren pedalı yumuşaksa → hidrolik sistemde hava vardır.
Soft brake pedal → air in the hydraulic system.

Fren pedalı sertse → vakum (booster) sistem arızalıdır.
Hard brake pedal → vacuum booster system has failed.

These two cover almost every "weird pedal feel" question on the exam.

2E. Hoca's Live Class Additions (2026-04-14) — Fren Sistemi

Addition 1 — Physical mechanism: piston pushes the balata outward

Hoca walked through the physical mechanism in detail, beyond what the slides say:

"Siz ayağınızın altındaki fren pedalına bastığınızda tekerleklerin iç kısmında pistonlar var. Bunlar dışarı doğru açılıyor. Kampanayı sıkıştırıyor ve içeriden, iç kısmından tekerleklere sürtünerek tekerleğin hızının azaltılmasını ya da çok sert bastığında tamamen durmasını sağlıyor."

Plain English: Inside each drum brake (kampana), there are pistons sitting next to the balatas. When you press the brake pedal, hydraulic pressure arrives at the wheel cylinder → the pistons push outward → this spreads the balatas apart → the balatas press against the inside of the kampana (drum) → friction between the balata and the drum slows the wheel, or stops it if you press hard.

Key concept: the balatas don't just "touch" the drum — they are forced outward by pistons inside the wheel cylinder. The pistons are the hydraulic-to-mechanical translator. Fluid pressure (fren yağı) → piston force → balata friction at the drum.

Addition 2 — Every vehicle type has its own brake system (bus/truck air brakes vs car hydraulic)

Hoca emphasized that brake systems differ by vehicle type and gave a clear sound-based example:

"Her araçta teknik özelliğine göre frenleme sistemi aynı mıdır değil midir. Otobüsü düşünün, kamyonu düşünün, tırı düşünün. Her birinde farklı fren sistemi kullanılıyor olabilir. Mesela otobüsle örnek vereyim. Havanın tüpü bazısında var, o kompresörden böyle ses gelir. Ama normal bir otomobilde biz bu sesi duymayız."

Plain English: In city buses, kamyons (trucks), and tırlar (semi-trailers), the braking system is usually a hava freni / havalı fren (compressed-air brake, Westinghouse system). These systems use a kompresör (compressor) that builds up pressure in an air tank — and when the driver releases the brake pedal, you hear a distinctive "psssshhh" hissing sound as compressed air is released. That noise is the sound of an air-brake system at work.

In a normal passenger car (otomobil), the brake system is hydraulic (hidrolik), not air-based — no compressor, no air tank, no hissing noise. Just fluid pressure moving silently through the lines.

Exam-ready distinction:

Vehicle type Brake system type Sound
Otomobil (car) Hidrolik fren (hydraulic, fluid-based) Silent
Otobüs, kamyon, tır Hava freni / Westinghouse (air-based, with kompresör) Hissing "psshhh" on release

Takeaway hoca kept repeating: "Her araçta farklı fren sisteminin olduğu mantığı aklınızda bulunsun" — remember that different vehicles have different brake systems. This is the framing you carry into any exam question about brake types.

Addition 3 — Real story: the student who forgot to release the handbrake in the exam

Hoca told a concrete anecdote about how exam failure happens when this rule is violated:

"Bunu sınavda yapan bir aday oldu. El frenini indirmeyi unuttu. Araba bağıra bağıra zorla arabayı götürdü. Hatta böyle 'dın dın' ötüyor. 'Ne oluyor' falan dedi. Bir 10 metre kaldı. Komisyon başkanı dedi ki 'çek. Hocam yer değiştirelim.'"

Plain English: A student in hoca's class went into the direksiyon sınavı, forgot to release the handbrake, and started driving with it still engaged. The car was groaning and making "dın dın" warning noises as the rear balatas were being destroyed. The student asked "what's happening?" — but the komisyon başkanı (committee chairman) stopped the car after only 10 meters and failed the student on the spot, telling them to switch seats with the instructor.

Critical practical lessons from the anecdote:

  1. El freni çekili halde araç ASLA hareket ettirilmez. Not even one meter. The committee fails you immediately — no second chance.
  2. Modern cars WILL move with the handbrake engaged if you floor the gas pedal enough. The handbrake is just friction, not a hard mechanical lock, so enough engine torque will overcome it. "The car is moving" does NOT mean "the handbrake is off". Always verify the el freni ışığı (handbrake warning light) on the dashboard is OFF before driving.
  3. "Dın dın ötüyor" = the sound of balatas being dragged against the drum while the car tries to move against the handbrake. If you ever hear that noise, stop immediately and check the handbrake.
  4. Simultaneously, balatas ısınır → yanar → fren tutmaz (see slide 3 fault 1) — the brakes are actively being destroyed while this happens.

Exam-ready phrasings:
- "El freni çekili unutulursa ne olur?" → Balatalar ısınır, yanar, bir müddet sonra frenler tutmaz olur. Araç sınavda direkt kalma sebebidir.
- "El freni çekiliyken araç hareket ettirilebilir mi?" → Teknik olarak gaza yüklenilirse hareket eder, ama asla yapılmaması gereken bir şeydir — sınavda direkt kalma sebebidir.

Addition 4 — Hoca's framing: detail slides are for reference, the core concept is "different vehicles, different brakes"

Hoca explicitly said about the önemli dipnotlar slide (slide 4):

"Burada yine biraz detay bilgi var. O yüzden geçiyorum. Mesela diyor ki hidrolik yağın bakımını yapalım. Westing house'lu araçlarda motor stop edilirse fren tutmaz. Havalı frende kompresör donmuyorsa hava rekorlarında kaçak vardır. Biraz detay bilgi. Ben dediğim gibi sadece sizin her araç için otobüs düşünün, tır falan hepsinin frenleme sisteminin aynı olmadığını, farklılık arz ettiğini bilseniz benim için yeterli."

Plain English: Hoca said the önemli dipnotlar (daily hydraulic fluid check, Westinghouse-motor-stop, air-brake compressor leaks at rekorlar) are reference-level details — they're in the slides but not the core of what's tested.

The main thing you need to know: different vehicle types have different brake systems. That's the core exam concept. Everything else is supporting detail.

3. Fren çeşitleri — Types of brakes

The exam recognizes two main types of brake:

Turkish English Role
Ayak freni Foot brake / service brake The main pedal — slows and stops the car while driving
El freni Handbrake / parking brake A lever or button that locks the rear wheels to keep a parked car from rolling

Remember: el freni hareket halindeyken değil, park halindeyken kullanılır — the handbrake is for parked cars, not for slowing a moving car.
