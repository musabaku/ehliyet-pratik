Gabari, Taşıma Sınırı, Azami Ağırlık, Dingil Ağırlığı — Vehicle Dimensions & Weight Definitions

Last updated: 2026-04-15
Slides covered: 1 slide from 2026-04-15 class — 4 legal definitions + 5 prohibitive gabari signs (2 m / 3 m / 4.5 m / 6 t / 7 t)

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

The Karayolları Trafik Kanunu (KTK) defines four measurement concepts that together decide whether a vehicle is legally allowed onto a given road:

  1. Gabari — the outer envelope (length × width × height)
  2. Taşıma Sınırı (Kapasite) — what the vehicle is allowed to carry
  3. Azami Ağırlık — how much the whole thing weighs when loaded
  4. Dingil Ağırlığı — how much weight each axle transfers to the road

These four terms show up as bridge, tunnel, and narrow-road prohibition signs (the red-circle signs with a number followed by "m" or "t"). The exam tests both the definitions and the meaning of each sign.

2. SLIDE 1 — The 4 measurement definitions + gabari signs

- 3 m — genişlik sınırı (width limit)
- 4.5 m — yükseklik sınırı (alternative height limit)
- 6 t — dingil ağırlığı sınırı (axle weight limit)
- 7.00 t — yüklü ağırlık sınırı (total loaded weight limit)

To the right, a diagram of a truck approaching a clearance-limited bridge.

Clean English explanation

Gabari — "Vehicle envelope / overall dimensions"

Gabari: Araçların yüklü veya yüksüz olarak karayolunda güvenli seyirlerini temin amacı ile uzunluk, genişlik ve yüksekliklerini belirleyen ölçülerdir.
(The length, width, and height dimensions of vehicles — whether loaded or unloaded — that define the envelope required for safe travel on the public road.)

Three dimensions:
- Uzunluk — length
- Genişlik — width
- Yükseklik — height

Key point: gabari applies whether loaded or unloaded (yüklü veya yüksüz). A flatbed truck carrying tall cargo is subject to the height gabari rule even if it's empty on the return trip with the cargo still on it.

Taşıma Sınırı (Kapasite) — "Carrying limit (capacity)"

Taşıma Sınırı (Kapasite): Bir aracın güvenle taşıyabileceği, en çok yük ağırlığı veya yolcu ve hizmetli sayısıdır.
(The maximum cargo weight, or the maximum number of passengers and crew, a vehicle can safely carry.)

Two separate expressions:
- For cargo vehicles → maximum weight of cargo (in kg or tons)
- For passenger vehicles → maximum number of persons (passengers + crew / hizmetli)

This is what the vehicle is allowed to carry — not its total mass.

Azami Ağırlık — "Maximum total weight"

Azami Ağırlık: Aracın güvenle taşıyabileceği yükle birlikte ağırlığıdır.
(The vehicle's own weight together with the maximum cargo it can safely carry.)

In plain terms: azami ağırlık = curb weight + max cargo. It's the total mass of the loaded vehicle. This is the number tested against the red-circle weight signs (e.g., "7 t").

Dingil Ağırlığı — "Axle weight (axle load)"

Dingil Ağırlığı: Araçlarda aynı dingile bağlı tekerleklerden Karayolu yapısına aktarılan ağırlıktır.
(The weight transferred to the road surface through the wheels attached to a single axle.)

  • A two-axle truck has its total weight split between front axle and rear axle (not always 50/50)
  • Bridges/roads are rated for axle weight because a single axle concentrates the pressure
  • The 6 t sign means no single axle may transmit more than 6 tons to the road — even if total vehicle weight is much higher

Critical exam fact

Gabari ≠ ağırlık. Gabari is about size (length/width/height), not weight. A truck with a short but extremely heavy cargo violates weight signs but not gabari signs, and vice versa.

Azami ağırlık ≠ taşıma sınırı. Taşıma sınırı is just the load the vehicle can carry; azami ağırlık includes the vehicle itself plus that load. Classic distractor: the exam will describe "the max cargo weight" and ask for the term — the answer is taşıma sınırı, not azami ağırlık.

Dingil ağırlığı is per-axle, not total. A 6 t axle sign can block a 5-ton truck if its rear axle alone is loaded to 6.5 t.

3. Reading the 5 prohibitive gabari signs

Each sign is a red circle with a black number. Red circle = prohibition.

Sign Meaning Definition category
2 m (or 3 m) with width arrows "Genişliği X m'den fazla olan taşıt giremez" — no vehicles wider than X meters Gabari – genişlik
4.5 m with height arrows "Yüksekliği X m'den fazla olan taşıt giremez" — no vehicles taller than X meters Gabari – yükseklik
6 t "Dingil ağırlığı 6 tondan fazla olan taşıt giremez" — no axles above 6 tons Dingil ağırlığı
7.00 t "Yüklü ağırlığı 7 tondan fazla olan taşıt giremez" — no loaded vehicles above 7 tons total Azami ağırlık

Rule of thumb for reading these signs:
- "m" (meters) → gabari (length/width/height)
- "t" (tons) alone → azami (yüklü) ağırlık
- "t" with axle symbol → dingil ağırlığı

4. Why this matters for the exam

  1. Definition match-up — the exam asks "Aracın yükle birlikte ağırlığına ne denir?" → Azami ağırlık (trap: not "taşıma sınırı").
  2. Gabari scope — "Gabari neyi ifade eder?" → uzunluk, genişlik, yükseklik (all three — not weight).
  3. Dingil vs toplam — "Aynı dingildeki tekerleklerden yola aktarılan ağırlık?" → Dingil ağırlığı.
  4. Kapasite vs ağırlık — "Bir aracın güvenle taşıyabileceği en çok yük ağırlığı veya yolcu sayısı?" → Taşıma sınırı / Kapasite.
  5. Sign reading — red circle with "7 t" → yüklü ağırlığı 7 tondan fazla olan taşıt giremez; red circle with "6 t" + axle icon → dingil ağırlığı 6 tondan fazla olan taşıt giremez.
  6. Yüklü veya yüksüz — gabari applies in both states. A vehicle carrying high cargo is still under the height gabari even on its return journey.

5. Memorize These

Gabari = uzunluk + genişlik + yükseklik. (Vehicle envelope: length, width, height. Not weight.)

Taşıma sınırı (kapasite) = maximum load the vehicle can safely carry (cargo kg OR passenger count).

Azami ağırlık = vehicle's own weight + maximum cargo. The total loaded mass.

Dingil ağırlığı = weight transmitted to the road by wheels on one axle. Per-axle, not total.

Sign reading shortcut:
- metre (m) → gabari
- ton (t) alone → azami ağırlık
- ton (t) + dingil → dingil ağırlığı
