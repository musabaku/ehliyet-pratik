Direksiyon ve Ön Düzen Sistemi — Steering & Front-End System

Last updated: 2026-04-14 (class 2)
Covered in class: Parçaların isimleri + önemli dipnotlar (slide 1), Direksiyon + ön düzen diyagramı (slide 2)

1. Overview — Görev (Purpose)

The direksiyon sistemi (steering system) translates the driver's hand motion on the steering wheel into a change of direction at the front wheels. The ön düzen (front-end / front suspension geometry) is the set of angles and alignments that keep those front wheels pointing correctly, rolling straight, and wearing evenly.

Direksiyon sistemi: Ön tekerleklerin yön değiştirmesini, sürücünün komutuyla sağlayan sistemdir.
(The system that changes the direction of the front wheels in response to driver input.)

The two systems are always taught together because the steering hardware (direksiyon kutusu, rot, rot başı) lives inside the ön düzen area, and a fault in one almost always shows up as a symptom in the other (e.g., worn rot başları → direksiyonda boşluk hissedilir).

Parçaların isimleri — Part names (Turkish → English)

# Turkish English What it is

4 Direksiyon simidi Steering wheel The wheel the driver holds. "Simit" literally means "ring" in Turkish — a round ring shape.

Önemli Dipnotlar — 4 facts to memorize cold

Dipnot 1 — Where faults come from

Direksiyon sistemindeki arızalar genellikle direksiyon kutusundaki arızalardan meydana gelir.
Faults in the steering system usually originate from the direksiyon kutusu (steering box).

Why: the direksiyon kutusu holds the most complex moving parts (gears, dişliler). It's the component under constant load and the one most likely to wear out or leak. If something's wrong with the steering "feel", the gearbox is suspect #1.

Dipnot 2 — What lubricant goes inside it

Direksiyon kutusuna dişli yağı konulur.
The steering box is filled with dişli yağı (gear oil).

Plain English: Not motor oil. Not hydraulic fluid. Dişli yağı — specialized gear oil for the gears inside the direksiyon kutusu. This is a classic two-way exam trap: the exam might ask you what goes in the steering box, and the wrong answers will offer "motor yağı" or "fren yağı".

Memorize:
- Motor → motor yağı (engine oil)
- Fren → fren yağı / hidrolik yağı (brake fluid)
- Direksiyon kutusu → dişli yağı (gear oil)
- Soğutma → antifriz + su (antifreeze + water)

Dipnot 3 — Play/slop in the steering

Direksiyonda boşluk varsa rot başlarına bakılır.
If there is play / slop in the steering (direksiyonda boşluk), check the rot başları (tie-rod ends).

Plain English: "Direksiyonda boşluk" means the steering wheel can turn slightly without the front wheels responding — you turn the simit a few degrees and nothing happens. The most common cause is worn rot başları (tie-rod ends); the ball joints inside them develop slack, and that slack translates into dead-zone at the steering wheel.

Exam trigger phrase: "Direksiyonda boşluk hissediliyorsa…" → always answer rot başı arızası / rot başı aşınması.

Dipnot 4 — Wrong front-end alignment → tire wear

Ön düzen ayarları bozuk ise lastikler içten ve dıştan aşınır.
If the ön düzen (front-end alignment) is wrong, tires wear on the inside and outside edges.

Plain English: When rot ayarı (toe) or camber is wrong, the tires don't roll flat on the road — they scuff sideways or lean on one edge. Result: uneven edge wear on the tread. Wear on one specific edge (only inside OR only outside) points to camber issues; wear on both edges points to toe / rot bozukluğu. The slide's wording "içten ve dıştan aşınır" covers both.

Exam trigger phrase: "Lastiklerin iç veya dış kenarları aşınıyorsa…" → ön düzen ayarı bozuktur. Fix: rot-balans ayarı yaptır.

Clean English explanation — how it all works together

When you turn the direksiyon simidi (1) clockwise, rotation travels down the direksiyon mili (2) into the direksiyon kutusu (3). Inside the kutu is a rack-and-pinion gear: the pinion (small cog) rotates with the mil, and its teeth push against the rack (a long bar with teeth — visible in the diagram as the toothed center bar). The rack slides sideways, which pushes one rot (4) and pulls the other. Each rot ends in a rot başı (5) — a ball joint — which pushes the wheel hub and rotates the front wheel around its steering axis. The salıncaklar (6) and amortisörler (7) keep the wheel attached to the car vertically while allowing the steering mechanism to turn the wheel horizontally.

Why both systems are on the same slide: you literally cannot separate them on the hardware level — the rot ends are attached to the wheel hubs, which are held by the salıncaks, which move up and down on the amortisörs. Every steering input also bends the suspension a bit, and every bump in the road tugs at the rot başları. This is why "ön düzen ayarı" (front-end alignment) checks rot, balans, kamber, kaster all together at the same service.

The "0 / 0" gauges

The two small gauges above each wheel in the diagram (showing "0" over "0") represent an alignment measurement tool — at a tire-and-alignment shop, this is the reading you want to see on the machine: 0 degrees of toe deviation and 0 degrees of camber deviation means the wheel is pointing perfectly straight and sitting perfectly vertical. Any non-zero reading means the ön düzen needs adjustment.

Critical exam fact

Rot ve rot başı direksiyon sisteminin parçasıdır — ancak ön düzen ayarlarıyla birlikte kontrol edilir.
Rot and rot başı are parts of the steering system — but they are checked together with the front-end alignment.

4. Why this matters for the exam

Typical exam question patterns on this topic:

  1. "Direksiyon kutusuna ne konulur?" → Dişli yağı (gear oil, NOT motor oil and NOT brake fluid)
  2. "Direksiyonda boşluk hissediliyorsa hangi parça arızalıdır?" → Rot başı (tie-rod end)
  3. "Lastikler içten/dıştan aşınıyorsa ne ayarı bozuktur?" → Ön düzen ayarı (rot ayarı)
  4. "Direksiyon sistemindeki arızalar genellikle nereden kaynaklanır?" → Direksiyon kutusundan
  5. "Aracın titremesi (vibration) neyin göstergesidir?" → Balans ayarı bozuk (wheel balance is off — contrast with rot ayarı, which is about direction, not vibration)
  6. "Direksiyon sisteminin parçaları nelerdir?" → Rot ve rot başları, direksiyon kutusu, direksiyon mili, direksiyon simidi

Rot vs Balans (the classic exam trap)

Rot ayarı Balans ayarı
What it is Toe alignment — the angle at which each front wheel points Wheel balance — weight distribution around the wheel's circumference
What goes wrong Wheels don't point where steering wheel points; car pulls to one side Wheels wobble as they spin
Symptom Lastikler içten/dıştan aşınır (edge wear on tires), car drifts Direksiyonda titreme (vibration through the steering wheel), especially at speed
Fix Rot ayarı Balans ayarı (weights added to the rim)

If an exam question mentions vibration → balans. If it mentions edge wear or pulling → rot.
